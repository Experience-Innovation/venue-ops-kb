# Orchestra Pit and Lift Systems

Orchestra pit and lift management in performing arts centres, where hydraulic or mechanical lift systems raise the pit to stage level, audience-seating level, or lower it for orchestral use, with specific safety requirements for netting, travel limits, and staffing.

## Overview

Many performing arts centres incorporate orchestra pits and pit-lift systems that allow the pit to be configured for multiple uses: lowered for orchestral performance, raised to stage level for extended staging, or brought to audience-seating level to increase front-row capacity. Technical specifications emphasize safety requirements including pit netting, travel limits, and staffing approvals when pits are opened or reconfigured. This is an operational concern foreign to theme parks, fairgrounds, or convention centres.

## Configuration Modes

- **Orchestral position** — Lowered for musicians, typically 47 inches (1200mm) or more below stage level
- **Stage extension** — Raised to stage level for additional performance area
- **Audience seating** — Raised to auditorium floor level for additional front-row seats
- **Storage/maintenance** — Lowered fully for equipment storage or system maintenance

## Safety Requirements

When the orchestra pit lift is lowered beyond defined thresholds (e.g., 47 inches), safety protocols require pit netting to prevent falls, travel-limit interlocks to prevent overtravel, and specific staffing approvals before the pit area can be occupied. These requirements are documented in venue technical specifications and align with stage-machinery control standards.

## Venue Type Variations

Orchestra pits are specific to performing arts centres and some multi-purpose theatres. Stadiums, arenas, convention centres, and theme parks do not incorporate orchestra pit infrastructure.
