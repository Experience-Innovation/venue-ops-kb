# Scoreboard and Game Presentation Operations

Dedicated scoreboard and game-presentation crews that program scoring, statistics, sponsor content, and in-game entertainment across LED boards, ribbons, center-hung displays, and IPTV systems in stadiums and arenas.

## Overview

Game presentation operations represent a distinct operational function in stadiums and large arenas, employing dedicated production teams that coordinate real-time scoring, statistics, sponsor activations, and in-game entertainment across massive display systems. Unified control architectures enable simultaneous triggers across all boards and displays from a dedicated control room.

## Staffing Structure

Game presentation teams typically include a producer/game director, technical director, LED/graphics operators, replay operators, and scoreboard operators. These roles work from a dedicated control room integrated with broadcast and data systems, coordinating with referees, PA announcers, and league data feeds in real time.

## Key Operations

- Real-time scoring and statistics updates from league data feeds
- Sponsor content activation sequences timed to game breaks
- In-game entertainment programming (fan cams, contests, video packages)
- Simultaneous control of center-hung boards, ribbon boards, concourse displays, and IPTV
- Mode switching between sport configurations (hockey, basketball, concert)

## Venue Type Variations

Stadiums operate the most complex game-presentation setups with the largest number of displays and highest production values. Arenas with center-hung scoreboards operate at comparable complexity for hockey and basketball. Performing arts centres and convention centres do not operate game-presentation functions.
