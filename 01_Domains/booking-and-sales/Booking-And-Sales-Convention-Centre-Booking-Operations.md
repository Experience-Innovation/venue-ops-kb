# Convention Centre Booking Operations

Convention centres are fundamentally different from entertainment venues in their booking operations. They are B2B rental facilities operating complex, multi-space, multi-organizer scheduling environments. Their customers are meeting planners, associations, and corporations rather than concert promoters or sports teams. Revenue is driven by space rental, F&B services, and exhibitor services rather than ticket sales [[Source-0311-IAVM-Convention-Center-Performance-Reporting-Framework]].

## How Convention Centre Booking Differs

Key structural differences between convention centre and entertainment venue booking include: lead times of 2-10 years for citywides versus 3-12 months for concerts [[Source-0312-Northstar-Planning-Citywide-Convention]]; primary KPIs of RevPOS and Occupancy Percentage versus Per Cap and Event Yield; critical CVB/DMO role versus minimal CVB involvement; and attendee registration systems (such as Cvent or Eventbrite) versus primary ticketing platforms [[Source-0311-IAVM-Convention-Center-Performance-Reporting-Framework]].

## Space Classification and Allocation

The IAVM Convention Center Performance Reporting Framework classifies rentable space into five primary categories: Exhibit Hall Space (large contiguous flat floor for trade show exhibits), Ballroom Space (higher finish quality for food functions and plenary sessions), Meeting Rooms (flexible space for breakouts and educational sessions), Theatre Space (fixed seats on sloped floor with stage focus), and Other Rentable Space (pre-function, concourse, and lobby space when licensed exclusively) [[Source-0311-IAVM-Convention-Center-Performance-Reporting-Framework]].

Gross Rentable Square Feet/Meters, the denominator for occupancy calculations, includes only these five categories. Common/public space and back-of-house areas are excluded [[Source-0311-IAVM-Convention-Center-Performance-Reporting-Framework]].

## Citywide Convention Booking and CVB Relationships

Citywide conventions — events requiring the full convention centre plus significant hotel room blocks across multiple properties — are typically generated and coordinated by the destination's CVB/DMO before being formally contracted with the convention centre [[Source-0312-Northstar-Planning-Citywide-Convention]].

The operational sequence follows a defined pattern: the CVB identifies and qualifies the lead (often 3-10 years out for major citywides), coordinates the bid by assembling a destination proposal packaging convention centre space, hotel room blocks, and destination services, then executes a Destination Booking Agreement (DBA) [[Source-0388-Destinations-International-DBA]]. The DBA, formalized by Destinations International, documents the booking commitment, cancellation liability, and displacement protection for both destination and meeting professional [[Source-0388-Destinations-International-DBA]]. Following the DBA, the convention centre executes its own license agreement with space allocation, dates, and financial terms [[Source-0311-IAVM-Convention-Center-Performance-Reporting-Framework]].

The IAVM specifically recommends that convention centres track performance by generator — DMO-sourced business versus centre-direct business — to understand which channel is more productive and cost-effective for different market segments [[Source-0311-IAVM-Convention-Center-Performance-Reporting-Framework]].

## Room Block Management

Room block management coordinates between the convention centre, the event organizer, and the CVB/hotel block system to align sleeping room reservations with event attendance projections. Room nights (sleeping rooms blocked or occupied multiplied by number of nights) are a primary economic impact metric for citywide conventions [[Source-0311-IAVM-Convention-Center-Performance-Reporting-Framework]].

## Lead Time Cycles

Major citywide conventions with more than 5,000 attendees typically book 3-10 years out. Mid-size conventions (1,000-5,000 attendees) book 1-3 years out. Corporate meetings under 500 attendees book 3-12 months out, while consumer shows book 6-18 months and community events 1-6 months [[Source-0312-Northstar-Planning-Citywide-Convention]]. Post-COVID trends indicate shorter planning cycles for corporate events relative to historical norms [[Source-0312-Northstar-Planning-Citywide-Convention]].

## Lost Business Reporting

Lost business reporting tracks potential events that reached Tentative or Definite stage but did not convert, along with the reason for loss. IAVM-recommended loss reason codes include: dates/space unavailable, destination does not fit the event's rotation, competitive pricing, hotel pricing, venue facilities, CVB services, and "Convention Center Uncompetitive." The last category is the most actionable for capital planning. Systematic lost business tracking enables identification of patterns informing expansion, renovation, and pricing strategy decisions [[Source-0311-IAVM-Convention-Center-Performance-Reporting-Framework]].

## Exhibitor Services Revenue

Convention centres generate significant exhibitor services revenue from electrical connections, internet drops, compressed air, water, and rigging services sold to individual exhibitors. Where this revenue stream exceeds 5% of total facility revenue, the IAVM recommends reporting it as a distinct line item [[Source-0311-IAVM-Convention-Center-Performance-Reporting-Framework]].
