# University Venue Governance and Booking

University-owned venues operate under governance structures fundamentally different from commercial or municipal venues. The institutional hierarchy (board of governors, provost, athletics director, facilities management) creates multi-layered approval requirements for external bookings, and academic calendar priority creates scheduling constraints not present in commercial operations [[Source-0432-R22-University-SmallVenue]].

## Dual-Use Scheduling

University venues must balance academic use (athletics, convocation, performing arts programs, student events) with external revenue-generating bookings. Academic use typically receives absolute scheduling priority — the athletics schedule is set first, followed by academic events, with remaining availability offered to external clients. This creates highly fragmented booking windows and requires sophisticated scheduling coordination between venue management and academic departments [[Source-0432-R22-University-SmallVenue]].

## Student Organization Priority

Student organizations typically receive preferential booking terms (reduced or waived rental, extended setup windows, relaxed minimum spending requirements) as part of the university's educational mission. Managing the balance between student event support and revenue optimization requires clear priority policies and transparent allocation frameworks [[Source-0432-R22-University-SmallVenue]].

## Institutional Approval Requirements

External bookings at university venues often require approval beyond the venue manager — risk management review, legal counsel approval for complex contracts, and institutional communications review for events with reputational implications. This multi-stage approval process extends booking timelines compared to commercial venues and can create friction with promoters and event organizers accustomed to faster decision-making [[Source-0432-R22-University-SmallVenue]].

## Revenue vs. Mission Balance

University venues face the structural tension between generating revenue to offset institutional costs and fulfilling their educational and community mission. Revenue optimization strategies must respect institutional values including accessibility, academic freedom, and community engagement. Revenue targets are typically set as cost-recovery or subsidy-reduction goals rather than profit maximization [[Source-0432-R22-University-SmallVenue]].
