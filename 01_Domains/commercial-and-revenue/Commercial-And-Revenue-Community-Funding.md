# Community Funding and Local Partnerships

Three key venue funding streams beyond traditional revenue: community crowdfunding (normalized during pandemic), corporate and foundation CSR grants, and local business partnerships providing donations or in-kind support, alongside venue-anchored community development models.

## Overview

Beyond traditional ticket sales, sponsorship, and F&B revenue, venues can access alternative funding streams through community engagement and local partnerships. The pandemic era normalized crowdfunding for cultural and entertainment venues as communities rallied to support venues facing closure. Corporate social responsibility (CSR) programs, foundation grants, and local business partnerships provide additional non-traditional revenue streams. Academic research also positions venues as anchors for community development, creating broader economic and social value that supports funding arguments.

## Key Components

### Community Crowdfunding

- Crowdfunding became a normalized funding mechanism for venues during the pandemic, when communities contributed directly to keep local venues operational
- Platforms like GoFundMe, Kickstarter, and Indiegogo enable venues to run targeted campaigns for specific projects — renovations, equipment upgrades, accessibility improvements, or programming initiatives
- Successful venue crowdfunding campaigns leverage the emotional connection between communities and their local cultural venues
- Post-pandemic, crowdfunding has transitioned from emergency survival to project-specific fundraising

### Corporate and Foundation CSR Grants

- Corporate social responsibility (CSR) programs from regional and national companies provide grant funding to venues that align with their community investment priorities
- Foundation grants target specific outcomes — arts education, community health, cultural preservation, environmental sustainability — that venues can deliver through programming
- Grant applications require venues to articulate measurable community impact, moving beyond anecdotal benefit claims

### Local Business Partnerships

- Local businesses provide financial donations, in-kind support (products, services, labor), or cross-promotional partnerships to venue operations
- In-kind support reduces operating costs while building community business relationships
- Cross-promotional partnerships (restaurant packages, hotel bundles, retail discounts) create mutual value for venues and surrounding businesses
- The Barclays Center example demonstrates local hiring commitments and diverse vendor partnership programs that strengthen community economic ties

### Venue-Anchored Community Development

- Academic research positions venues as anchors for mixed-use district development, generating economic activity beyond the venue walls
- Venues contribute to community health promotion through programming, gathering spaces, and cultural engagement
- MLB Green club initiatives link sustainability programs to community engagement, creating dual-purpose value
- Local hiring and diverse vendor partnerships strengthen the venue's role as a community economic engine

## Venue Type Variations

Community funding applicability varies by venue ownership model and community integration. Publicly owned civic venues (convention centres, performing arts centres) have the strongest case for community funding given their direct public benefit mandate. Privately owned arenas and stadiums leverage community development arguments in support of public subsidy requests. Smaller independent venues have historically been the most successful at crowdfunding due to strong personal community connections. All venue types can benefit from local business partnerships regardless of ownership structure.
