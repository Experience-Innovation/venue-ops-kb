# Crowd Manager Staffing Requirements

NFPA 101-mandated staffing ratios for trained crowd managers in assembly occupancies, requiring 1 crowd manager per 250 attendees with AHJ discretion for reductions.

## Overview

NFPA 101 (Sections 12.7.6 and 13.7.6) mandates trained crowd managers in all assembly occupancies, with a baseline ratio of 1 crowd manager per 250 attendees.

## Staffing Requirements

- **Baseline ratio:** 1:250 (NFPA 101)
- **High-energy events:** 1:50 to 1:100 for concerts, festivals, sporting events
- **AHJ discretion:** Reduction allowed in fully sprinklered buildings
- **Religious worship exception:** Up to 2,000 occupants may be exempt

## Training Requirements

NFPA 101 defines 10 required crowd manager training duties covering:
- Emergency procedures and evacuation routes
- Crowd flow management and density awareness
- Communication protocols
- First aid awareness
- Fire safety equipment familiarity

## Jurisdictional Variations

- **IFC (International Fire Code):** 2018 edition reduced crowd manager threshold from 1,000 to 500
- **California Fire Code Section 403.3:** State-specific crowd manager requirements
- **Virginia:** Statewide Fire Prevention Code Section 403.3 — 1:250 for events >1,000
- **ICC/NASFM:** Standardized online crowd manager training program available

## Canadian Context

No federal mandate, but crowd management requirements are enforced through local fire codes and event permitting. Calgary requires fire safety plans for special events including crowd management provisions.
