# Flow Management and Crush Prevention

Operational strategies for managing crowd flow safely through venues, including multiple entry points, timed entry, barrier design, distributed amenities, and real-time flow monitoring.

## Overview

Flow management prevents the dangerous crowd density conditions that lead to crushes. Key principles include dispersing crowd pressure, eliminating bottlenecks, and maintaining escape routes.

## Design Strategies

- **Multiple dispersed entry points:** Preventing concentration at single gates
- **Timed/wave entry:** Staggered arrival windows via ticketing platforms
- **Looping pathways:** No dead ends — all paths lead to exits
- **Distributed amenities:** Food, drink, merchandise, and restrooms spread to prevent crowd clustering
- **Ultra-wide concourses:** Preventing bottlenecks at high-traffic points
- **Compartmentalized crowds:** Section-based management reducing total crowd interaction

## Front-of-Stage Safety

- Curved or angled crash barricades (not straight barriers)
- 6-10 foot security/medical buffer pit between audience and stage
- D-shaped and T-shaped pen barriers for lateral channeling
- Load sensors on barriers to detect dangerous pressure buildup

## Egress Management

- Section-by-section release (phased exit) after events
- Separate pedestrian and vehicle exit flows
- Lighted, signed egress routes activated immediately at event end
- Transit coordination to absorb departing crowds

## Real-World Lessons

The 2022 Seoul Itaewon stampede and 2017 Las Vegas Route 91 shooting both reinforced the critical importance of designed egress routes, crowd monitoring, and rapid response capability.
