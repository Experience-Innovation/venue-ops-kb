# Fruin's Level of Service (LOS) Framework

Foundational pedestrian density classification scale (LOS A through LOS F) developed by John Fruin in 1971, used globally as the reference framework for crowd density assessment.

## Overview

John Fruin's Level of Service (LOS) framework, published in 1971 through the Transportation Research Board, defines a six-level pedestrian density scale that remains the foundational reference for crowd management worldwide.

## LOS Levels

| Level | Density | Conditions |
|-------|---------|------------|
| LOS A | <0.3 persons/sq m | Free flow, no restrictions |
| LOS B | 0.3-0.5 persons/sq m | Minor speed reduction |
| LOS C | 0.5-0.7 persons/sq m | Speed reduced, some conflicts |
| LOS D | 0.7-1.1 persons/sq m | Restricted movement, unavoidable contact |
| LOS E | 1.1-2.2 persons/sq m | Severely restricted, shuffling movement |
| LOS F | >2.2 persons/sq m | Body-to-body contact, danger zone |

## Application in Venues

- LOS C: Standard target for concourse and walkway design
- LOS D: Intervention threshold — crowd management activates mitigation
- LOS E-F: Emergency conditions requiring immediate response
- Wembley Stadium observations have been used to validate the framework in modern venue contexts

## Modern Extensions

The framework has been extended through multi-agent simulation models and AI-based density estimation. The GCMA Field Guide to Crowds (2nd edition, April 2025) and UNOCT crowd management guidance both reference Fruin as foundational.
