# NFPA Assembly Occupancy Compliance

NFPA 101 Life Safety Code Chapter 12/13 governs occupancy requirements for assembly venues, defining maximum occupant loads based on floor area and occupant load factors, means of egress capacity calculations, and the staffing thresholds that trigger mandatory crowd management planning [[Source-0424-SGSA-Green-Guide-6th]].

## Occupant Load Factors

NFPA 101 prescribes occupant load factors by use type: concentrated seating without arms (7 net sq ft per person), less concentrated use (15 net sq ft), standing spaces (5 net sq ft), and exhibition/display areas (see below). The calculated maximum occupant load determines exit capacity requirements, fire alarm system sizing, and crowd management staffing obligations [[Source-0421-Helbing-Crowd-Turbulence]].

## Crowd Manager Requirement

NFPA 101 Section 12.7.6.2 requires assembly venues with 250 or more occupants to have trained crowd managers. The ratio is typically 1 crowd manager per 250 occupants, though jurisdictional adoption varies. Crowd managers must be trained in crowd management techniques, emergency procedures, and the specific venue layout [[Source-0424-SGSA-Green-Guide-6th]].

## Means of Egress Capacity

Egress width is calculated based on the occupant load: 0.3 inches per person for stairways, 0.2 inches per person for level components and ramps. Each exit must accommodate at least one-half the total occupant load when multiple exits are provided. The requirement that egress capacity accommodate full evacuation within defined timeframes is the regulatory bridge between occupancy limits and life safety [[Source-0421-Helbing-Crowd-Turbulence]].

## UK Equivalent — Green Guide

The SGSA Guide to Safety at Sports Grounds (Green Guide), now in its 6th edition (2018), provides the UK equivalent framework. Originating from the Taylor Report following the 1989 Hillsborough disaster, the Green Guide details safe capacity assessment methodology and is enforced through the Safety of Sports Grounds Act 1975 and Fire Safety and Safety of Places of Sport Act 1987 [[Source-0424-SGSA-Green-Guide-6th]].
