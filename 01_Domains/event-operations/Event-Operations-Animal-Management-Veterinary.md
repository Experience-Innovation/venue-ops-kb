# Animal Management and Veterinary Services

Fairground management of livestock and show animals including on-site veterinary services, animal health certificates, biosecurity protocols, manure management, stabling operations, and compliance with IAFE's National Code of Show Ring Ethics.

## Overview

Fairs manage large populations of livestock and show animals, governed by IAFE's National Code of Show Ring Ethics. This code requires proof of ownership, animal health certificates from licensed veterinarians, and prohibition of drug misuse. Fair books specify on-site veterinarians, daily rounds, and procedures for exhibitors to request animal checks, integrating animal welfare into fair operations at a level that has no parallel in convention centres, stadiums, arenas, or performing arts centres.

## Key Components

- On-site veterinary services with daily animal rounds
- Animal health certificate verification at check-in
- Proof of ownership documentation
- Drug testing and ethical compliance enforcement
- Show ring management for livestock judging, horse shows, and auctions
- Stabling operations including feeding, watering, and bedding
- Manure management and waste removal

## Regulatory Framework

The IAFE National Code of Show Ring Ethics is the governing document for livestock exhibition across North American fairs. It mandates that exhibitors provide animal health certificates from licensed veterinarians, establishes ownership proof requirements, and prohibits drug misuse. Violations can result in exhibitor suspension and reporting to breed associations.

## Venue Type Variations

Animal management is specific to fairgrounds and exhibition grounds that host agricultural shows. Theme parks may have animal encounters but under different regulatory frameworks (USDA, AZA). Urban convention centres, arenas, stadiums, and PACs do not manage livestock operations.
