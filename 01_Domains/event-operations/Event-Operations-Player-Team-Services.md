# Player and Team Services

Venue-operated services for professional sports teams including locker rooms, hydrotherapy, medical suites, warm-up areas, equipment rooms, and visiting-team logistics, governed by league protocols and collective agreements.

## Overview

Stadiums and arenas hosting professional sports operate large-scale player facilities that are governed by league protocols rather than venue-internal standards. NHL protocols stipulate that home clubs must supply locker-room attendants and support services for visiting teams, with specific limits on attendant counts and defined equipment room procedures. These operational rules have no direct analogue in convention centres, performing arts centres, or theme parks.

## Key Components

- Home and visiting team locker rooms with showers and medical facilities
- Hydrotherapy and recovery rooms adjacent to playing surface
- Equipment storage, laundry, and maintenance areas
- Warm-up areas and training staff workspaces
- Secure access control for team-only areas
- Visiting-team logistics coordination

## League Governance

Professional league protocols dictate specific operational requirements. NHL rules permit 2 locker-room attendants per club on-site during games, with defined responsibilities for equipment handling, laundry, and visiting-club support. Similar league-specific rules apply across NBA, NFL, and MLS venues, creating a governance layer that is external to the venue's own operational standards.

## Venue Type Variations

Stadiums hosting field sports (NFL, MLS, CFL) operate the largest player facilities due to roster sizes. Arenas hosting hockey and basketball maintain comparable facilities at smaller scale. Smaller arenas sometimes deploy temporary or curtain-divided visiting locker rooms. Convention centres and PACs do not operate player services.
