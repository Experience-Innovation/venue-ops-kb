# Wardrobe and Costume Operations

Performing arts wardrobe departments that oversee costume construction, maintenance, laundry, quick-changes, and storage for resident companies and touring shows, managed by a Head of Wardrobe and staffed by cutters, stitchers, dressers, and wig technicians.

## Overview

Wardrobe departments in performing arts centres create a distinct operational group not paralleled in convention centres, stadiums, or theme parks. The Head of Wardrobe oversees all aspects of implementing the costume design, managing dressers, coordinating fittings, supervising costume construction, and ensuring health-and-safety compliance for costume work. IATSE wardrobe classifications define the roles within running crews.

## Key Functions

- Costume construction including period pieces, suits, and specialty garments
- Wig and wig styling maintenance
- Daily laundering and garment maintenance between performances
- Quick-change management during live performances
- Fitting coordination with performers and designers
- Costume storage and inventory management across productions

## Staffing Structure

Wardrobe teams include the Head of Wardrobe, cutters (pattern-makers), stitchers, dressers specializing in quick-changes, and wig and makeup technicians. Running crew wardrobe assignments are defined by IATSE classifications, with the wardrobe head managing all costumes once they are accepted from the costume shop for use in production.

## Venue Type Variations

PACs with resident theatre, opera, or dance companies operate the largest wardrobe departments. Touring houses provide wardrobe support (laundry, pressing, dressing rooms) but not full costume shops. Convention centres, arenas, and stadiums do not operate wardrobe departments. Theme parks employ costume departments for character performers, but these operate under entertainment rather than production frameworks.
