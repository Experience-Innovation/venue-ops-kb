# Ice Arena Refrigeration and Rink Management

Ice arena operations require specialized refrigeration plant management (ammonia or HFC systems), dehumidification, ice surface quality control, and energy-intensive resurfacing operations, with regulatory pressure from the Kigali Amendment driving HFC phasedown timelines.

## Refrigeration Systems

- **Ammonia (NH3):** Traditional large-rink standard — efficient but requires IIAR-2 safety compliance
- **HFC (R-507A, R-449A):** Common in smaller rinks — faces Kigali Amendment phasedown schedule
- **CO2 (R-744):** Emerging low-GWP alternative — transcritical systems gaining adoption in new builds

## Dehumidification

Arena dehumidification prevents fog, condensation, and poor ice quality. Options include desiccant wheel systems, DX dehumidifiers, and low-emissivity ceiling panels.

## Resurfacing Operations

REALice water treatment technology eliminates the need for hot water in Zamboni operations, saving 5,000+ kWh annually per rink. Ice thickness is maintained at 1.25-1.5 inches.

## Regulatory Impact

The Kigali Amendment mandates 85% HFC phasedown by 2036 (developed countries), forcing arena operators to plan refrigerant transitions in capital budgets.
