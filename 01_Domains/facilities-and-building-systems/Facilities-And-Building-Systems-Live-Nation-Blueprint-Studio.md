# Live Nation Blueprint Studio Venue Design Practice

Blueprint Studio is Live Nation's in-house venue design practice, handling all purpose-built venue fit-outs engineered specifically to music-fan specifications and identified capacity gaps in local and regional markets. In June 2025, Live Nation announced a $1 billion venue investment program covering 18 new or revitalized US venues, with Blueprint Studio serving as the design authority. Newly opened US amphitheaters built under this model deliver F&B spending 10%+ higher than the rest of the Live Nation portfolio — a measurable validation that fan-specification-driven design generates stronger commercial outcomes.

## Framework / Model / Program

Blueprint Studio inverts the traditional venue design model — rather than contracting external architecture firms on a project-by-project basis, Live Nation maintains an in-house design practice that accumulates fan-behavior data, operational learnings, and commercial performance benchmarks across the portfolio. Each new venue is designed against current fan specifications (sight lines, F&B adjacency, restroom capacity, merchandise flow, arrival/egress patterns) and identified capacity gaps (market-specific demand that existing venues cannot absorb). The $1B investment program covers 18 new or revitalized venues in the US. The in-house model creates a continuous feedback loop — operational data from existing venues informs the design of the next cohort, which generates new data in turn.

## Operational Application in Venues

The Blueprint Studio model offers a template for portfolio venue operators considering in-house design capability: the investment pays off when there is sufficient project volume to justify a standing design team and when operational data can be systematically fed back into design decisions. For individual venue operators, the relevance is directional — even without an in-house studio, operators can adopt the fan-specification-driven methodology (sight lines, F&B adjacency, capacity gap analysis) and the commercial-outcome feedback loop (measuring per-cap F&B spending against design choices). The 10%+ F&B spending lift at new amphitheaters is a concrete benchmark for evaluating whether a redesign or new-build investment is likely to deliver commercial return.

## Key Metrics / Outcomes

- Investment program: $1 billion announced June 2025
- Scope: 18 new or revitalized US venues
- F&B performance: new US amphitheaters deliver 10%+ higher per-cap F&B spending vs rest of portfolio
- Design model: in-house practice (Blueprint Studio) replaces project-by-project external architecture contracting
- Design inputs: fan specifications + identified capacity gaps
- Continuous feedback loop: operational data from existing venues informs next-cohort design
