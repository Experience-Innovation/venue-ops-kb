# Plumbing and Water Systems for High-Occupancy Venues

Plumbing design for assembly occupancies requires IBC-mandated fixture counts based on occupant load, high-capacity domestic water systems, grease interceptors for F&B operations, stormwater management, and water-efficient fixtures to meet sustainability targets.

## Code Requirements

IBC Table 2902.1 sets minimum plumbing fixture counts based on occupancy classification and occupant load.

## Design Considerations

- **Peak demand:** Design for simultaneous use during intermission — not average use
- **Gender equity:** Potty parity legislation compliance
- **F&B support:** Dedicated hot water, grease interceptors, three-compartment sinks
- **Stormwater:** Large roof and parking areas require retention/detention systems

## Sustainability Integration

LEED Water Efficiency credits reward low-flow fixtures (sensor faucets, dual-flush toilets, waterless urinals). Convention centres with cooling towers address water use through optimized cycles of concentration.
