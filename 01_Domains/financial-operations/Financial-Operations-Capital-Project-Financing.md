# Capital Project Planning and Financing

Major venue capital projects begin with structured needs assessment and are financed through a variety of public and private mechanisms, each with distinct risk profiles, tax implications, and governance requirements [[Source-0415-Nuveen-Stadium-Financing]].

## Capital Needs Assessment

The process follows four stages: (1) Facility Condition Assessment (FCA) commissioned from qualified engineers documenting all building systems by priority class, (2) Facility Condition Index (FCI) calculation — total deferred maintenance divided by current replacement value (below 5% = good, 5–10% = fair, above 10% = poor), (3) remaining useful life analysis by major system, and (4) deferred maintenance quantification as the starting point for renovation business cases [[Source-0413-GFOA-Distinguished-Budget]].

## Bond Financing Structures

Tax-exempt municipal bonds are the most common capital financing for North American venues. **Revenue bonds** are secured by venue operating revenues; **GO bonds** by the issuing government's taxing authority; **special tax bonds** by dedicated streams such as hotel taxes, in-stadium sales taxes, and ticket taxes. Recent examples: Nashville Titans stadium ($2.1B total, $760M Metro Nashville special tax/revenue bonds); Buffalo Bills stadium ($250M Erie County GO bonds, $600M state contribution). Credit rating agencies evaluate coverage of average annual debt service — below 1.2x coverage can trigger downgrades [[Source-0415-Nuveen-Stadium-Financing]] [[Source-0419-Moodys-New-Orleans-CC-Rating]].

## Tax-Exempt Bond Compliance

IRS Code §141 private activity bond tests require ongoing monitoring: more than 10% of bond-financed property used for private business AND more than 10% of debt service secured by private payments triggers loss of tax-exempt status. Management contracts with private operators must satisfy IRS safe harbors. Post-issuance compliance extends over the full bond life, with record-keeping requirements for at least 3 years after final maturity [[Source-0415-Nuveen-Stadium-Financing]].

## Hotel/Motel Tax and Tourism Levies

Dedicated hotel room tax revenue is a primary capital and operating funding mechanism for convention centres and arenas in both the US and Canada. Alberta's Tourism Levy Act requires accommodation providers to collect and remit the provincial levy for tourism marketing and development [[Source-0413-GFOA-Distinguished-Budget]].

## GASB Requirements for Capital Assets

Under GASB 87, all venue leases exceeding 12 months require balance sheet recognition as right-of-use assets and lease liabilities. GASB 96 applies the same treatment to subscription-based IT arrangements (cloud ticketing, event management, ERP). GASB 104 (effective FY beginning after June 15, 2025) requires separate disclosure for lease assets, subscription assets, and assets held for sale [[Source-0417-GASB-87-Leases]].

## Project Delivery Methods

Design-Bid-Build provides full competitive bidding but highest change order risk; Construction Manager at Risk (CMAR) provides cost certainty through Guaranteed Maximum Price while maintaining design control — frequently preferred by public authorities for $100M+ projects; Design-Build offers fastest delivery with single-point accountability. Standard construction contingency ranges from 5–10% (DB) to 10–20% (DBB) [[Source-0415-Nuveen-Stadium-Financing]].
