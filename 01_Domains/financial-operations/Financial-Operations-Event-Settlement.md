# Event Settlement and Reconciliation

Event settlement is the financial reconciliation process that closes every venue event, translating contracted deal terms into actual cash distributions between venue, promoter, and artist. The settlement structure is determined by the deal type negotiated at contracting [[Source-0412-IAVM-Arena-Performance-Handbook]].

## Deal Structures

Four primary deal structures govern settlement mechanics. **Flat rental** (four-wall) provides the simplest settlement — the venue invoices a fixed rental plus house nut against the promoter's deposit. **Guarantee vs. split** (versus deal) requires calculating Gross Box Office Receipts, deducting approved expenses, and distributing overage per the agreed split (e.g., 80/20). **Co-promotion** shares both costs and revenues, requiring full transparent accounting. **In-house/self-promoted** events reconcile internally against the venue's own P&L [[Source-0412-IAVM-Arena-Performance-Handbook]] [[Source-0414-Ticket-Fairy-Settlement]].

## Day-of-Show Settlement Workflow

The settlement sequence proceeds: (1) preliminary settlement prepared before doors open with estimated expenses against projected revenue, (2) box office closes with actual GBOR/NBOR confirmed, (3) expense review with all receipts compiled, (4) final settlement meeting between venue financial representative and promoter's tour accountant, (5) cash distribution via certified check or wire transfer. Non-refundable deposits of 50% of rental are standard, with balance due at settlement [[Source-0420-Prism-Settlement-Best-Practices]].

## F&B Settlement

F&B settlement mechanics depend on the concession model: self-operated venues reconcile POS systems against contracted pricing; third-party concessionaires provide revenue share calculations against gross sales; catered events are invoiced per contracted menu plus service charges. IAVM recommends tracking F&B revenue per capita and per cover separately for event-type benchmarking [[Source-0411-IAVM-CC-Performance-Handbook]].

## Ancillary Revenue Streams

Ancillary reconciliation covers parking (per-cap rebates or full venue retention), merchandise (15–25% hall fee commission), facility fees (per-ticket surcharges), premium seating/VIP packages, Wi-Fi charges (per-device at convention centres), and sponsorship activation fees. Each stream has distinct settlement timing and contract provisions [[Source-0412-IAVM-Arena-Performance-Handbook]].

## Dispute Prevention

Settlement disputes most commonly arise over expense deductibility in versus deals, merchandise commission calculations, and uncorroborated expense claims. Best practices include requiring all deductible categories defined explicitly in contracts, mandating receipts for every line item, conducting preliminary settlement walk-throughs before events, and specifying arbitration as the dispute resolution mechanism [[Source-0414-Ticket-Fairy-Settlement]].
