# FOOD-TRAK Inventory Management System (McCormick Place)

McCormick Place, North America's largest convention centre, deployed the FOOD-TRAK inventory management system in partnership with Zebra Technologies to transform its kitchen inventory operations. The implementation raised kitchen inventory accuracy from 80% to 98% while cutting weekly inventory labor hours from 30+ to approximately 16, effectively halving labor costs associated with inventory management. The case demonstrates that convention centre F&B operations can achieve step-change improvements through targeted inventory technology deployment.

## Framework / Model / Program

FOOD-TRAK is a specialized food and beverage inventory management platform that combines barcode scanning, mobile data capture, recipe costing, and usage analytics into a unified operational system. The Zebra Technologies partnership provides the mobile scanning hardware and integration layer. At McCormick Place, the system replaced manual inventory counting processes — typically clipboard-based and error-prone — with barcode-scanning workflows that capture inventory data at the point of count. Recipe costing and usage analytics layer on top of the raw inventory data to surface variance, theoretical-vs-actual consumption gaps, and procurement optimization opportunities. The operational model compresses inventory cycles, reduces labor, and improves data accuracy simultaneously.

## Operational Application in Venues

Convention centres, arenas, and stadiums with high-volume, high-variety F&B operations face consistent inventory challenges: large SKU counts, frequent menu changes, distributed storage locations, and tight event-day turnaround pressure. FOOD-TRAK-style systems address all four by digitizing counts, standardizing recipes, and surfacing variance automatically. The McCormick Place outcomes — 80% to 98% accuracy and 50% labor reduction — suggest the ROI case is strong for venues of similar scale. Smaller venues can benefit proportionally. The staff morale improvement noted at McCormick Place is a secondary but meaningful outcome: inventory counting is often the most-disliked recurring task in kitchen operations, and replacing manual counts with barcode scanning removes a frustration driver.

## Key Metrics / Outcomes

- Inventory accuracy: increased from 80% to 98%
- Weekly inventory labor: reduced from 30+ hours to approximately 16 hours
- Labor cost: halved
- Staff morale: improved
- Deployment venue: McCormick Place (North America's largest convention centre)
- Technology partnership: FOOD-TRAK + Zebra Technologies
- Capabilities: barcode scanning, mobile data capture, recipe costing, usage analytics
