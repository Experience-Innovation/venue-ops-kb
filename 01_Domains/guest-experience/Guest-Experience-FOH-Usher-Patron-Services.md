# Front-of-House Usher and Patron Services Programs

Performing arts FOH operations relying on paid and volunteer usher programs for ticket scanning, seating, accessibility support, lobby management, and patron services, differentiated from game-oriented guest-services staffing in arenas and stadiums.

## Overview

Front-of-house operations in performing arts centres rely heavily on usher programs that typically mix paid staff and volunteers. This model differs significantly from game-oriented guest-services staffing in arenas and stadiums, where most positions are paid event staff focused on crowd management and concourse operations. PAC ushers handle ticket scanning, seating guests, accessibility support, program distribution, and lobby management in a more service-oriented context.

## Staffing Model

PAC FOH programs typically employ a FOH manager, paid ushers, volunteer ushers, greeters, and bar staff. Volunteer ushers are a significant component, often receiving free admission in exchange for service, with dedicated systems to schedule hours, track participation, and manage position assignments across multi-performance runs. FOH floor captains supervise sections and coordinate with house management.

## Key Functions

- Ticket scanning and seating management
- Accessibility support for patrons requiring assistance
- Program and playbill distribution
- Lobby management during pre-show, intermission, and post-show
- Late-seating protocols (holding latecomers until appropriate pause)
- Emergency evacuation assistance

## Venue Type Variations

PACs operate the most service-oriented FOH programs, with volunteer components rarely found in stadiums or arenas. Arena and stadium guest services focus more on crowd management, security support, and wayfinding. Convention centres operate registration support rather than usher programs. Theme parks employ guest-relations staff rather than usher-model positions.
