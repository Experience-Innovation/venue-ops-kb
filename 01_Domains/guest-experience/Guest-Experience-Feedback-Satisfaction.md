# Guest Feedback and Satisfaction Measurement

Guest satisfaction measurement at venues combines standardized metrics (NPS, CSAT), real-time feedback capture, social media sentiment analysis, and venue-type-specific benchmarking to drive continuous experience improvement [[Source-0428-Disney-Service-Design]].

## Net Promoter Score (NPS)

NPS measures likelihood to recommend on a 0–10 scale, categorizing respondents as Promoters (9–10), Passives (7–8), or Detractors (0–6). Venue NPS benchmarks vary significantly by event type: concerts and sports events typically score higher (emotional engagement) than conventions and trade shows (functional satisfaction). NPS is most valuable when tracked over time and segmented by event type, venue zone, and demographic [[Source-0428-Disney-Service-Design]].

## CSAT and Touchpoint-Level Measurement

Customer Satisfaction Score (CSAT) measures satisfaction with specific touchpoints on a 1–5 or 1–7 scale. Venue CSAT is most actionable when collected at the touchpoint level — entry experience, F&B, restrooms, seating, egress — rather than as a single overall score. Touchpoint-level CSAT identifies the specific operational levers with highest impact on overall satisfaction [[Source-0428-Disney-Service-Design]].

## Real-Time Feedback Systems

Modern venue feedback systems move beyond post-event surveys to capture in-venue sentiment through SMS/app-based micro-surveys (1–2 questions triggered by location or time), emoji-button kiosks at restrooms and exits, QR-code prompted feedback at concession stands, and social media monitoring dashboards aggregating live mentions. Real-time systems enable service recovery during the event rather than after departure [[Source-0428-Disney-Service-Design]].

## Benchmarking by Venue Type

Guest satisfaction benchmarking requires venue-type-appropriate comparison sets. IAVM VenueDataSource provides peer benchmarking capabilities. Key satisfaction drivers differ by type: convention centres prioritize connectivity and meeting room quality; arenas prioritize sightlines, F&B speed, and entry experience; performing arts centres prioritize acoustics, comfort, and intermission service [[Source-0428-Disney-Service-Design]].
