# Guest Journey Mapping for Venues

Guest journey mapping is a structured methodology that traces the complete attendee experience from pre-event awareness through post-event follow-up, identifying friction points, moments of truth, and opportunities for experience differentiation at each phase [[Source-0428-Disney-Service-Design]].

## Five-Phase Journey Framework

The venue guest journey comprises five phases: (1) **Pre-event** — ticket purchase, information seeking, travel planning, expectations formation; (2) **Arrival** — parking/transit, wayfinding to entrance, queue experience, credential verification; (3) **In-venue** — concourse navigation, F&B, merchandise, restrooms, seating; (4) **Event** — the core experience (performance, exhibition, meeting); (5) **Departure** — egress, transportation, post-event communication. Each phase has distinct touchpoints, emotional states, and operational levers [[Source-0428-Disney-Service-Design]].

## Moments of Truth

Jan Carlzon's "moments of truth" concept — each point of contact between the guest and the organization is an opportunity to form an impression — applies directly to venue operations. Critical moments of truth in venue operations include: first visual impression of the facility, greeting at entry, wait time at concessions, restroom cleanliness, sightline quality from seat, and ease of departure. Mapping these moments enables targeted resource allocation to the touchpoints with highest impact on overall satisfaction [[Source-0428-Disney-Service-Design]].

## Service Recovery Integration

Journey maps should identify the most common failure points and pre-position service recovery protocols. Disney's HEARD model (Hear, Empathize, Apologize, Resolve, Diagnose) provides a structured recovery framework. Venues that empower front-line staff with service recovery authority (e.g., complimentary upgrades, F&B credits) at the point of failure achieve higher recovery satisfaction than those requiring managerial escalation [[Source-0428-Disney-Service-Design]].

## Data-Driven Journey Optimization

Modern journey mapping integrates quantitative data (dwell time by zone from WiFi/BLE, queue wait times from sensors, POS transaction timing, NPS by touchpoint) with qualitative research (intercept surveys, focus groups, social media sentiment) to prioritize improvements with measurable ROI [[Source-0428-Disney-Service-Design]].
