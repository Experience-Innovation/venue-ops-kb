# Tailgating and External Fan Zones

Pre-event tailgating ecosystems and external fan zones in stadium parking lots and plazas, involving traffic management, open-flame policies, alcohol controls, crowd analytics, and external entertainment programming.

## Overview

Football stadiums and large outdoor venues manage complex pre-event tailgating ecosystems across parking lots and plazas. These operations extend the venue's operational perimeter well beyond the building itself, requiring dedicated coordination of traffic management, open-flame policies, alcohol enforcement, and external entertainment zones. Crowd-analytics vendors emphasize end-to-end journey design targeting a 30-minute parking-to-seat experience.

## Key Components

- Traffic management and parking lot access control
- Open-flame and grilling zone designation and enforcement
- Alcohol control and responsible beverage service in external areas
- External entertainment programming and vendor coordination
- Crowd analytics using camera, LiDAR, and Wi-Fi sensors
- Ingress timing coordination from external zones to gates

## Operational Considerations

Tailgating operations require a fundamentally different staffing and management approach than interior venue operations. External environments are less controllable, with open-flame cooking, alcohol consumption, and large unstructured crowds creating unique safety and crowd-management challenges. Analytics platforms provide real-time visibility into external crowding to help manage the transition from tailgate zones into the stadium.

## Venue Type Variations

Tailgating is most extensive at NFL and college football stadiums with large surface parking lots. Downtown arenas and PACs typically lack the physical space for tailgating. Convention centres, theme parks, and fairgrounds do not operate tailgating programs, though fairgrounds may host comparable pre-event gathering in outdoor areas.
