# Sensory-Inclusive Venue Programs

Venue programs supporting guests with autism, PTSD, sensory processing disorders, and related conditions through sensory-friendly performances, quiet rooms, sensory bags, staff training certifications (IBCCES, KultureCity), and environmental modifications.

## Overview

Sensory-inclusive programming represents a growing area of venue accessibility that extends beyond ADA compliance to serve guests whose needs are not fully addressed by physical accessibility accommodations alone. These programs address the sensory environment of venues — lighting, sound levels, crowd density, and environmental predictability — which can create barriers for individuals with autism spectrum disorder, PTSD, dementia, sensory processing disorders, and other conditions.

## Sensory-Friendly Performances

Sensory-friendly or relaxed performances modify the standard show environment to reduce sensory stimulation. Typical modifications include reduced lighting levels and elimination of strobe effects, lowered sound levels and reduced use of sudden loud noises, open auditorium doors to allow patrons to exit and re-enter freely, designated quiet spaces available throughout the performance, and relaxed audience behavior expectations (movement, vocalization permitted). These performances are typically scheduled as additional shows rather than replacements for standard performances.

## Quiet Rooms and Sensory Spaces

Dedicated sensory rooms or quiet spaces provide a retreat for guests experiencing sensory overload. These spaces typically feature low lighting, reduced noise, comfortable seating, weighted blankets, noise-canceling headphones, fidget tools, and calming visual elements. Venues position these spaces in accessible locations that allow guests to step away from stimulating environments and return when ready.

## IBCCES Certified Autism Center Program

The International Board of Credentialing and Continuing Education Standards (IBCCES) offers the Certified Autism Center (CAC) designation for venues. The certification requires comprehensive staff training on autism and sensory sensitivities, environmental assessments of the venue's sensory profile, and implementation of specific accommodations. Training covers communication strategies, de-escalation techniques, and recognition of sensory overload indicators.

## KultureCity Sensory Inclusive Certification

KultureCity partners with venues to achieve Sensory Inclusive certification through staff training and provision of sensory bags containing noise-canceling headphones, fidget tools, verbal cue cards, and weighted lap pads available for guest checkout. The program includes creation of visual social stories — illustrated guides that walk guests through the venue experience step by step, reducing anxiety about unfamiliar environments. KultureCity also provides a mobile app to help guests locate sensory-friendly features within certified venues.

## Notable Programs

The Kennedy Center in Washington, D.C. operates accessibility programs including sensory-friendly performances across multiple performance types, pre-visit resources, and dedicated accessibility staff. Disney's Disability Access Service (DAS) provides a scheduling accommodation for guests who cannot wait in conventional queues due to developmental disabilities, allowing them to schedule return times for attractions rather than waiting in the physical queue environment.
