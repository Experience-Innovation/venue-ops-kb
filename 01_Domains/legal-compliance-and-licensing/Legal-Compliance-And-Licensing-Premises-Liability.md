# Venue Premises Liability and Duty of Care

Premises liability imposes a legal duty on venue operators to maintain reasonably safe conditions for invitees — guests who enter with the operator's express or implied invitation for a purpose connected to the operator's business. Venue attendees are classified as invitees under US common law, receiving the highest duty of care [[Source-0433-R24-Legal-Liability]].

## Duty of Care Standard

The venue operator's duty to invitees includes: inspecting the premises to discover dangerous conditions, warning invitees of known or discoverable hazards, and taking reasonable steps to protect invitees from foreseeable harm. The "reasonableness" standard is fact-specific — a wet concourse during a rain event requires different mitigation than a structural hazard known for months [[Source-0433-R24-Legal-Liability]].

## Common Venue Liability Exposures

The most frequent premises liability claims at venues involve slip-and-fall incidents (wet floors, spilled concessions, ice/snow at entrances), negligent security (inadequate response to fights, assaults, active threats), structural hazards (seating failures, railing deficiencies, trip hazards), and crowd management failures (crush injuries, inadequate egress). Each category has a distinct evidentiary framework and damages profile [[Source-0433-R24-Legal-Liability]].

## Negligent Security

Venue operators face liability for criminal acts of third parties when the venue knew or should have known of the risk and failed to provide adequate security. Foreseeability is typically established through prior incident history at the venue, the nature of the event, and the surrounding area's crime statistics. The duty extends to providing adequate lighting, security staffing, surveillance, and emergency response capability [[Source-0433-R24-Legal-Liability]].

## Canadian Occupiers' Liability

Canadian provinces have enacted occupiers' liability statutes (e.g., Ontario Occupiers' Liability Act, Alberta Occupiers' Liability Act) that replace common law with a statutory duty of reasonable care owed to all persons entering the premises. The statutory framework simplifies the US common law categories (invitee/licensee/trespasser) into a single reasonable care standard, though the practical duty remains similar in scope [[Source-0433-R24-Legal-Liability]].
