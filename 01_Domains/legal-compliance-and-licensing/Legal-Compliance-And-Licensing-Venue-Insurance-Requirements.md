# Venue Insurance and Liability Requirements

Municipalities and venue operators commonly require commercial general liability (CGL) limits of $5M+ per occurrence, additional insured endorsements, and certificates of insurance (COIs) as conditions of event rental and contractor engagement.

## Overview

Venue insurance requirements establish the minimum coverage thresholds that protect facility owners, event organizers, contractors, and the public from financial exposure arising from incidents during events. These requirements are typically codified in rental agreements, municipal permitting conditions, and contractor engagement terms.

## Commercial General Liability (CGL) Requirements

- Standard CGL limits for major venues range from $2M to $5M+ per occurrence, with aggregate limits often set at $10M or higher
- Per-occurrence limits protect against single-incident exposure; aggregate limits cap total annual payouts
- Umbrella or excess liability policies commonly required to bridge the gap between primary CGL limits and venue-mandated thresholds
- Municipal requirements vary by jurisdiction — some cities mandate specific coverage floors as conditions of event permitting

## Additional Insured vs. Certificate Holder

- **Additional insured** endorsement extends the policyholder's coverage to a named third party (e.g., the venue owner), providing direct rights under the policy
- **Certificate holder** designation merely confirms that coverage exists — it does not extend coverage to the named party
- Venues typically require additional insured status, not just certificate holder designation, to ensure direct recourse under the event organizer's or contractor's policy
- The distinction is operationally critical: a certificate holder has no right to file a claim under the policy, while an additional insured does

## COI Tracking and Administration

- Certificates of insurance must be collected and verified prior to load-in or contractor access
- Tracking systems range from manual spreadsheet-based processes to dedicated COI management platforms
- Key verification points include: coverage dates (must span the full event period), named insured accuracy, additional insured endorsement presence, and limit adequacy
- Expired or non-compliant COIs should trigger automatic hold on venue access until resolved

## Exhibitor and Tenant Insurance

- Trade shows and conventions typically require exhibitors to carry their own CGL coverage, with the show organizer and venue named as additional insureds
- Permanent tenants (restaurants, retail operators within a venue) carry ongoing policies with the venue owner as additional insured
- Minimum limits for exhibitors are commonly set at $1M per occurrence, with higher thresholds for food service or high-risk activations
