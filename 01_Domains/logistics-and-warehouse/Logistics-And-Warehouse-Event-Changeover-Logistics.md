# Event Changeover Logistics

A changeover (also "flip" or "turn") is the complete operational sequence of dismantling one event setup, cleaning and resetting the venue, and constructing the next event configuration — all within a defined time window. Changeover speed is a primary scheduling constraint limiting revenue density for multi-use venues, as every hour of changeover time represents locked-out revenue across ticketing, concessions, merchandise, and rental [[Source-0395-Ticket-Fairy-Rapid-Changeovers]].

## Arena Changeovers

Arena changeovers are the most operationally intensive in the venue industry, driven by the NHL/NBA dual-use model and concert programming. Documented benchmarks include:

- **United Center** (Chicago): 50-person crew, 2.5 hours for hockey-to-basketball, involving 530 decking panels, 232 court panels, and 130 pieces of boards and glass [[Source-0396-Hussey-Seating-Changeover]]
- **American Airlines Center**: 40-person crew in ~3 hours standard; 60-person crew under 2 hours; six color-coded teams with 3,500 lower-bowl seats repositioned via hydraulic lifts [[Source-0396-Hussey-Seating-Changeover]]
- **Climate Pledge Arena** (Seattle): demonstrated 3-hour concert-to-NHL conversion (best case); 12 hours as comfortable timeline [[Source-0395-Ticket-Fairy-Rapid-Changeovers]]
- **Crypto.com Arena** (Los Angeles): six playoff games in 80 hours — approximately one turnover every 12 hours [[Source-0395-Ticket-Fairy-Rapid-Changeovers]]

Large arena overnight changeovers require 80–100+ specialized personnel working overlapping shifts, with a Changeover Director as single point of coordination [[Source-0395-Ticket-Fairy-Rapid-Changeovers]].

## Critical Path Analysis

Complex changeovers require formal critical path mapping — identifying the longest sequential chain of tasks that determines minimum completion time. For an arena ice-to-concert changeover, the critical path typically runs: ice protection/floor decking → stage load-in → rigging → audio → lighting focus → safety sign-off. Tasks that can be parallelized (cleaning, FOH reset, AV pre-rigging) occur simultaneously off the critical path [[Source-0395-Ticket-Fairy-Rapid-Changeovers]].

## IATSE Jurisdiction

IATSE collective agreements govern production-related changeover work in North American venues. Key provisions include a minimum 4-hour changeover call, overtime after 8 hours, mandatory 8-hour rest periods, and exclusive jurisdiction over sets, scenery, sound equipment, lights, and stage machinery. Venue operators must account for IATSE jurisdiction when planning changeovers involving touring productions [[Source-0397-IATSE-Contract-2023-2027]].

## Convention Centre Room Turns

Convention centre changeovers range from 15–45 minute breakout room resets to multi-day exhibit hall transformations. Move-out for 200,000+ sq ft trade shows typically takes 1–2 days, followed by 4–8 hours of hall restoration. Multi-concurrent events require coordinated scheduling to prevent cross-contamination of changeover activity between halls [[Source-0395-Ticket-Fairy-Rapid-Changeovers]].

## Technology and Optimization

Changeover technology includes digital floor plans (EMS-based), mobile checklists with real-time completion dashboards, dedicated operations command centres with CCTV feeds, automated rigging systems, and push-button retractable seating. Pre-positioning incoming event equipment before the outgoing event concludes and conducting mock changeover drills during off-hours are among the most effective acceleration tactics, with documented gains of 15–30 minutes per changeover from storage layout optimization [[Source-0395-Ticket-Fairy-Rapid-Changeovers]] [[Source-0396-Hussey-Seating-Changeover]].
