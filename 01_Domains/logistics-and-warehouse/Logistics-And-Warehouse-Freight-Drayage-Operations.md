# Freight and Drayage Operations

Freight and drayage operations encompass the end-to-end management of exhibitor materials, touring production equipment, and operational supplies moving through venue loading docks, across show floors, and between facilities. General service contractors (GSCs) — primarily Freeman and GES in North America — hold exclusive material handling authority on trade show floors as contracted by show organizers [[Source-0400-Freeman-Freight-Process]] [[Source-0401-GES-Trade-Show-Freight]].

## Exhibitor Freight Flow

The exhibitor freight lifecycle follows five stages: advance warehouse receiving (up to 30 days prior), show site delivery via coordinated truckloads, dock receiving with bill of lading verification, material handling (drayage) from dock to booth space, and outbound shipping in reverse sequence. The GSC bears custody of freight through the entire chain from advance warehouse to dock delivery [[Source-0402-DTS-Advance-Warehouse]] [[Source-0400-Freeman-Freight-Process]].

## Drayage Billing

Drayage in the US is billed per hundredweight (CWT — per 100 lbs), typically with a 300-lb minimum. Advance warehouse handling rates run approximately 20–25% higher than direct-to-show rates due to additional handling. The exclusive contractor model means no other party may move freight on the show floor using wheeled or motorized equipment — the "hand carry" threshold (items one person can carry without wheels) defines the exemption boundary [[Source-0401-GES-Trade-Show-Freight]].

## Touring Production Logistics

Concert touring represents the highest-velocity freight movement in the venue industry. Major touring productions require 30–60+ semi-trucks; mega-tours (e.g., Taylor Swift's Eras Tour) require approximately 80–90 trucks. Trucks are loaded in reverse order at the previous venue so items needed first at the next venue are at the truck door. Digital platforms like HET Hub provide real-time GPS tracking of production truck convoys [[Source-0395-Ticket-Fairy-Rapid-Changeovers]].

## Cross-Border Freight Compliance

Canadian venues hosting US-based shows face CBSA customs requirements. Shows approved under the International Events and Convention Services Program (IECSP) receive designated exemptions including potential duty-free entry and GST/HST relief for foreign exhibitor goods. Common errors include failing to confirm IECSP approval status before shipping and missing approval documentation at the border. Touring productions crossing the US-Canada border require licensed entertainment industry customs brokers and may use ATA Carnets for temporary importation [[Source-0409-CBSA-IECSP-Trade-Shows]].

## Freight Insurance and Liability

Liability shifts at each custody handoff — from carrier to GSC at dock, GSC to exhibitor during show, exhibitor back to GSC at dismantle. GSC contracts include per-CWT liability limits substantially lower than replacement value of high-value exhibit components. Exhibitors with valuable assets should carry separate event/exhibition insurance covering the gap [[Source-0401-GES-Trade-Show-Freight]].
