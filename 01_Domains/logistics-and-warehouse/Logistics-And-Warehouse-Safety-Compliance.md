# Logistics Safety and Regulatory Compliance

Venue logistics operations are governed by a comprehensive regulatory framework spanning forklift operations, fall protection, energy isolation, ergonomics, and hazardous materials handling. US venues operate under OSHA standards while Canadian venues operate under structurally parallel but provincially administered occupational health and safety legislation [[Source-0403-OSHA-1910-178-Forklift]] [[Source-0407-Alberta-Manual-Handling-Hazards]].

## Forklift Operations (OSHA 29 CFR 1910.178)

Every powered industrial truck operator must be trained and certified before solo operation, with refresher training every 3 years or immediately following unsafe operation. Pre-shift inspections are mandatory. Venue environments present uniquely high pedestrian risk because forklifts operate in spaces that also include event attendees, exhibitors, and service staff — not the controlled warehouse environment for which most PIT safety programs are designed. Pedestrian accidents account for more than 25% of all forklift-related accidents [[Source-0403-OSHA-1910-178-Forklift]].

## Fall Protection (OSHA Subpart D)

Fall protection is required for employees on walking-working surfaces 4 feet or more above a lower level. In venue logistics, this applies to dock operations with fall exposure, warehouse storage access on elevated platforms, and rigging/changeover work at height. Dockboards must be secured against movement with wheel chocks during loading [[Source-0404-OSHA-Subpart-D-Walking-Surfaces]].

## Lockout/Tagout (OSHA 29 CFR 1910.147)

LOTO procedures apply to dock leveler maintenance, conveyor systems, HVAC equipment, forklift battery systems, ice resurfacers, and stage automation. The procedure requires: notify affected employees, shut down equipment, isolate energy sources, apply locks, verify de-energization before beginning work. Lockout is preferred over tagout [[Source-0405-OSHA-LOTO-1910-147]].

## Ergonomics for Changeover Crews

Changeover and event setup work involves high-volume repetitive lifting, carrying, pushing, and pulling. NIOSH guidelines specify team lifting for heavy/bulky objects, task rotation, and powered equipment for loads over long distances. Alberta OHS guidance recommends lifts between knee and chest height (the "low-risk zone") with clear verbal coordination for team lifts. OSHA has no specific mandatory ergonomics standard but the General Duty Clause requires employers to address recognized ergonomic hazards [[Source-0406-NIOSH-Ergonomic-Guidelines]] [[Source-0407-Alberta-Manual-Handling-Hazards]].

## Hazardous Materials (OSHA 1910.1200 / NFPA 30 / WHMIS)

Venues store corrosives (bleach), flammables (floor strippers), refrigerants, generator fuel, and pest control chemicals. OSHA's HazCom standard requires a written program, SDS for all chemicals, proper labeling, and worker training. NFPA 30 limits flammable storage to 60 gallons per approved cabinet with 18-gauge steel construction and 2-inch raised door sill. In Canada, WHMIS 2015 (aligned with GHS) provides equivalent requirements [[Source-0408-NFPA-30-Flammable-Storage]].

## Canadian Provincial Framework

Canadian provinces administer parallel but distinct OHS frameworks. Alberta and Ontario both require joint health and safety committees for workplaces with 20+ workers, right-to-refuse-unsafe-work provisions, and incident reporting with scene preservation. The Canadian Centre for Occupational Health and Safety (CCOHS) provides supplementary guidance on warehouse safety and manual handling [[Source-0407-Alberta-Manual-Handling-Hazards]].
