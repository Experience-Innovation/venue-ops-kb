# Marketing Analytics and Attribution

Marketing analytics for venues integrates digital campaign data, ticketing transactions, in-venue behavior, and CRM records to measure marketing effectiveness, attribute revenue to specific channels, and optimize budget allocation [[Source-0429-Salesforce-Venue-CRM]].

## Attribution Modeling

Multi-touch attribution assigns credit for ticket purchases and revenue across the marketing touchpoints a buyer encountered. Venue-applicable models include first-touch (awareness credit), last-touch (conversion credit), linear (equal credit across all touchpoints), and data-driven (algorithmic weighting based on historical conversion patterns). The unique challenge for venues is connecting digital marketing touchpoints to physical ticket purchases that may occur through multiple channels (online, box office, reseller) [[Source-0429-Salesforce-Venue-CRM]].

## Channel Performance Measurement

Key channel metrics for venue marketing include: email (open rate, click-through rate, revenue per send), social media (engagement rate, reach, conversion rate to ticket page), paid digital (cost per acquisition, ROAS, view-through attribution), direct mail (response rate, revenue per piece), and owned media (website traffic, app engagement, push notification effectiveness). Channel performance should be benchmarked by event type, as marketing mix effectiveness varies significantly between concert, sports, and convention audiences [[Source-0429-Salesforce-Venue-CRM]].

## Marketing Mix Optimization

Budget allocation across channels should be driven by marginal return analysis — shifting budget from channels with diminishing returns to channels with growth potential. Venue marketing budgets typically allocate 40–60% to digital channels, 15–25% to owned media, 10–20% to earned/social, and 5–15% to traditional. Post-event analysis comparing marketing spend to actual revenue by event enables continuous mix optimization [[Source-0429-Salesforce-Venue-CRM]].
