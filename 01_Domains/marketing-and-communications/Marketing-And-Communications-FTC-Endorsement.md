# FTC Endorsement and Influencer Compliance

FTC Endorsement Guides (16 CFR Part 255) requiring clear disclosure of material connections between endorsers and brands, with 2023 revisions expanding coverage to tags, likes, and likeness depictions, applied to venue influencer programs and sponsored content.

## Overview

The Federal Trade Commission's Endorsement Guides (16 CFR Part 255) establish the legal framework for truthful advertising through endorsements and testimonials in the United States. These guides require that any material connection between an endorser and a brand be clearly and conspicuously disclosed. The 2023 revisions significantly expanded the scope of the guides to address modern social media practices, making them directly relevant to venue influencer programs, sponsored suite experiences, and social media partnerships.

## Key Components

### Core Requirements (16 CFR Part 255)

- Material connection disclosure — any relationship between an endorser and a brand that might affect the weight or credibility of the endorsement must be disclosed
- Conspicuous placement — disclosures must be clear and prominent, not buried in hashtag strings or placed where consumers are unlikely to notice them
- Honest opinions — endorsements must reflect the honest opinions, findings, beliefs, or experience of the endorser
- Substantiation — advertisers are liable for claims made through endorsements, the same as if they made the claims directly

### 2023 Revisions

- Expanded coverage to include tags, likes, and other social media interactions that function as endorsements
- Likeness depictions — using a person's image or likeness in advertising may constitute an endorsement requiring disclosure
- Virtual influencers and AI-generated endorsements are now explicitly addressed
- The revised guides clarify that both the endorser and the brand can be held liable for non-disclosure

### Venue Application

- Influencer campaigns — venues providing complimentary tickets, suite access, or hospitality in exchange for social media coverage must ensure influencers disclose the relationship using clear language such as #ad or #sponsored
- Sponsored suite hosts — brands hosting guests in sponsored suites who post about the experience must disclose the sponsorship
- Team and performer sponsorships — sponsored content featuring venue events must carry appropriate disclosure
- Staff and employee posts — venue employees posting about their employer's events on personal accounts may trigger disclosure requirements

### Cross-Border Applicability

- US law applies if sponsored posts reach US consumers, regardless of where the endorser is located
- Canadian concurrent standards exist under the Competition Act and Ad Standards Canada guidelines
- Venues hosting international events or working with international influencers must consider multi-jurisdiction compliance

## Venue Type Variations

The FTC Endorsement Guides apply equally across all venue types, but the intensity of influencer marketing activity varies. Concert venues and arenas with active social media programs and influencer partnerships face the highest compliance volume. Convention centres working with industry influencers for trade show promotion must ensure exhibitor-sponsored content carries appropriate disclosures. All venue types must train marketing staff on disclosure requirements and include compliance language in influencer partnership agreements.
