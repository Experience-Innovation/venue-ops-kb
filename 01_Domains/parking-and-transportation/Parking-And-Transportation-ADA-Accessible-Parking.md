# ADA Accessible Parking Requirements

2010 ADA Standards requiring minimum accessible and van-accessible parking spaces per facility, with scoping formulas, technical requirements for space dimensions and access aisles, and accessible route requirements from parking to venue entrances.

## Overview

The 2010 ADA Standards for Accessible Design establish mandatory minimum requirements for accessible parking at all facilities open to the public, including venue parking lots and structures. The standards define scoping formulas (how many accessible spaces are required based on total parking supply), technical specifications for space dimensions and access aisles, and requirements for accessible routes connecting parking to venue entrances. Compliance is a federal civil rights obligation, not a building code option.

## Key Components

- **Scoping formulas** — the number of required accessible spaces scales with total parking supply: lots with 1-25 total spaces require 1 accessible space; 26-50 require 2; 51-75 require 3; 76-100 require 4; 101-150 require 5; 151-200 require 6; 201-300 require 7; 301-400 require 8; 401-500 require 9; for 501-1,000 spaces, 2% of total are required; for 1,001+ spaces, 20 plus 1 for each 100 over 1,000 ([[Source-0137-ADA-Standards-Accessible-Design]])
- **Van-accessible spaces** — at least one of every six accessible spaces (or fraction thereof) must be van-accessible, requiring a minimum 132-inch-wide space with a 60-inch-wide access aisle and 98-inch minimum vertical clearance ([[Source-0138-US-Access-Board-Parking]])
- **Standard accessible spaces** — minimum 96-inch-wide parking space with a 60-inch-wide access aisle; access aisles must connect to an accessible route
- **Accessible route to entrances** — ADA Section 206.2.1 requires an accessible route from accessible parking spaces to the accessible building entrance; the route must be the shortest practical distance and must not require traveling behind parked vehicles
- **Per-facility calculation** — accessible space counts are calculated per parking facility (lot or structure), not aggregated across the entire venue campus; each lot must independently meet its scoping requirement

## Regulatory Requirements

- The 2010 ADA Standards for Accessible Design (28 CFR Part 36) are enforceable by the U.S. Department of Justice
- Accessible spaces must be located on the shortest accessible route to the accessible entrance
- Signage requirements include the International Symbol of Accessibility and "Van Accessible" designation where applicable
- Surface requirements: accessible spaces and access aisles must be firm, stable, and slip-resistant with maximum 1:48 slope
- Facilities that fail to provide required accessible parking are subject to DOJ enforcement actions and private lawsuits

## Venue Type Variations

Large venue campuses with multiple parking facilities must calculate accessible space requirements independently for each lot or structure. Venues with temporary or overflow parking for events must maintain accessible space ratios in temporary configurations. Parking structures require additional attention to vertical clearance for van-accessible spaces and accessible route design across levels.
