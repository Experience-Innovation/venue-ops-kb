# Event-Day Parking Accessibility

Operational requirements ensuring ADA compliance during events when parking configurations change for VIP, media, or overflow areas, including maintaining accessible space counts, proximity to entrances, and accessible shuttle vehicles.

## Overview

Event-day parking operations frequently reconfigure standard parking layouts to accommodate VIP areas, media compounds, production staging, and overflow parking. These reconfigurations can inadvertently reduce accessible parking availability, relocate accessible spaces further from entrances, or create barriers on accessible routes. ADA requirements remain fully enforceable during temporary event configurations — the venue's obligation to provide accessible parking does not diminish because of event-day operational needs.

## Key Components

- **Temporary lot reconfiguration rules** — when parking areas are repurposed for VIP, media, or production use, the accessible space count for the remaining public parking must still meet ADA scoping requirements based on the available supply ([[Source-0137-ADA-Standards-Accessible-Design]])
- **VIP/media zone compliance** — VIP and media parking zones that are open to credentialed members of the public must independently provide accessible spaces; zones restricted to employees may fall under different requirements
- **Overflow lot requirements** — temporary overflow parking activated for high-demand events must include accessible spaces at the required ratio, with accessible routes to shuttle pickup or venue entrances
- **Accessible shuttle vehicles** — when shuttle service connects remote parking to the venue, a portion of the shuttle fleet must be wheelchair-accessible with lifts or ramps; the DOJ has clarified that inaccessible shuttles serving accessible parking lots create a compliance gap ([[Source-0149-ADA-Network-Event-Planning]])
- **Event-specific parking plans** — DOJ guidance recommends that venues develop event-specific accessible parking plans documenting how accessible spaces, routes, and shuttle service will be maintained under each event configuration
- **Proximity to entrances** — accessible spaces must remain on the shortest accessible route to the venue entrance, even when lot configurations change; relocating accessible spaces to remote areas of a lot violates the proximity requirement

## Regulatory Requirements

- ADA requirements apply to all temporary and event-day parking configurations
- The DOJ has issued guidance clarifying that event-specific reconfiguration does not reduce the venue's accessible parking obligations
- Venues may face complaints and enforcement actions if event-day configurations reduce accessible parking below required minimums
- Staff training must cover accessible parking maintenance during lot reconfiguration

## Venue Type Variations

Stadiums and arenas with large surface lots face the greatest reconfiguration complexity on event days. Convention centres hosting concurrent events may need to manage accessible parking allocation across multiple event organizers. Amphitheatres and fairground-exhibition venues with temporary or grass-surface parking face additional challenges in maintaining firm, stable, slip-resistant surfaces for accessible spaces and routes.
