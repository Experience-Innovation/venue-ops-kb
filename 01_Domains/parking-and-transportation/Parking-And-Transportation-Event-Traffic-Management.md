# Event Traffic Management

Coordinated traffic management strategies for venue events covering road access control, wayfinding, transit agency coordination, rideshare integration, and temporary measures such as road closures, one-way systems, and dedicated lanes, guided by FHWA national frameworks.

## Overview

The Federal Highway Administration's (FHWA) "Managing Travel for Planned Special Events" handbook provides the national framework for event traffic management in the United States. This framework establishes a structured approach to planning, implementing, and managing traffic operations for events that generate travel demand beyond normal conditions. Effective event traffic management requires coordination across venue operators, municipal transportation departments, transit agencies, law enforcement, and transportation network companies (TNCs).

## Key Components

### Traffic Management Plan (TMP)

- A TMP is the core planning document, typically required as a condition of the event permit from local departments of transportation
- Key TMP components include site access plans, parking allocation maps, corridor flow routes, transit and shuttle integration, and lane control strategies
- TMPs define pre-event, event, and post-event traffic phases with distinct operational protocols for each

### Road Access and Flow Control

- Temporary road closures around venue perimeters create pedestrian-priority zones
- One-way traffic systems on surrounding streets increase throughput during ingress and egress
- Dedicated lanes for buses, emergency vehicles, and rideshare services separate traffic streams
- Variable message signs (VMS) provide real-time wayfinding to available parking facilities

### Agency Coordination

- Local DOT approval is typically a condition of the event permit, requiring advance TMP submission
- Law enforcement provides traffic control at key intersections during ingress and egress peaks
- Transit agencies coordinate additional service frequency or special event routes

### Industry Examples

- The Syracuse Carrier Dome has implemented comprehensive traffic management plans integrating multiple access corridors for events
- Super Bowl LIII demonstrated large-scale multi-agency traffic coordination including transit integration, road closures, and designated rideshare zones across a metropolitan area

## Venue Type Variations

Stadium and arena events generate the most concentrated traffic surges due to fixed start times and mass simultaneous egress. Convention centres experience more distributed arrival and departure patterns across the day. Fairground and exhibition events may span multiple days with varying daily attendance, requiring adaptive traffic plans that scale with demand.
