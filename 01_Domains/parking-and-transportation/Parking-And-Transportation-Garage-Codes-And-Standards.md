# Parking Garage Codes and Standards

Building code and safety requirements for parking structures covering lighting (IES RP-20), ventilation (IMC/ASHRAE 62.1/NFPA 88A), and fire protection (NFPA 88A/NFPA 13), with the 2023 NFPA 88A edition requiring sprinkler systems in all parking garages.

## Overview

Parking garages at large venues are governed by an overlapping framework of lighting, ventilation, and fire protection standards. These codes address the unique hazards of enclosed or semi-enclosed structures where vehicles concentrate — including carbon monoxide accumulation, limited natural light, fire risk from fuel loads, and pedestrian safety in vehicle-dominant environments. Compliance requires coordinated design across mechanical, electrical, and fire protection disciplines.

## Key Components

### Lighting Standards (IES RP-20)

- IES RP-20 is the primary reference for parking facility illuminance levels
- Minimum illuminance ranges from 0.2 foot-candles (fc) for basic open lots to 10 fc for ramps and entrances where visibility transitions occur
- Vertical illuminance requirements apply where CCTV surveillance is deployed, ensuring cameras can capture usable imagery
- Uniformity ratios prevent dark spots that create both safety and security hazards
- Stairwells, elevators, and pedestrian walkways within garages require higher illuminance than driving lanes

### Ventilation Requirements

- IMC Section 404.1 establishes baseline mechanical ventilation requirements for enclosed parking garages
- ASHRAE 62.1 specifies a ventilation rate of 0.75 CFM per square foot for enclosed garages
- NFPA 88A specifies a higher rate of 1 CFM per square foot for parking structures
- Carbon monoxide (CO) and nitrogen dioxide (NO2) detection systems are required to modulate ventilation fans based on actual pollutant levels
- Demand-controlled ventilation using CO/NO2 sensors reduces energy consumption while maintaining air quality

### Fire Protection

- NFPA 88A (Standard for Parking Structures) is the primary fire protection standard for garages
- The 2023 edition of NFPA 88A introduced a universal sprinkler requirement — all parking garages must now be protected by automatic sprinkler systems per NFPA 13
- Prior editions allowed trade-offs between open-air design and sprinkler protection; the 2023 edition eliminated these exceptions
- Means of egress requirements ensure pedestrians can exit safely during a fire event
- Structural fire resistance ratings vary based on garage type (open, enclosed, underground) and height

## Venue Type Variations

Arena and stadium garages typically handle high-volume surge loading before and after events, requiring robust egress design beyond minimum code. Convention centre garages may operate at moderate capacity for extended periods across multi-day events. Underground garages at performing arts centres and integrated resorts face the most stringent ventilation and fire protection requirements due to limited natural ventilation.
