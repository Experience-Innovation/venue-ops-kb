# Ride-Share Zone Management

Designated pick-up/drop-off (PUDO) zones for rideshare services at large venues, with geofenced areas coordinated with TNCs, capacity planning, signage, traffic marshals, and neighborhood impact mitigation strategies.

## Overview

The rapid growth of transportation network companies (TNCs) such as Uber and Lyft has made dedicated pick-up/drop-off (PUDO) zone management a critical component of venue transportation planning. Without designated zones, rideshare activity disperses into surrounding neighborhoods, creates traffic conflicts at venue entrances, and degrades the guest experience through long wait times and confusion. Effective PUDO zone management requires coordination between the venue, TNC platforms, local transportation authorities, and on-site traffic operations staff.

## Key Components

### PUDO Zone Design

- Designated zones are sited to separate rideshare traffic from pedestrian flows, bus operations, and general vehicle traffic
- Multi-lane queuing configurations allow multiple simultaneous pick-ups and reduce wait times
- Clear signage directs guests from venue exits to the appropriate PUDO zone
- Lighting and weather protection improve the guest experience at pick-up locations

### Geofencing and TNC Coordination

- Geofencing in rideshare apps directs drivers to designated PUDO zones rather than allowing ad hoc stops
- Venue operators coordinate with TNC platforms to establish virtual pick-up pins that align with physical zone locations
- Pin placement is designed to avoid conflicts with emergency access routes and ADA loading zones

### Operational Staffing

- Staffed traffic marshals manage vehicle flow within PUDO zones during peak post-event egress
- Marshals verify rider-driver matches and maintain queue discipline
- Staffing levels scale with anticipated rideshare volume based on historical event data

### Neighborhood Impact Mitigation

- PUDO zones are sited to prevent rideshare staging from spilling into residential streets
- FHWA guidance on drop-off planning addresses the integration of TNC activity into broader event traffic management plans
- Some venues establish secondary PUDO zones at a distance from the venue to distribute traffic load

### Industry Examples

- SoFi Stadium has implemented a comprehensive rideshare management program with designated PUDO zones, geofenced TNC coordination, and staffed traffic marshals to manage post-event rideshare demand

## Venue Type Variations

Arenas and stadiums face the most intense PUDO demand due to simultaneous post-event egress of large crowds. Convention centres experience more distributed rideshare activity throughout the day. Amphitheatres often have constrained site access that limits PUDO zone placement options. Fairground and exhibition venues may require temporary PUDO zones that shift location based on event configuration.
