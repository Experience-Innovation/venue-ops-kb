# Premium Revenue Models and Pricing

Luxury suites sold across multi-year leases, partial season packages, and single-event rentals, with bundled F&B as yield tool and all-inclusive club programs expanding from ultra-premium to larger segments.

## Overview

Premium hospitality revenue models have evolved from simple multi-year suite leases into a diversified portfolio of pricing structures. The shift reflects changing buyer behavior — fewer corporations commit to long-term fixed contracts, while demand grows for flexible, per-event, and partial-season options. Bundled food and beverage has emerged as a key yield management tool, with all-inclusive programs expanding beyond ultra-premium tiers into larger club seat segments.

## Key Components

- **Per-event pricing** — ALSD benchmark data shows 84% of venues now offer per-event premium pricing options, up from predominantly season-long commitments ([[Source-0122-ALSD-Premium-Seat-Benchmarks]])
- **Party suite F&B bundling** — 68% of party suites include food and beverage in the package price, compared to 41% of traditional suites, reflecting the trend toward all-inclusive models ([[Source-0122-ALSD-Premium-Seat-Benchmarks]])
- **All-inclusive club programs** — entry-level all-inclusive club seat packages start at approximately $25 per person for food and beverage, providing venues with guaranteed per-capita revenue regardless of individual spending patterns ([[Source-0123-SBJ-Premium-Seating-Facilities]])
- **Multi-year leases** — traditional suite leases of 3-10 years remain a revenue anchor but represent a declining share of total premium revenue as venues diversify their product mix
- **Partial season packages** — half-season and quarter-season suite packages that reduce buyer commitment while maintaining premium inventory utilization
- **Guaranteed per-cap revenue** — all-inclusive pricing converts variable concession spending into predictable per-person revenue, simplifying forecasting and reducing service complexity

## Industry Context

The revenue model evolution is driven by the need to maximize yield across a broader buyer base. All-inclusive pricing reduces friction at the point of consumption, increases perceived value, and simplifies operational delivery — staff focus on replenishment rather than transactions. The $25/person entry point for all-inclusive club programs positions bundled F&B as accessible beyond the traditional ultra-premium tier.

## Venue Type Variations

Arenas with 40-80 event nights per year can sustain per-event premium pricing more effectively than stadiums with 8-10 home dates, where season-package models remain more common. Stadium premium revenue strategies increasingly rely on non-game-day event utilization to justify premium infrastructure investment.
