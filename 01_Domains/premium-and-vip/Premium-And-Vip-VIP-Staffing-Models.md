# VIP Staffing Models

Layered staffing models adapted from luxury hotel workflows, with specific roles for premium operations including managers, concierges, attendants, and escalation paths for service delivery and recovery.

## Overview

Premium hospitality areas require staffing models that deliver service levels comparable to luxury hotels, adapted for the high-volume, time-compressed environment of live events. The staffing approach is layered — each role has a defined scope, escalation path, and guest touchpoint — ensuring consistent service delivery across suites, clubs, and VIP lounges.

## Key Elements

- **Premium operations manager** — oversees all premium areas for a given event; responsible for pre-event setup verification, staff deployment, and real-time issue resolution
- **Suite/club concierge** — dedicated guest-facing role providing personalized service to suite holders and club members; handles advance communication, special requests, and relationship continuity across events
- **Suite attendants** — assigned to specific suite blocks (typically 4-6 suites per attendant); responsible for in-suite food and beverage service, restocking, and guest comfort
- **Housekeeping/turnover staff** — dedicated to suite and club area reset between events and during intermissions; ensures presentation standards are maintained throughout the event
- **Personal escorts** — assigned to high-value guests for arrival through departure; provide wayfinding, access facilitation, and a single point of contact
- **Advance communication** — pre-event outreach to suite holders and VIP guests confirming arrangements, gathering special requests, and setting service expectations ([[Source-0124-Salesforce-Kraft-Sports-CRM]])
- **Proactive check-ins** — scheduled touchpoints during events (typically at 30-minute intervals) rather than reactive service only
- **Service recovery logging** — documented process for capturing, escalating, and resolving service failures in premium areas, with follow-up protocols to restore guest confidence ([[Source-0123-SBJ-Premium-Seating-Facilities]])

## Industry Context

The luxury hotel workflow adaptation reflects the premium hospitality industry's recognition that guest expectations in suites and clubs are set by their experiences at high-end hotels, not by general admission event experiences. Staffing ratios in premium areas are significantly higher than general concourse operations, and training programs emphasize anticipatory service, personalization, and discretion.

## Venue Type Variations

Arenas and stadiums deploy the full layered model for game-day and concert operations. Convention centres adapt the model for high-profile events, galas, and trade show VIP programs, with concierge roles often blended with event coordination. Performing arts centres typically operate with smaller premium footprints and may combine the concierge and attendant roles.
