# AIPC Quality Standards Certification

The only sector-specific quality standard built for convention centre management, covering 11 operational areas with Silver, Gold (2-year), and Platinum (3-year) certification levels requiring external audit.

## Overview

The AIPC (International Association of Convention Centres) Quality Standards program is the only quality certification framework designed specifically for convention centre operations. Unlike general-purpose frameworks such as Baldrige or EFQM, the AIPC standards address the unique operational realities of managing large-scale meeting and exhibition facilities.

## 11 Operational Areas

The certification covers 11 operational areas that span the full scope of convention centre management:

- **Customer Service** — client engagement, satisfaction measurement, and service recovery
- **Facilities and Operations** — building management, maintenance, and operational effectiveness
- **Employee Relations** — workforce development, engagement, and retention
- **Health, Safety, Security, and Emergency** — risk management, emergency preparedness, and occupational safety
- **Cybersecurity** — digital infrastructure protection and data governance
- **Inclusivity** — accessibility, diversity, and equitable service delivery
- **Sustainability** — environmental management, resource efficiency, and reporting
- **Financial Integrity** — fiscal management, transparency, and accountability
- **Community Engagement** — civic partnerships, social impact, and stakeholder relations

## Certification Levels

Three certification tiers reflect progressive maturity:

- **Silver** — foundational standards met across all operational areas
- **Gold** — advanced practices with 2-year certification validity
- **Platinum** — best-in-class performance with 3-year certification validity

All certification levels require external audit by qualified assessors, ensuring independent verification of compliance.

## Venue Sector Context

The AIPC Quality Standards program has been adopted by convention centres globally. Halifax Convention Centre achieved Gold re-certification in January 2026, demonstrating the ongoing commitment required to maintain certification. The program's convention-centre-specific design means its criteria directly address operational challenges — from exhibition hall turnovers to delegate registration flow — that generic frameworks do not explicitly cover.
