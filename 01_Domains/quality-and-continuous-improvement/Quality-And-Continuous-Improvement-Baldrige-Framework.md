# Baldrige Performance Excellence Framework

U.S. Presidential Award for performance excellence using a 1,000-point scoring system across seven categories with Results weighted at 450 points, ADLI logic for process scoring, and LeTCI for results scoring. Redesigned in 2024 to emphasize organizational resilience.

## Overview

The Baldrige Performance Excellence Framework, administered by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST), is the most comprehensive organizational assessment model in the United States. Established by the Malcolm Baldrige National Quality Improvement Act of 1987, the framework provides a systems perspective for understanding performance management across seven interdependent categories.

## Seven Categories and Point Allocation

The framework distributes 1,000 points across seven categories, with Results carrying the heaviest weight:

- **Leadership** — 120 points
- **Strategy** — 85 points
- **Customers** — 85 points
- **Measurement, Analysis, and Knowledge Management** — 90 points
- **Workforce** — 85 points
- **Operations** — 85 points
- **Results** — 450 points

The 450-point weight on Results reflects the framework's emphasis on demonstrated outcomes over process descriptions alone.

## Scoring Logic

The framework uses two distinct scoring methodologies:

- **ADLI** (Approach-Deployment-Learning-Integration) — used for evaluating process categories (1-6). Assessors examine whether an approach is systematic, how broadly it is deployed, whether learning and improvement cycles exist, and how well it integrates across the organization.
- **LeTCI** (Levels-Trends-Comparisons-Integration) — used for evaluating Results (category 7). Assessors examine current performance levels, trends over time, comparisons with benchmarks and competitors, and integration of results across key areas.

## 2024 Resilience Redesign

The 2024 edition of the Baldrige Criteria introduced significant restructuring to emphasize organizational resilience, reflecting post-pandemic lessons about the need for adaptive capacity in all sectors. This redesign affects how venues approaching the framework should structure their self-assessment.

## State-Level Alliance Programs

Beyond the national award, the Alliance for Performance Excellence coordinates state-level award programs, with over 1,000 organizations participating annually through state and regional programs. These programs use the Baldrige Criteria and provide a stepping stone toward the national award.

## Venue Sector Context

The Ritz-Carlton Hotel Company remains the only hospitality-sector organization to win the national Baldrige Award, receiving it in 1992 and again in 1999. No venue — convention centre, arena, stadium, or performing arts centre — has won the national award. However, the framework's seven categories map directly to venue operational domains, making it a valuable self-assessment tool even without pursuing the formal award.
