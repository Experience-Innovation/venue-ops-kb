# EFQM Excellence Model

Dominant European quality framework reorganized in 2020 from 9 to 7 criteria grouped in Direction, Execution, and Results blocks with 1,000-point total, using RADAR scoring logic and recognition levels from Committed to Excellence through Global Excellence Award.

## Overview

The European Foundation for Quality Management (EFQM) Model is the most widely adopted organizational excellence framework in Europe, used across public and private sectors including venue and event management. Originally launched in 1991 with nine criteria, the model underwent a major restructure in 2020.

## 2020 Restructure

The current model organizes seven criteria into three blocks:

- **Direction** — Purpose, Vision & Strategy; Organizational Culture & Leadership
- **Execution** — Engaging Stakeholders; Creating Sustainable Value; Driving Performance & Transformation
- **Results** — Stakeholder Perceptions; Strategic & Operational Performance

The 1,000-point total is distributed across these blocks, with the model emphasizing cause-and-effect linkages between what an organization does (Direction and Execution) and what it achieves (Results).

## RADAR Scoring Logic

RADAR provides the assessment and scoring methodology:

- **Results** — What the organization achieves
- **Approach** — What the organization plans to do and why
- **Deployment** — How the organization implements the approach
- **Assessment** — How the organization monitors and reviews effectiveness
- **Refinement** — How the organization learns and improves

RADAR operates as a continuous improvement cycle, with assessors evaluating maturity at each stage.

## Recognition Levels

The EFQM recognition scheme operates on a tiered basis:

- **Committed to Excellence** — entry level, demonstrating commitment to improvement
- **Recognized for Excellence** — 3-star, 4-star, and 5-star levels based on scoring thresholds
- **Global Excellence Award** — the highest tier, awarded through competitive assessment

The assessment process uses trained assessors who conduct consensus scoring and site visits for higher recognition levels.

## Venue Sector Relevance

While the EFQM Model is less commonly adopted by North American venues than Baldrige-derived frameworks, it is widely used by European convention centres, exhibition venues, and cultural facilities. Its emphasis on stakeholder engagement and sustainable value creation aligns well with the multi-stakeholder nature of venue operations.
