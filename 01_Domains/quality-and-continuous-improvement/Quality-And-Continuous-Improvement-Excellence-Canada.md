# Excellence Canada Organizational Excellence Standard

Canadian Award for Excellence conferred under Governor General authority, using the 2021 Organizational Excellence Standard (OES) with six drivers and Bronze-Silver-Gold-Platinum certification requiring external verification.

## Overview

Excellence Canada is the Canadian national authority for organizational excellence, administering the Canadian Award for Excellence under the authority of the Governor General of Canada. The organization's 2021 Organizational Excellence Standard (OES) provides a comprehensive framework for Canadian organizations across all sectors, including venue operations.

## Six OES Drivers

The Organizational Excellence Standard is structured around six interdependent drivers:

- **Leadership** — governance, vision, values, and accountability
- **Planning** — strategic planning, goal setting, and resource allocation
- **Customers** — understanding, engagement, and satisfaction measurement
- **Our People** — workforce development, engagement, health, and well-being
- **Processes** — design, management, improvement, and innovation
- **Partners** — supplier management, community engagement, and collaboration

Each driver is assessed across progressive maturity levels, with organizations demonstrating increasing sophistication as they advance through certification tiers.

## Certification Levels

Excellence Canada offers four certification levels, each requiring external verification:

- **Bronze** — foundational practices in place
- **Silver** — systematic approaches deployed across the organization
- **Gold** — evidence of learning and improvement cycles
- **Platinum** — sustained, best-in-class performance with demonstrated results

External assessors conduct on-site verification at higher certification levels. The Canadian Award for Excellence represents the pinnacle of recognition within this system.

## Venue Sector Relevance

The Excellence Canada framework is particularly relevant to Canadian convention centres, arenas, and performing arts centres that operate under public-sector governance structures. Its six drivers map closely to the operational domains that define venue management, and the progressive certification model provides a structured improvement pathway.
