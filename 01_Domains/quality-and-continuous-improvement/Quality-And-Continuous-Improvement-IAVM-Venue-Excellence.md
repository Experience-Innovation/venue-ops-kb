# IAVM Venue Excellence Award

Annual award recognizing 5 IAVM-member venues scored on four weighted criteria: operational efficiency (40%), team building and professional development (20%), safety and security (20%), and service to the community (20%).

## Overview

The Venue Excellence Award (VEA) is administered by the International Association of Venue Managers (IAVM) and recognizes up to five member venues annually for demonstrated excellence across four operational dimensions. It is the most prominent sector-specific recognition program for public assembly venues in North America.

## Four Weighted Criteria

Applications are scored against four criteria with unequal weighting:

- **Operational Excellence** (40%) — process effectiveness, resource management, and innovation in operations
- **Team Building and Professional Development** (20%) — workforce development programs, training initiatives, employee engagement, and succession planning
- **Safety and Security** (20%) — emergency preparedness, risk management programs, safety protocols, and security infrastructure
- **Service to the Community** (20%) — community engagement, social responsibility, economic impact, and civic partnerships

The 40% weight on the operational criterion reflects IAVM's emphasis on core venue management competency.

## IAVM Value Statements

The VEA is grounded in six IAVM value statements that define the association's vision of venue management excellence. These values inform the assessment criteria and provide a normative framework for evaluating applications.

## Application and Recognition

Winners are selected through a competitive application process. Venues that receive the award must wait three years before re-applying, ensuring that recognition rotates across the membership. The award is presented at the IAVM annual conference (VenueConnect).

## Venue Sector Scope

The VEA is open to all IAVM member venues across the public assembly spectrum: convention centres, arenas, stadiums, performing arts centres, amphitheatres, and fairground/exhibition facilities. This breadth of scope makes it the most inclusive venue-specific award program, though it does not use a standardized scoring rubric comparable to Baldrige or EFQM.
