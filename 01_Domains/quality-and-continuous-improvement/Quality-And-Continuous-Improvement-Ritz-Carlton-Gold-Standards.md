# Ritz-Carlton Gold Standards Service Framework

The Ritz-Carlton Gold Standards are a codified service-culture framework that has earned the hotel company two Malcolm Baldrige National Quality Awards (1992 and 1999) — the only service organization to achieve the distinction twice. The Gold Standards are carried by every employee as a printed card and are reinforced daily across more than 100 properties worldwide. The framework is notable for treating employees as internal customers and for decentralizing decision authority to the frontline through explicit dollar empowerment.

## Framework / Model / Program

The Gold Standards comprise seven interlocking components. The **Motto** ("We are Ladies and Gentlemen serving Ladies and Gentlemen") signals mutual dignity. The **Three Steps of Service** (warm welcome, anticipate and comply, fond farewell) structure every guest interaction. The **Credo** articulates the brand promise. The **Employee Promise** is the company's reciprocal commitment to staff. **12 Service Values** are specific, first-person behavioral expectations ("I build strong relationships and create Ritz-Carlton guests for life"). The **Daily Line-Up** is a 15-20 minute all-staff gathering held at every property, on every shift, to review a single Service Value, share guest stories (wow stories), and align the day. **Employee Empowerment** authorizes each employee — from housekeeper to general manager — to spend up to $2,000 per guest incident resolving problems without requesting manager approval.

## Operational Application in Venues

Hospitality-forward venues (integrated resorts, conference hotels attached to convention centres, premium arena clubs) adapt the Gold Standards by codifying their own motto, three-step service model, and service values, then instituting a daily line-up as the operational heartbeat. The Daily Line-Up is particularly transferable: a 15-minute pre-shift gathering reviewing one value and sharing a recent guest story costs little and builds culture faster than quarterly training. The $2,000 empowerment figure is adjusted to venue economics (convention centres often set $100-$500 frontline discretion), but the principle of pre-authorized service recovery dollars eliminates the most common guest-experience failure mode: a frontline staff member having to escalate a simple problem and forcing the guest to wait. Venues report faster service recovery, higher Net Promoter Scores, and reduced escalation volume to duty managers.

## Key Metrics / Outcomes

- Two-time Malcolm Baldrige National Quality Award winner (1992, 1999) — only service organization with dual wins
- $2,000 per-guest empowerment authority eliminates escalation delay on most service recovery situations
- Daily Line-Up operates across 100+ properties, every shift, as the cultural reinforcement rhythm
- 12 Service Values written in first person ("I") to drive personal ownership rather than departmental compliance
- Employee Promise establishes reciprocal obligation framework treating staff as internal customers
- Framework has been externally taught via Ritz-Carlton Leadership Center to thousands of organizations across industries
