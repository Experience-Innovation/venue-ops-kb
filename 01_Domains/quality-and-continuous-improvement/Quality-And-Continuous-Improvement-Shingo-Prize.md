# Shingo Prize for Operational Excellence

A 10-principle, 4-dimension framework focusing on cultural transformation and behavioral alignment, using a 1,000-point scoring system with Bronze (575-674), Silver (675-774), and Prize (775+) tiers.

## Overview

The Shingo Prize, administered by the Shingo Institute at Utah State University, is named after Japanese industrial engineer Shigeo Shingo. Unlike frameworks that emphasize process measurement and results reporting, the Shingo Model focuses on cultural transformation — the degree to which guiding principles drive ideal behaviors throughout an organization.

## Four Dimensions

The Shingo Model is structured as the "Shingo Diamond" with four interdependent dimensions:

- **Cultural Enablers** — the leadership behaviors and systems that create the right environment
- **Continuous Improvement** — the tools, techniques, and practices that drive ongoing improvement
- **Enterprise Alignment** — the management systems that align work with organizational purpose
- **Results** — the measurable outcomes that demonstrate enterprise-wide performance

The diamond visualization emphasizes that culture enables improvement, alignment sustains it, and results validate it.

## 10 Guiding Principles

The Shingo Model is anchored in 10 guiding principles organized across the four dimensions. These principles — such as "Respect Every Individual," "Lead with Humility," and "Seek Perfection" — are assessed through observable behaviors rather than documented processes. This behavioral focus distinguishes the Shingo approach from Baldrige and EFQM, which rely more heavily on documented systems and reported results.

## Scoring and Recognition Tiers

The 1,000-point scoring system yields three recognition tiers:

- **Bronze Medallion** — 575-674 points
- **Silver Medallion** — 675-774 points
- **Shingo Prize** — 775+ points

Assessment is conducted through on-site evaluation by trained examiners who observe behaviors, interview employees at all levels, and evaluate the degree to which principles are embedded in daily work.

## Venue Sector Relevance

The Shingo Model's emphasis on cultural alignment and frontline behavioral consistency is directly applicable to venue operations, where guest experience quality depends on the behaviors of hundreds of event-day staff. The framework's focus on respect, humility, and continuous improvement complements the more metrics-driven approaches of Baldrige and EFQM.
