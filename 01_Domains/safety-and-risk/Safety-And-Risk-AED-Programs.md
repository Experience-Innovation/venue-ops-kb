# AED Programs and Placement

Automated External Defibrillator programs for venues, covering placement density, maintenance, staff training, and the regulatory landscape across US and Canadian jurisdictions.

## Overview

AED (Automated External Defibrillator) programs are critical for venues hosting large gatherings. Sudden cardiac arrest survival decreases by approximately 10% for every minute without defibrillation, making the collapse-to-shock time target of 5 minutes or less essential.

## Placement Standards

- AED accessible within 3 minutes from any location in the venue
- Co-located with first aid stations
- Zone-based trained operators assigned per event
- Locations documented on venue medical plans and reviewed 30+ days pre-event

## Regulatory Landscape

### United States
- All 50 states have Public Access Defibrillation (PAD) laws with Good Samaritan immunity
- California requires AEDs in buildings >300 occupancy
- New York requires AEDs in public assembly buildings and state buildings
- Florida mandates AEDs in schools

### Canada
- Manitoba: Mandatory since January 31, 2014 (first province)
- British Columbia: Mandatory under Bill M 219 (2024)
- Ontario: Bill 141 phased implementation
- Alberta: No mandatory AED legislation (voluntary)

## Maintenance

NFPA 99 governs AED maintenance schedules. Post-use debriefing and EMS registration are recommended best practices.
