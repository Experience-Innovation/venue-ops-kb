# Business Continuity Planning (BCP) for Venues

Frameworks for sustaining and restoring venue operations after disruption, covering Business Impact Analysis, recovery time objectives, IT disaster recovery, and infrastructure dependency planning.

## Overview

Business Continuity Planning (BCP) ensures venues can sustain or rapidly restore critical operations following a disruption. CISA's January 2026 guide specifically addresses dependency disruptions for stadiums and arenas across four infrastructure categories: communications, energy, transportation, and water/wastewater.

## Key Components

- **Business Impact Analysis (BIA):** Identifies critical functions and acceptable downtime
- **Recovery Time Objectives (RTO):** Maximum acceptable time to restore each function
- **IT Disaster Recovery:** Backup and recovery for ticketing, POS, security, and communications systems
- **3-2-1 Backup Rule:** 3 copies, 2 different media types, 1 off-site

## Critical Venue Systems

Priority systems for continuity planning:
- Ticketing and access control
- POS and payment processing
- PA/life safety and emergency notification
- HVAC and climate control
- Security surveillance and access control
- Internet and operational technology networks

## Infrastructure Dependencies

CISA guidance emphasizes planning for external dependency failures — power grid, telecommunications, water supply, and transportation access — that venues cannot directly control but must mitigate ([[Source-0002-CISA-Dependency-Disruptions-Guide-2026]]). The IAVM Venue Safety and Security Committee provides industry frameworks for integrating BCP into broader venue emergency preparedness programs ([[Source-0007-IAVM-Venue-Safety-Security-Committee]]).
