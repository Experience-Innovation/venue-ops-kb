# Crisis Communication Protocols

Pre-scripted messaging systems and technology chains for venue emergency communications, covering tiered announcements, mass notification platforms, and designated spokesperson protocols.

## Overview

Crisis communication protocols ensure accurate, timely information reaches all venue occupants, staff, and external responders during emergencies. Pre-scripted messaging for every scenario type eliminates decision paralysis under pressure.

## Announcement Tiers

1. **Pre-evacuation:** Informational, calm tone, prepare to move
2. **Full evacuation:** Directive, clear instructions, route guidance
3. **Active threat:** Specific protocol (shelter, evacuate, or lockdown depending on threat type)

## Technology Stack

- **PA/voice evacuation systems:** Primary notification for in-venue
- **SMS/push notification:** Secondary channel for mobile devices
- **Social media:** Real-time updates for incoming and departed guests
- **Mass notification platforms:** Single-platform multi-channel dispatch (Regroup, AlertMedia, CareHawk)
- **Integration with RapidSOS:** Precise location data to 911

## Best Practices

- Pre-scripted, scenario-specific statements for each hazard type
- Designated spokespersons trained in crisis communication ([[Source-0130-Fisher-Phillips-Crisis-Comms]])
- Tested technology chain validated through tabletop exercises
- Communication tree with clear escalation paths
- Bilingual/multilingual messaging for diverse audiences
- Five-step crisis communications framework: pre-crisis preparation, spokesperson designation, message development, media response protocols, and post-crisis review ([[Source-0130-Fisher-Phillips-Crisis-Comms]])
