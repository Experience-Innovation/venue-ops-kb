# Fire Watch Protocols

Procedures for maintaining fire watch when fire protection systems are impaired, during construction, or during events involving pyrotechnics or open flame.

## Overview

Fire watch is required when fire alarm or suppression systems are impaired beyond specified thresholds, during construction activities, or during events involving pyrotechnics or open flame.

## Trigger Conditions — NFPA 101 and NFPA 25

NFPA 101 Life Safety Code specifies time-based thresholds for fire watch activation ([[Source-0300-NFPA-101-Fire-Watch-Trigger-Thresholds]]):

| Trigger Condition | Threshold | Code Section | Action Required |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fire alarm system out of service | >4 cumulative hours in 24-hr period | NFPA 101 Section 9.6.1.6 | Notify AHJ; evacuate or provide approved fire watch |
| Automatic sprinkler system out of service | >4 cumulative hours in 24-hr period | NFPA 101 Section 9.7.6.1 | Notify AHJ; evacuate or provide approved fire watch |
| Water-based fire protection system out of service | >10 hours in 24-hr period | NFPA 25 Section 15.5.2 | Evacuate, fire watch, temporary water supply, or approved ignition-source program |

The 4-hour threshold is **cumulative** (not consecutive) within a 24-hour period. The fire watch obligation triggers when the sum of all outages exceeds 4 hours, not when a single continuous outage reaches that duration ([[Source-0300-NFPA-101-Fire-Watch-Trigger-Thresholds]]).

**NFPA 101 fire watch definition** (Section 3.3.108 / 3.3.91): "The assignment of a person or persons to an area for the express purpose of notifying the fire department, the building occupants, or both of an emergency; preventing a fire from occurring; extinguishing small fires; or protecting the public from fire or life safety dangers" ([[Source-0300-NFPA-101-Fire-Watch-Trigger-Thresholds]]).

Additional trigger conditions include:
- During and for 60 minutes after hot work (welding, cutting)
- During large-scale construction or renovation
- During events with pyrotechnics, open flame, or cooking

## IFC Comparison

International Fire Code (IFC) Section 901.7 ("Systems Out-of-Service") takes a stricter approach than NFPA 101: it requires notification and fire watch or evacuation **immediately** when required fire protection systems are out of service, with no time-delay threshold ([[Source-0301-IFC-901-7-Systems-Out-of-Service]]). The code official is empowered to waive the requirement where there is no substantial life-safety risk. IFC Section 901.7 is adopted by reference in state and local fire codes including New York, California, and New Jersey ([[Source-0301-IFC-901-7-Systems-Out-of-Service]]).

IFC Section 3504.2 requires fire watch during hot work activities to continue for **not less than 30 minutes** after the conclusion of hot work ([[Source-0301-IFC-901-7-Systems-Out-of-Service]]).

## Requirements

- Dedicated fire watch personnel with no dual-tasking
- Continuous patrol of affected areas
- Direct communication with fire department
- Pre-event survey and 7-day prior notification (Calgary requirement)
- Documentation of patrol rounds and observations

## Pyrotechnics-Specific — NFPA 1126

NFPA 1126 (2025 Edition) governs proximate pyrotechnics in venues. Specific setback distances with code section references ([[Source-0302-NFPA-1126-Pyrotechnics-2025-Edition]]):

| Requirement | Distance / Standard | Code Section (2025 Ed.) |
|---|---|---|
| Minimum audience separation | 15 feet or 2x fallout radius (whichever greater) | Section 8.4.1 |
| Concussion mortar setback | Minimum 25 feet in secured area | Section 8.4.2 |
| No-smoking zone | Within 25 feet | Section 8.5.5.1 |
| Trajectory of comets/mines | Not over audience | Section 8.2.10 |
| Airbursts over audience | Minimum height = 3x diameter of effect; no sparks within 15 ft of floor | Sections 8.2.13(1) and (2) |

**Edition numbering note:** The 2020 edition used different section numbering (audience separation at Section 6.4.1; concussion mortars at Section 6.4.2). Venue operators should confirm which edition is adopted by their local AHJ ([[Source-0302-NFPA-1126-Pyrotechnics-2025-Edition]]).

Additional requirements:
- Sprinkler system must be operational
- Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) permitting
- HVAC/smoke detector coordination to prevent false alarms

### Canada — Fireworks Operator Certificate (FOC)

In Canada, the Fireworks Operator Certificate is governed by the Explosives Act (R.S.C. 1985, c. E-17) and Explosives Regulations, 2013 (SOR/2013-211, ss. 387 and 423), issued by Natural Resources Canada, Explosives Regulatory Division ([[Source-0302-NFPA-1126-Pyrotechnics-2025-Edition]]). Six certification levels exist: Display Assistant, Pyrotechnician, Senior Pyrotechnician, Special Effects Pyrotechnician, Special Effects Pyrotechnician — Detonating Cord, and Visitor Pyrotechnician. Application fee is CAD $150.
