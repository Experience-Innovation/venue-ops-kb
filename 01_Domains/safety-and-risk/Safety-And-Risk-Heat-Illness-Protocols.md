# Heat-Related Illness Protocols

Venue protocols for preventing and responding to heat-related illness, using Wet Bulb Globe Temperature (WBGT) monitoring, cooling infrastructure, and evidence-based response procedures.

## Overview

Heat-related illness is an escalating risk for outdoor venues and events. The Taylor Swift concert in Rio de Janeiro (2023) — resulting in one death linked to overcrowding, blocked exits, and inadequate ventilation — demonstrated the consequences of insufficient heat safety planning.

## Monitoring

- **WBGT (Wet Bulb Globe Temperature):** Gold standard for heat stress assessment
- OSHA proposed two-threshold system (2025): initial trigger at 80F WBGT, high-heat trigger at 90F WBGT
- Designated event meteorologist recommended for large outdoor events

## Mitigation Infrastructure

- Cooling areas and cool rooms distributed throughout venue
- Refillable water stations (not just vendor-sold water)
- Metered queue entry to prevent prolonged standing in sun
- Shade structures at standing areas and queues

## Staff Protocols

- Staff heat illness training mandatory
- Acclimatization protocols for multi-day events
- Heat illness recognition and response procedures
- Mandatory hydration breaks for event staff
