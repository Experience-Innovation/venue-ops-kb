# Life Safety Evaluation (NFPA 101)

Formal evaluation required by NFPA 101 for assembly occupancies exceeding 6,000 occupants, covering egress, fire systems, emergency power, and command center operations.

## Overview

NFPA 101 requires a formal Life Safety Evaluation for assembly occupancies exceeding 6,000 persons. This evaluation goes beyond standard fire protection to assess the complete life safety system of the venue.

## Scope of Evaluation

- Egress capacity and arrangement
- Fire alarm and detection systems
- Automatic sprinkler systems and smoke control
- Emergency lighting and exit signage
- Elevator operations during emergencies
- Emergency power and lighting
- Fire command center operations
- Crowd management plans

## Key Requirements

- Must be approved by the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ)
- Annual evaluation required for venues above the occupant threshold
- A Life Safety Building Systems Reference Guide must be maintained for large assembly occupancies
- Contains key operational information used for event planning

## 2024 Updates

The 2024 edition of NFPA 101 expanded requirements to include security features alongside fire-safety procedures in emergency action plans, reflecting the evolving threat landscape for public assembly venues.
