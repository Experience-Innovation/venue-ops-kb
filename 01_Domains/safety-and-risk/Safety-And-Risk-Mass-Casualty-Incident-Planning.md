# Mass Casualty Incident (MCI) Planning

Pre-event planning frameworks for mass casualty events at venues, including triage systems, casualty collection points, hospital coordination, and escalation triggers.

## Overview

Mass Casualty Incident (MCI) planning prepares venues for events where the number of casualties overwhelms available medical resources. Planning includes tiered trigger criteria, pre-designated triage zones, ambulance staging, hospital coordination, and family reunification areas.

## Triage Systems

- **START Triage** (Simple Triage and Rapid Treatment): Four-category system (Black/Red/Yellow/Green) originating from Newport Beach Fire Department and Hoag Hospital. Most widely used in North America.
- **SALT Triage** (Sort, Assess, Lifesaving Interventions, Treatment/Transport): Alternative system with additional assessment steps.

## Tiered Response

- **Code Yellow:** Minor MCI, local resources adequate
- **Code Orange:** Moderate MCI, mutual aid requested
- **Code Red:** Major MCI, full regional activation

## Staffing and Resources

Pre-event MCI escalation plans must address ambulance staging, helicopter landing zones where applicable, casualty collection points, and pre-arranged agreements with receiving hospitals. Medical plan pre-approval is recommended 30 days prior to events.
