# Medical Staffing Models for Mass Gatherings

Evidence-based frameworks for determining medical staffing levels at venue events, using patient presentation rates and tiered response time benchmarks.

## Overview

Medical staffing at mass gatherings follows evidence-based models that scale with attendance, event type, and risk profile. The Patient Presentation Rate (PPR) — patients per 1,000 attendees — is the primary planning metric.

## Staffing Ratios

Evidence-based minimums (from Saunders et al. and NIH StatPearls):
- **Physician:** 1 per 5,000-50,000 attendees
- **Registered Nurse:** 1 per 2,600-15,000 attendees
- **EMT/Paramedic:** 1 per 2,600-65,000 attendees
- **Mobile medical team:** 1 per 20,000 attendees

## Response Time Benchmarks

- **BLS (Basic Life Support):** Within 4 minutes from any location
- **ALS (Advanced Life Support):** Within 8 minutes
- **Transport to hospital:** Within 30 minutes

## Patient Presentation Rates

- General benchmark: 0.5-2 patients per 1,000 spectators
- 95.97% of presentations are mild severity
- 2023 Ryder Cup benchmark: 16.5 per 10,000 attendees

## Tiered Planning Model

Events scale staffing by size: <5K (basic), 5K-25K (intermediate), >25K (advanced with physician oversight and MCI capability).
