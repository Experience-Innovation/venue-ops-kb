# Medical Staffing Models for Mass Gatherings

Evidence-based frameworks for determining medical staffing levels at venue events, using patient presentation rates and tiered response time benchmarks.

## Overview

Medical staffing at mass gatherings follows evidence-based models that scale with attendance, event type, and risk profile. The Patient Presentation Rate (PPR) — patients per 1,000 attendees — is the primary planning metric.

## Staffing Ratios

The foundational staffing framework originates from **Sanders AB et al. (1986)** (Ann Emerg Med 15(5):515-9, PMID 3963529), a study of 15 Arizona facilities providing public events ([[Source-0291-Sanders-1986-Mass-Gathering-Medical-Care]]). The comprehensive current statement of staffing ratios comes from the NAEMSP Position Statement resource document by Schwartz et al. (2015) ([[Source-0293-NAEMSP-Mass-Gathering-Position-Statement-2015]]).

**Correction:** The previously cited "Saunders et al." is a misspelling. No independently authored "Saunders" mass gathering framework could be verified. The correct citation is **Sanders AB** (one 'd') ([[Source-0291-Sanders-1986-Mass-Gathering-Medical-Care]]).

| Staff Category | Recommended Ratio | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Physician | 1 per 5,000-50,000 attendees | StatPearls/NAEMSP ([[Source-0292-NIH-StatPearls-EMS-Mass-Gatherings-2023]]) |
| Nurse | 1 per 2,600-15,000 attendees | StatPearls/NAEMSP ([[Source-0292-NIH-StatPearls-EMS-Mass-Gatherings-2023]]) |
| EMT | 1 per 2,600-65,000 attendees | StatPearls/NAEMSP ([[Source-0292-NIH-StatPearls-EMS-Mass-Gatherings-2023]]) |
| Paramedic | 2 (or 1 paramedic + 1 EMT) per 10,000 | PMC review |
| Basic First Aid | 1 per 1,000 attendees | PMC review |

## Response Time Benchmarks

The response time standards originate from Sanders 1986 and are cited by the NIH StatPearls chapter and the NAEMSP Position Statement ([[Source-0291-Sanders-1986-Mass-Gathering-Medical-Care]], [[Source-0293-NAEMSP-Mass-Gathering-Position-Statement-2015]]):

- **BLS (Basic Life Support):** Within 4 minutes from any location
- **ALS (Advanced Life Support):** Within 8 minutes
- **Transport to hospital:** Within 30 minutes

## Patient Presentation Rates

The 0.5-2 per 1,000 spectator benchmark is supported by three independent studies ([[Source-0292-NIH-StatPearls-EMS-Mass-Gatherings-2023]]):

| Author | Year | Finding |
|---|---|---|
| Arbon P, Bridgewater FH, Smith C | 2001 | PPR = 0.992/1,000 across 201 Australian mass gatherings (12M+ total attendees) |
| Milsten AM, Maguire BJ, Bissell RA, Seaman KG | 2002 | Literature review confirmed 0.5-2 per 1,000 as observed range across diverse events |
| Sanders AB et al. | 1986 | Foundational US survey establishing baseline reference range |

### 95.97% Mild Presentations Statistic

This specific figure originates from **Locoh-Donou S et al. (2013)** (Am J Emerg Med 31(5):843-6, PMID 23453125), a study analyzing EMS records from October 2009 to August 2011 at a southeastern US university, covering 79 mass gathering events with 670 patient presentations and 917,307 cumulative attendance ([[Source-0294-Locoh-Donou-2013-Mass-Gathering-Descriptive-Analysis]]). The finding: 95.97% of cases were mild (non-life-threatening, no transport); 27 cases required hospital transport; 3 were life-threatening ([[Source-0294-Locoh-Donou-2013-Mass-Gathering-Descriptive-Analysis]]).

## Tiered Planning Model

Events scale staffing by size: <5K (basic), 5K-25K (intermediate), >25K (advanced with physician oversight and MCI capability).

## US States with Codified Requirements

Seven US states and the District of Columbia have enacted legislation stipulating minimum onsite medical resources at mass gathering events: New York, Pennsylvania, Maryland, Georgia, Iowa, Oregon, and Washington D.C. ([[Source-0292-NIH-StatPearls-EMS-Mass-Gatherings-2023]]).
