# Tabletop and Emergency Exercises

Structured discussion-based and functional exercises that validate venue emergency response plans, test decision-making under pressure, and build coordination between internal staff and external responders.

## Overview

Tabletop exercises are structured activities following a six-step process that test emergency response plans through facilitated discussion without deploying resources. They sit within a broader exercise hierarchy: walkthrough, tabletop, functional, and full-scale.

## Exercise Types

- **Walkthrough:** Verbal review of plans with key personnel
- **Tabletop:** Discussion-based scenario with "injects" for adaptive decision-making
- **Functional:** Simulated response with communications and resource deployment
- **Full-scale:** Complete mobilization including evacuations, external agencies, and real-time coordination
- **Cyber tabletop:** Dedicated exercises for IT/OT security scenarios

## Key Resources

- FEMA VTTX (Virtual Tabletop Exercise) program provides no-cost, 4-hour structured exercises
- CISA recommends annual tabletop exercises for all venue types
- Some major arenas conduct annual full-scale evacuation exercises

## Best Practices

- Include all stakeholder groups: venue staff, security, medical, fire, law enforcement, AV/production
- Use realistic injects that force adaptive decision-making
- Document lessons learned and update EAPs based on findings
- Conduct separate cyber tabletop exercises for IT/OT systems
