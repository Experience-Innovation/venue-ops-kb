# Active Assailant Response Protocols

Frameworks for responding to active assailant incidents at venues, including the DHS Run-Hide-Fight model and the ALICE multi-option methodology.

## Overview

Active assailant preparedness is a critical security requirement for all venue types. Two primary frameworks exist, with venues increasingly adopting elements of both.

## Run-Hide-Fight (DHS/FBI)

Sequential, individual-focused framework:
1. **Run:** Evacuate if safe path exists
2. **Hide:** Find secure location, barricade, silence devices
3. **Fight:** Last resort — commit to aggressive action

In Canada, the variant "Run-Hide-Defend" is promoted by Workplace Safety & Prevention Services (WSPS).

## ALICE (Alert, Lockdown, Inform, Counter, Evacuate)

Group-oriented, multi-option framework developed by the ALICE Training Institute:
- Provides more options than sequential lockdown
- A 2018 peer-reviewed study found 50% reduction in persons shot in classroom settings and 58% in open areas compared to lockdown-only approaches

## Venue-Specific Considerations

- Pre-event staff briefings on active threat scenarios
- Pre-planned command posts with law enforcement/EMS coordination
- Behavioral threat assessment and pre-attack indicator identification
- Situational awareness training, escape/evade/attack, and bleeding control
- DHS SAFETY Act provides liability protection for qualified anti-terrorism technologies
- CISA Active Shooter Preparedness program and FEMA IS-907 training course
- California SB 553 (effective July 2024) mandates Workplace Violence Prevention Plans
