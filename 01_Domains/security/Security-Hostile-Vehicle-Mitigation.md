# Hostile Vehicle Mitigation (HVM)

Physical and deployable barrier systems protecting venue perimeters from vehicle-borne attacks, including crash-rated bollards, temporary barriers, and CPTED design principles.

## Overview

Hostile Vehicle Mitigation (HVM) protects venues from vehicle-borne attacks through physical barriers, landscape design, and traffic management. The January 2025 New Orleans Sugar Bowl vehicle attack underscored the critical need for effective HVM at all public assembly venues.

## Barrier Standards

- **ASTM F2656 (M50 rating):** US vehicle barrier testing standard
- **BSI PAS 68:** UK impact test specification for vehicle security barriers
- **IWA 14-1:** International vehicle security barrier standard (ISO/BSI)
- **K-4 to K-8 ratings:** US Department of State barrier crash ratings

## Barrier Types

- **Fixed bollards:** Permanent, crash-rated (e.g., FAAC JS Series — rated to stop 7.5-tonne vehicle at 80 km/h)
- **Retractable bollards:** Allow authorized vehicle access while maintaining perimeter security
- **Temporary/deployable systems:** For event-day deployment (e.g., Zaun RDS PAS68 crash fence)
- **Landscape-integrated:** Berms, planters, water features as HVM barriers

## Design Principles

- 50-foot separation zone between vehicle traffic and pedestrian areas
- Double fencing where applicable
- Serpentine vehicle approaches to reduce approach speed
- CPTED principles (natural surveillance, territorial reinforcement)
- ADA-compliant sections maintaining accessible routes through barriers
