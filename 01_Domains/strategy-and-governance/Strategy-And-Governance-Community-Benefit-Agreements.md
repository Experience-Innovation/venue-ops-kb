# Community Benefit Agreements for Venue Projects

Legally binding agreements between venue developers and community coalitions establishing local hiring targets, living wage commitments, affordable housing contributions, youth programming, and small-business procurement goals as conditions of public investment in venue construction.

## Overview

Community Benefit Agreements (CBAs) are legally binding contracts negotiated between venue developers (or public entities) and coalitions of community organizations, establishing specific commitments that the developer must fulfill in exchange for community support of the project. CBAs have become increasingly common in major venue construction and renovation projects, particularly where significant public subsidy or tax incentive is involved.

## CBA Structure

A typical CBA identifies the developer's obligations, the community coalition's representative organizations, and enforcement mechanisms. The agreement is legally enforceable as a contract, with provisions for monitoring, reporting, and remedies for non-compliance. Community coalitions typically include neighborhood associations, labor organizations, small business groups, affordable housing advocates, and youth-serving organizations.

## Local Hiring Targets

CBAs commonly establish percentage-based local hiring targets for both construction and permanent operational employment. Construction targets may require 30-50% of labor hours to be performed by workers residing within a defined geographic area. Permanent employment targets establish similar local hiring preferences for venue operations positions. First-source hiring agreements direct employers to local workforce development programs as the initial recruitment channel.

## Living Wage Commitments

Living wage provisions in CBAs establish minimum compensation levels above statutory minimum wage, typically benchmarked to local cost-of-living indices. These provisions may apply to direct venue employees, contracted service providers, and event-day temporary staff, ensuring that public investment in venue infrastructure supports sustainable employment.

## Affordable Housing and Community Investment

Large venue projects that drive surrounding property value increases may include affordable housing set-asides, anti-displacement protections, or direct financial contributions to community housing funds. These provisions address concerns that venue-driven development can displace existing residents through rising housing costs.

## Youth and Education Programming

CBAs frequently include commitments to youth programming, including sport-for-development initiatives, educational field trip partnerships, internship and apprenticeship pipelines, and free or reduced-price event access for youth organizations. These provisions leverage the venue as a community asset beyond its commercial programming.

## Economic Impact Measurement

Venues operating under CBAs typically commit to regular reporting on economic impact metrics, including local employment numbers, wages paid, local procurement spending, and community program participation. Standardized measurement frameworks enable comparison across projects and verification of compliance with CBA commitments.

## Workforce Development

Apprenticeship pipelines and workforce development partnerships connect venue construction and operations employment to structured career pathways. These programs provide training, certification, and advancement opportunities for community residents, creating sustained economic benefit beyond initial hiring commitments.
