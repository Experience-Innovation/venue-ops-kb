# Cross-Framework Operational Domain Mapping

Synthesis mapping how Baldrige, EFQM, Excellence Canada, AIPC, and IAVM frameworks categorize the same operational domains, enabling venues to leverage assessment work across multiple frameworks.

## Overview

The major quality and excellence frameworks used in the venue industry — Baldrige, EFQM, Excellence Canada, AIPC Quality Standards, and IAVM Venue Excellence Award — each evaluate organizational performance through their own category structures. Despite different terminology and weighting, these frameworks assess substantially overlapping operational domains. A cross-framework mapping reveals where assessment work for one framework can be directly leveraged for another, reducing the total effort required for venues pursuing multiple certifications or awards.

## 12 Shared Operational Domains

Analysis of the five major frameworks identifies 12 operational domains that appear, in some form, across multiple frameworks:

1. **Leadership** — governance, vision, accountability (Baldrige Category 1, EFQM Direction, Excellence Canada Driver 1, IAVM implicit)
2. **Strategy** — planning, goal-setting, resource allocation (Baldrige Category 2, EFQM Direction, Excellence Canada Driver 2)
3. **Customer Engagement** — satisfaction, service delivery, relationship management (Baldrige Category 3, EFQM Execution, Excellence Canada Driver 3, AIPC Area 1)
4. **Financial Performance** — fiscal management, revenue, efficiency (Baldrige Results, EFQM Results, AIPC Area 8)
5. **Workforce** — development, engagement, retention (Baldrige Category 5, EFQM Execution, Excellence Canada Driver 4, AIPC Area 3, IAVM Criterion 2)
6. **Processes and Quality** — operational management, improvement cycles (Baldrige Category 6, EFQM Execution, Excellence Canada Driver 5, AIPC Areas 1-2)
7. **Safety and Security** — emergency preparedness, risk management (AIPC Area 4, IAVM Criterion 3)
8. **Sustainability** — environmental management, resource efficiency (AIPC Area 7, EFQM implicit)
9. **Technology and Innovation** — digital infrastructure, cybersecurity (AIPC Area 5, Baldrige Category 4 partial)
10. **Community Impact** — civic engagement, social responsibility (IAVM Criterion 4, AIPC Area 9, Excellence Canada Driver 6)
11. **Data and Knowledge Management** — measurement systems, analytics, organizational learning (Baldrige Category 4, EFQM Assessment/Refinement)
12. **Inclusivity** — accessibility, diversity, equitable service (AIPC Area 6)

## Framework Transfer Value

The degree of transferability varies by domain:

- **High transfer** — Leadership, Strategy, Workforce, Customer Engagement. Evidence gathered for any one framework substantially satisfies the requirements of others.
- **Medium transfer** — Processes, Data/Knowledge, Financial. Category structures differ enough that evidence may need reframing, but the underlying operational reality is the same.
- **Low transfer** — Safety/Security, Sustainability, Cybersecurity, Inclusivity. These appear in sector-specific programs (AIPC, IAVM) but are not explicit categories in general frameworks (Baldrige, EFQM), so evidence must be generated specifically.

## Strategic Value for Venues

Venues preparing for multiple certifications or awards should map their evidence portfolio against this cross-framework structure early in the assessment cycle. A convention centre pursuing both AIPC Gold and Excellence Canada Silver, for example, can generate evidence once and deploy it across both assessment processes — provided the evidence is structured to address each framework's specific criteria language.
