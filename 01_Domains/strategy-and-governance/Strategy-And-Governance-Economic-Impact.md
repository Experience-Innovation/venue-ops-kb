# Venue Economic Impact Measurement

Economic impact measurement is the primary justification framework for public investment in venue infrastructure. Defensible impact studies follow established methodological standards; advocacy-grade studies that inflate multipliers or conflate gross spending with net impact undermine the credibility of the entire sector's public funding case [[Source-0434-R25-Venue-Community]].

## Three-Tier Impact Framework

Economic impact analysis measures three tiers: direct impact (spending by event attendees and organizers within the local economy), indirect impact (supplier purchases made by directly impacted businesses), and induced impact (household spending by employees of directly and indirectly impacted businesses). The combined effect is expressed as a multiplier applied to direct spending [[Source-0434-R25-Venue-Community]].

## IMPLAN and Input-Output Modeling

IMPLAN (Impact Analysis for Planning) is the most widely used input-output model for venue economic impact studies in North America. The model uses Bureau of Economic Analysis data to estimate multiplier effects by industry sector and geographic area. Credible studies specify the geographic boundary (city, MSA, state/province), use local IMPLAN multipliers (not national averages), and clearly distinguish between gross and net impacts [[Source-0434-R25-Venue-Community]].

## Credibility Standards

Destinations International's Economic Impact Calculator and CEIR's methodology provide venue-sector-specific approaches. Credible studies: count only out-of-area visitors (local spending is transferred, not generated), avoid double-counting, use conservative multipliers (convention centre multipliers typically range 1.4–1.8x), specify time-switching and casual displacement adjustments, and present results with methodology transparency. Studies commissioned by venues seeking public funding face inherent credibility risk if methodology is not independently validated [[Source-0434-R25-Venue-Community]].

## Convention Centre Impact

Convention centre impact studies typically focus on delegate spending — the amount convention attendees spend on hotels, restaurants, transportation, retail, and entertainment during their visit. Delegate spending per diem varies by convention type and city; tracking against room night generation provides the most direct link between venue activity and hotel tax revenue that funds venue operations [[Source-0434-R25-Venue-Community]].
