# MGM 2020 Organizational Efficiency Transformation

The MGM 2020 Plan was a multi-year enterprise transformation announced by MGM Resorts International in 2017 targeting $300M in annualized Adjusted EBITDA uplift by the end of 2020. The program is notable in the integrated resort industry for its explicit two-phase sequencing — operational efficiency first, digital transformation second — and for its durable results: by Q1 2022, regional operations Adjusted EBITDA margin was 882 basis points above the Q1 2019 baseline. The program provides a documented template for large multi-property venue operators pursuing centralization and digital modernization simultaneously.

## Framework / Model / Program

The MGM 2020 Plan was structured around two workstreams totaling $300M annualized EBITDA uplift. The **Operational Efficiency workstream** targeted $200M through three centralized centers of excellence: labor management (scheduling, productivity standards, centralized workforce planning), strategic sourcing (consolidated procurement across 20+ properties), and revenue optimization (yield management, pricing analytics, room inventory control). The **Digital Transformation workstream** targeted $100M through four capability builds: enterprise data platform, dynamic pricing engine, digital guest experience (app, kiosks, mobile check-in), and loyalty program modernization (M life Rewards). Governance was led by a dedicated transformation office reporting to the CFO, with property general managers accountable for local execution and retention of savings. The sequencing was deliberate: efficiency savings funded the digital investment, avoiding incremental capital asks.

## Operational Application in Venues

Multi-venue operators (integrated resort groups, arena portfolios, stadium management companies) apply the MGM 2020 model by first consolidating back-office functions — procurement, labor planning, revenue management — into shared services or centers of excellence, then reinvesting the efficiency savings into digital platforms (data warehouse, dynamic pricing, guest app, loyalty). Single-venue operators adapt the sequencing at smaller scale: centralize scheduling and procurement first, then invest in data and digital second. The governance structure — dedicated transformation office, CFO oversight, GM accountability for savings retention — is the transferable insight; transformations that do not assign local accountability for holding savings typically leak 30-50% of projected benefits within 18 months.

## Key Metrics / Outcomes

- $300M annualized Adjusted EBITDA uplift target, split $200M operational + $100M digital
- Q1 2022 regional operations EBITDA margin 882 basis points above Q1 2019 baseline (durable multi-year result)
- Three operational centers of excellence: labor, sourcing, revenue optimization
- Four digital capabilities: data platform, dynamic pricing, guest experience, loyalty
- Efficiency-first sequencing self-funded digital investment without incremental capital requests
- Dedicated transformation office under CFO with GM-level accountability for savings retention
