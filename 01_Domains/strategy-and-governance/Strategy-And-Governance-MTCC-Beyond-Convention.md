# MTCC Beyond Convention Culture of Innovation

The Metro Toronto Convention Centre (MTCC) 2025-2028 Business Plan documents a three-pillar operational excellence framework that formalizes continuous improvement at the governance level of a major publicly accountable convention centre. The framework is notable as a publicly disclosed convention-centre governance model with explicit, quantified performance targets — providing a transparent template other convention centres can adopt or adapt. The plan ties operational pillars directly to measurable staff, safety, and innovation outcomes.

## Framework / Model / Program

The MTCC operational excellence framework rests on three pillars. **Pillar 1: Continuous SOP Refinement** establishes standard operating procedures as living documents with scheduled review cycles, cross-departmental ownership, and documented change control — preventing SOP drift and ensuring new staff inherit current best practice. **Pillar 2: Leadership Continuity via Succession Planning** requires identified successors for every senior role, documented development plans, and regular cross-training so venue operations do not degrade during leadership transitions. **Pillar 3: Culture of Innovation via Beyond Convention** is MTCC's branded initiative for surfacing, testing, and scaling frontline improvement ideas — including structured employee suggestion channels, innovation sprints, and recognition for adopted ideas. The three pillars are governed at the executive team level with progress reported to the MTCC board through the 2025-2028 business plan cycle.

## Operational Application in Venues

Other convention centres adopt the MTCC model by codifying their own three-pillar framework in their strategic or business plan, assigning executive sponsors to each pillar, and publishing quantified targets. The SOP refinement pillar is applied through annual SOP review cycles, ownership matrices, and documented change logs. Succession planning is operationalized through named successor designations, development plan reviews, and scheduled cross-training. The innovation pillar is typically operationalized through a named initiative (e.g., "Beyond Convention"), quarterly innovation challenges, and visible recognition. The power of the MTCC model is the quantified targets — venues that adopt pillars without targets typically produce activity without outcomes.

## Key Metrics / Outcomes

- 85%+ employee satisfaction target across the business plan horizon
- 100% same-day incident reporting target (safety and operational)
- 10% annual increase in employee recognition events
- Three-pillar structure: SOP refinement, succession planning, innovation culture
- Publicly disclosed in 2025-2028 Business Plan providing transparent governance template
- Executive-level sponsorship for each pillar with board-level progress reporting
