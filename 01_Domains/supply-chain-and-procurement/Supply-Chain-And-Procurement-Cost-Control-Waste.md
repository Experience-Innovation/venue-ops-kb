# Venue Cost Control and Waste Reduction

Venue F&B cost control targets food cost percentage (COGS as a percentage of F&B revenue), with industry benchmarks varying by venue type and service model. Convention centre catering operations typically target 28–35% food cost; arena concessions target 20–30%. Every percentage point of food cost improvement flows directly to operating margin [[Source-0426-Foodbuy-GPO]].

## Data-Driven Purchasing

Procurement aligned to POS data and event booking forecasts reduces both over-ordering (waste) and under-ordering (lost revenue from stockouts). Historical per-cap consumption data by event type provides the demand signal for purchasing decisions. GPO spend analytics platforms enable category-level variance analysis identifying where actual spend deviates from budgeted food cost [[Source-0426-Foodbuy-GPO]].

## Waste Tracking and Reduction

Food waste in venue operations occurs at three points: prep waste (trim, overproduction), service waste (unsold inventory, buffet excess), and post-consumer waste (plate waste). Technology platforms like Leanpath and Winnow provide automated waste measurement through AI-enabled scales and cameras that photograph waste before disposal, categorizing waste by type and assigning financial value. Pre-consumer waste measurement typically identifies 5–8% of food purchased going to waste before reaching the guest [[Source-0427-Entegra-Sodexo-GPO]].

## Menu Engineering for Cost Control

Menu engineering applies demand and profitability analysis to optimize offerings: high-popularity/high-margin items (stars) receive prominent placement; high-popularity/low-margin items (plowhorses) receive cost engineering; low-popularity/high-margin items (puzzles) receive marketing emphasis; low-popularity/low-margin items (dogs) are candidates for removal. This framework applies to both concession menus and catered event menus [[Source-0426-Foodbuy-GPO]].

## Inventory Turn Optimization

Venue inventory turn targets differ from restaurant operations due to event-driven demand variability. Between events, perishable inventory should approach zero; non-perishable and frozen inventory turns should target 12–15x annually. Off-season inventory management requires deliberate drawdown schedules to prevent carrying cost accumulation during dark periods [[Source-0427-Entegra-Sodexo-GPO]].
