# F&B Supply Chain Resilience

F&B supply chain resilience in venue operations addresses the challenge of maintaining food and beverage continuity across event-driven demand spikes, supplier disruptions, and the structural complexity of venue procurement where 30–40% of non-ticket revenue depends on uninterrupted supply [[Source-0426-Foodbuy-GPO]].

## Dual Sourcing and Supplier Diversification

Best practice requires at least two approved suppliers for every critical F&B category (proteins, produce, dairy) to mitigate single-source failure risk. GPO contracts provide pre-negotiated backup suppliers, but venue procurement teams must verify that backup relationships are operationally live — not just contractually available. Local supplier relationships complement national GPO contracts by providing rapid response capability for short-notice substitution [[Source-0427-Entegra-Sodexo-GPO]].

## Safety Stock and Par Level Management

Venues maintain par stock levels calibrated to peak concurrent event demand plus a safety buffer. F&B par calculations must account for the perishability constraint — excess safety stock in proteins and produce creates waste cost. Dry goods and frozen products allow deeper safety stock without waste risk. The optimal balance between supply security and waste minimization is a data-driven exercise leveraging POS history and event booking forecasts [[Source-0426-Foodbuy-GPO]].

## Post-COVID Supply Chain Lessons

The COVID-19 pandemic exposed structural vulnerabilities in venue F&B supply chains: single-distributor dependency, just-in-time inventory practices that eliminated buffers, and labor shortages at distribution centers. Post-pandemic best practice includes maintaining relationships with at least two distributors (not just two suppliers), extending lead times for critical items, and incorporating supply chain resilience metrics into vendor scorecards [[Source-0427-Entegra-Sodexo-GPO]].
