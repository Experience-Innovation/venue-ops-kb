# Sustainable Procurement Mandates

Venue procurement increasingly operates under environmental and social sustainability mandates from municipal owners, LEED certification requirements, ISO 20121 event sustainability management systems, and audience expectations. These mandates create procurement constraints that must be balanced against cost efficiency and GPO contract compliance [[Source-0426-Foodbuy-GPO]].

## Local Sourcing Requirements

Many publicly owned convention centres and arenas operate under municipal "buy local" procurement policies requiring a percentage of food purchases from local producers (typically defined as within a specified geographic radius). These requirements can conflict with national GPO contracts that route purchasing through broadline distributors. Best practice integrates local sourcing targets into GPO contract negotiations, with carve-outs for locally sourced categories [[Source-0427-Entegra-Sodexo-GPO]].

## Single-Use Plastics Regulation

Municipal and provincial single-use plastic bans directly affect venue procurement of serviceware, straws, bags, and packaging. Canada's federal single-use plastics ban and various US municipal ordinances require procurement teams to source compliant alternatives — often at higher unit cost. GPO contracts increasingly include green-certified alternatives (Green Seal, EPA Safer Choice) that satisfy both regulatory compliance and LEED credit pathways [[Source-0426-Foodbuy-GPO]].

## Certified Product Targets

LEED certification, Green Seal, and venue sustainability programs establish minimum percentages for certified sustainable products across cleaning chemicals, paper goods, and food categories. Procurement dashboards must track certified-product purchasing percentages against targets, with GPO-reported spend data providing the primary tracking mechanism [[Source-0427-Entegra-Sodexo-GPO]].
