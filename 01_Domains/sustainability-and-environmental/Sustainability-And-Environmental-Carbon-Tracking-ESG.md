# Carbon Tracking and ESG Reporting

Venue operators increasingly track greenhouse gas emissions across Scope 1 (direct), Scope 2 (electricity), and Scope 3 (value chain) per the GHG Protocol, with ESG reporting frameworks (GRI, SASB, CDP) driving disclosure requirements for publicly traded operators.

## GHG Protocol Scopes

- **Scope 1:** Direct emissions — on-site combustion (boilers, generators), refrigerant leaks, fleet vehicles
- **Scope 2:** Indirect — purchased electricity and steam
- **Scope 3:** Value chain — attendee travel, supply chain, waste disposal, employee commuting

## Reporting Frameworks

- **GRI (Global Reporting Initiative):** Most widely used sustainability reporting standard
- **SASB (now ISSB):** Industry-specific disclosure standards for investors
- **CDP:** Climate disclosure platform — carbon, water, forests
- **TCFD:** Climate-related financial risk disclosure

## Venue-Specific Challenges

Scope 3 dominates venue emissions — attendee travel can represent 80-90% of total event footprint. Convention centres are developing per-event carbon calculators and offering carbon offset programs to event organizers.
