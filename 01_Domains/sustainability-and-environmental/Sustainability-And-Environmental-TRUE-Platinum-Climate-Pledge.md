# TRUE Platinum Waste Diversion (Climate Pledge Arena)

Climate Pledge Arena in Seattle achieved TRUE Platinum Certification in 2024 with a 93% waste diversion rate — the highest rate of any sports venue globally. The arena is also the first zero-carbon-certified arena in the world and the first US West Coast venue to earn TRUE Platinum. Its operational model — eliminating fan-facing single-use plastic and having housekeeping staff sort waste onsite at the point of collection — demonstrates that extreme diversion rates are achievable at scale when the program is engineered into daily operations rather than treated as an end-of-pipe cleanup activity.

## Framework / Model / Program

TRUE (Total Resource Use and Efficiency) certification, administered by Green Business Certification Inc., is a zero-waste facility certification with tiered levels (Certified, Silver, Gold, Platinum) based on waste diversion performance, program rigor, and continuous improvement mechanisms. Platinum requires sustained diversion above 90% and full integration of zero-waste principles across procurement, operations, and staff training. Climate Pledge Arena's model eliminates all single-use fan-facing plastic (cups, lids, utensils, bottles, straws), replaces disposables with compostable or reusable alternatives, and — critically — has housekeeping staff perform primary waste sorting at the point of collection rather than relying on fan self-sorting or downstream mechanical separation. This upstream sort architecture is the operational unlock that enables 93% diversion.

## Operational Application in Venues

Arenas and stadiums pursuing high diversion rates can adopt Climate Pledge Arena's two-part operational pattern: (1) upstream elimination — remove single-use plastic from all fan-facing F&B, merchandise, and amenity touchpoints; and (2) point-of-collection sorting — redesign housekeeping roles and workflows so waste is sorted into compost, recycling, and landfill streams at the moment of pickup rather than at a central sorting station. The model requires investment in compostable supply contracts, reusable container programs, housekeeping training, and bin signage, but avoids the capital expense of large mechanical sorting infrastructure. Transferable practices include compost-stream F&B procurement, staff sort training curricula, and fan-facing communications that explain the elimination strategy rather than asking fans to sort at bins.

## Key Metrics / Outcomes

- Waste diversion rate: 93% (highest of any sports venue globally)
- Certification: TRUE Platinum (2024)

- First US West Coast TRUE Platinum venue
- All single-use fan-facing plastic eliminated

- Transferable to other arenas, stadiums, and large-format event venues
