# Double LEED Platinum Convention Centre (VCC)

Vancouver Convention Centre (VCC) is the world's first double LEED Platinum convention centre, earning LEED v4 Existing Building Platinum in 2024 on top of its original new-construction LEED Platinum certification. The operational-phase certification validates sustained performance rather than design intent, with documented outcomes including 38% reduction in potable water use via blackwater treatment plant expansion, waste diversion increased from 50% to 75%, and 30% emissions reduction from a 2010 baseline. VCC's achievement sets a first-mover benchmark for the convention centre sector and demonstrates that design-phase sustainability commitments can be matched by operating-phase performance.

## Framework / Model / Program

LEED v4 Operations + Maintenance (O+M) Existing Buildings certification is distinct from new-construction LEED — it requires demonstrated operational performance over a measurement period across energy, water, waste, indoor environmental quality, and building management systems. Platinum is the highest tier and demands top-decile performance across all categories. VCC's program combines capital investments (blackwater treatment plant expansion enabling onsite water reclamation), waste-stream redesign (lifting diversion from 50% to 75%), and emissions-reduction programs tracked against a 2010 baseline. The "double Platinum" designation signals that the venue achieved Platinum in both new-construction (LEED-NC) and operations (LEED O+M v4) — a combination no other convention centre had reached at the time of certification.

## Operational Application in Venues

Convention centres pursuing LEED O+M Platinum can use VCC's playbook as a reference model: invest in onsite water reclamation infrastructure (blackwater treatment is the highest-impact single intervention for water-intensive event venues), redesign waste streams to achieve 75%+ diversion, and track emissions against a fixed historical baseline to demonstrate sustained reduction. The operational programs require coordinated investment across facilities engineering, event operations, F&B procurement, and housekeeping — a single-department program will not achieve Platinum. VCC also demonstrates that convention centres (which were traditionally underrepresented in LEED O+M leadership) can compete with office and institutional buildings at the top tier. The first-mover position creates marketing and event-booking differentiation for clients with sustainability procurement criteria.

## Key Metrics / Outcomes

- Certification: World's first double LEED Platinum convention centre (LEED v4 O+M Platinum, 2024)
- Potable water use: 38% reduction via blackwater treatment plant expansion
- Waste diversion: increased from 50% to 75%
- Emissions: 30% reduction from 2010 baseline
- First-mover position in convention centre sector for LEED O+M Platinum
- Operating under BC Pavillion Corporation ownership
