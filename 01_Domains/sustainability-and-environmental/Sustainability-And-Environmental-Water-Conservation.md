# Water Conservation Strategies for Venues

Large venues consume significant water through cooling towers, irrigation, restroom facilities, F&B operations, and ice-making, with conservation strategies including low-flow fixtures, cooling tower optimization, rainwater harvesting, and greywater reuse systems.

## Major Consumption Categories

- **Cooling towers:** 30-50% of total water use in large venues — cycles of concentration optimization is highest-impact intervention
- **Restroom fixtures:** 20-30% — low-flow and sensor-operated fixtures reduce consumption
- **Irrigation:** 10-20% — weather-based smart controllers and drought-tolerant landscaping
- **F&B operations:** 10-15% — pre-rinse spray valves, efficient dishwashing
- **Ice-making (arenas):** 5-10% — REALice cold water treatment reduces water heating energy

## Conservation Technologies

- **Smart irrigation controllers:** Weather-based scheduling, soil moisture sensors
- **Cooling tower optimization:** Conductivity controllers, side-stream filtration
- **Rainwater harvesting:** Collection from large roof areas for irrigation and cooling tower makeup
- **Greywater reuse:** Sink water reused for toilet flushing (where code permits)
