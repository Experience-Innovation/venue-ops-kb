# General Service Contractor Models

## Overview

The general service contractor (GSC) ecosystem sits between show organizers and exhibitors in the convention centre environment, providing the physical installation and logistics services that make trade shows operable. Major GSCs — Freeman, GES (now operating within merged Informa/Emerald structures), and Shepard — trace their origins to the early 20th century and provide material handling (drayage), booth installation and teardown, electrical services, AV, furniture rental, and exhibitor display services ([[Source-0361-Exhibit-City-News-Tradeshow-Evolution|Exhibit City News]]).

## Exclusive/Official Contractor Model

Under the exclusive/official contractor model, the show organizer (or venue) designates a single GSC as the exclusive provider for specific services — most commonly material handling. Shepard, for example, is contracted as the exclusive provider for material handling at some shows. This model simplifies logistics, creates accountability, and provides the venue and organizer with predictable pricing and labor coordination. The tradeoff is that exhibitors pay whatever the exclusive contractor charges and have no competitive alternative ([[Source-0362-NFMT-Exhibitor-Official-Services-Contractor|NFMT Contractor Info]]).

## Preferred/Approved Contractor Model

Under the preferred/approved contractor model, the show organizer establishes a list of approved vendors, designating one as "official" while permitting Exhibitor Appointed Contractors (EACs) — contractors hired directly by exhibitors — to operate under specified conditions. EACs must typically register with the show organizer, provide current insurance certificates, and comply with all venue and show rules. This model increases exhibitor flexibility and drives pricing competition among contractors but multiplies the number of vendors the show manager must coordinate on the show floor ([[Source-0363-CEDIA-EAC-Rules-Regulations|CEDIA EAC Rules]]).

## GSC Service Categories

The core service categories provided by GSCs include ([[Source-0361-Exhibit-City-News-Tradeshow-Evolution|Exhibit City News]]):

- **Material handling (drayage):** Receiving, staging, and delivering exhibit materials from loading dock to booth location
- **Booth installation and teardown:** Assembly and disassembly of exhibit structures
- **Electrical services:** Power distribution to individual booth locations
- **Audio-visual equipment:** Screens, projectors, and sound systems for exhibitor use
- **Furniture rental:** Tables, chairs, flooring, and display fixtures
- **Exhibitor display services:** Custom signage, graphics, and booth enhancement

## EAC Governance Requirements

When the preferred/approved contractor model is used, exhibitor-appointed contractors must comply with explicit governance requirements ([[Source-0363-CEDIA-EAC-Rules-Regulations|CEDIA EAC Rules]]):

- Register with the show organizer prior to the event
- Provide current certificates of insurance meeting venue minimums
- Comply with all venue safety and operational rules
- Coordinate with the official contractor on shared logistics (particularly material handling and loading dock access)
- Operate within designated areas and timelines

## Operational Implications

The choice between exclusive and preferred contractor models involves a direct tradeoff between logistical simplicity and exhibitor cost competitiveness. Convention centre operators and show managers must evaluate which model best serves the specific event — large, complex multi-day trade shows with hundreds of exhibitors benefit from the coordination efficiency of exclusive contractors, while smaller shows or those with exhibitors who bring sophisticated in-house installation teams may benefit from the flexibility of the EAC model.
