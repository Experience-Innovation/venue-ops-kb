# Service Provider Management

## Overview

Third-party service provider management represents one of the most operationally complex functions in venue operations, requiring the venue executive to simultaneously manage contractual performance, operational integration, and governance alignment across multiple outsourced services. The IAVM Venue Executive Competency Standards identify managing service provider contracts (Domain 6.F) as a "High Risk/Very Important" competency requiring 3-4 years of proficiency ([[Source-0351-IAVM-Venue-Executive-Competency-Standards|IAVM CVE Standards]]).

## F&B Concessionaire Contract Structures

Food and beverage concessions are typically the largest outsourced service contract at any public assembly venue. The primary concessionaires operating in North American venues — Levy, Aramark, Delaware North, and Sodexo Live (formerly Centerplate) — compete for long-duration anchor contracts that give them operational stability in exchange for upfront capital investment in equipment and branded infrastructure ([[Source-0352-SBJ-Legends-ASM-OKC-Arena-Deal|SBJ OKC Arena Deal]]).

Key structural features of modern concessionaire agreements include ([[Source-0352-SBJ-Legends-ASM-OKC-Arena-Deal|SBJ OKC Arena Deal]]):

- **Capital investment funds:** The OKC Paycom Center arrangement required the concessionaire to pay 1% of annual gross sales into a capital investment fund for periodic repair and replacement of F&B equipment
- **Joint selection governance:** The concessionaire at OKC was selected jointly by the city, Thunder, and Legends/ASM rather than by any single party — reflecting how multi-party governance complicates operator decisions
- **Contract duration:** Aramark signed a 20-year, $175M agreement with the MLB Athletics for F&B at their planned Las Vegas stadium, illustrating the length and capital commitment expected in new-build partnerships

The OCC (Ottawa Convention Centre) outsources most F&B services to Aramark, which employs 230 staff including chefs and service staff, while the OCC's own core team numbers only about 18 full-time staff — demonstrating the scale disparity between venue core staff and outsourced service provider workforces ([[Source-0360-Ontario-Auditor-General-MTCC-OCC|Ontario AG MTCC/OCC Audit]]).

## Security Contractor Integration

Security contractor management requires integration of the contractor's staff into the venue's incident command and emergency response framework. The contractor's supervisors must operate within the venue's security hierarchy, not parallel to it. Following the 2017 Manchester Arena bombing, security budgets for major touring events increased by as much as 25% ([[Source-0355-Artist-Tools-Promoter-Guide-2026|Artist.tools Promoter Guide]]).

## Service Provider Transition Management

Changing a major service provider — particularly an F&B concessionaire after a multi-year agreement — is one of the operationally highest-risk transitions in venue management. Key provisions that should be embedded in outgoing contracts include:

- **Termination transition period:** The outgoing provider continues operations at agreed rates during a specified handover period
- **Asset return:** Clear delineation of equipment ownership with transfer procedures
- **Knowledge transfer:** Operational documentation, vendor relationships, inventory records, and software access
- **IP licensing:** Critical software or proprietary processes may need licensing for operational continuity
- **Personnel provisions:** Whether the new provider can recruit key personnel from the departing provider
- **KPI continuity:** Performance standards maintained throughout the transition period

Executing a three-way governance agreement between the venue, outgoing provider, and incoming provider during the transition period is a recognized best practice ([[Source-0351-IAVM-Venue-Executive-Competency-Standards|IAVM CVE Standards]]).

## Operational Implications

Service provider management competency directly affects guest experience quality, financial performance, and operational resilience. The core challenge is maintaining consistent service delivery while managing contractual relationships with multiple parties whose financial incentives may not always align with the venue's operational priorities.
