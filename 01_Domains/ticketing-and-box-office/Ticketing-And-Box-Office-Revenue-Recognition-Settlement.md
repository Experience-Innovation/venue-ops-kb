# Revenue Recognition and Settlement

Ticket revenue accounting follows specific recognition standards that govern when revenue can be recorded and how post-event financial reconciliation occurs between venues and promoters.

## Revenue Recognition Standards

Under US GAAP (ASC 606) and IFRS 15, ticket revenue is recognized at the point in time when the performance obligation is satisfied — that is, when the event occurs [[Source-0313-IAVM-Arenas-Performance-Reporting-Handbook]].

Until the event occurs, cash received for ticket sales is recorded as **deferred revenue**, a liability on the balance sheet. Non-refundable deposits are similarly treated as deferred revenue until the venue fulfills its service obligation. If an event is cancelled and refunds are issued, the deferred revenue liability is reversed without any revenue being recognized [[Source-0313-IAVM-Arenas-Performance-Reporting-Handbook]].

This treatment has significant implications for venue financial management. A venue that sells tickets months in advance for a season of events carries substantial deferred revenue liabilities, and the timing gap between cash receipt and revenue recognition must be managed carefully for cash flow planning and financial reporting.

## Post-Event Settlement

Post-event settlement is the financial reconciliation process between the venue and the promoter following an event. In a rental deal, settlement calculates the promoter's net position [[Source-0313-IAVM-Arenas-Performance-Reporting-Handbook]]:

The calculation begins with gross ticket receipts, then subtracts the facility fee, house nut, applicable taxes and deductions, producing a final payment or reconciliation. The IAVM pro forma template provides the standardized settlement framework, including tracking gross potential (maximum possible revenue at full capacity), actual gross receipts, expenses by category, and final split calculations [[Source-0313-IAVM-Arenas-Performance-Reporting-Handbook]].

## Arena Concert Financial Terms

The IAVM Arena Pro Forma template captures the specific financial terms tracked in arena concert license settlements [[Source-0313-IAVM-Arenas-Performance-Reporting-Handbook]]:

**Artist Guarantee** is the fixed minimum payment to the artist regardless of ticket sales. **Deal Type** specifies the revenue sharing structure: guarantee only, "versus" (guarantee versus a percentage of net box office receipts, whichever is greater), or straight percentage [[Source-0313-IAVM-Arenas-Performance-Reporting-Handbook]].

**Gross Potential** represents the maximum possible gross ticket revenue at full capacity across all stated price levels. **Net Gross Receipts** equals gross receipts less facility fee, taxes, and applicable deductions [[Source-0313-IAVM-Arenas-Performance-Reporting-Handbook]].

**House Nut** covers event-specific venue operating expenses including security, staffing, equipment, and front-of-house operations. **Merch Split** defines the percentage of merchandise sales retained by the venue versus the artist (typically 20-35% to the venue). **Facility Fee** is the per-ticket fee paid to the venue, either on top of the ticket price or included in it. Premium/VIP split and F&B split terms are negotiated when applicable to the deal structure [[Source-0313-IAVM-Arenas-Performance-Reporting-Handbook]].

## Convention Centre Settlement

For convention centres, post-event settlement reconciles against the pro forma established at contracting. The settlement stage is the final step in the IAVM booking pipeline, where final accounting is performed against the contracted financial terms and revenue is recognized upon completion of the event [[Source-0311-IAVM-Convention-Center-Performance-Reporting-Framework]].
