# Subscription and Season Ticket Management

Performing arts subscription models (music, dance, theatre series) that guarantee recurring revenue, incentivize artistic risk-taking, and require specialized ticketing platform features including auto-renewal, seat-priority logic, and series cross-mapping.

## Overview

Performing arts centres support subscription models that guarantee recurring revenue and incentivize artistic risk-taking, with subscriptions often comprising 40-60% of house capacity. This revenue model is structurally different from single-event ticketing at stadiums or convention registration. Specialized ticketing platforms support complex subscription tasks such as auto-renewal windows, seat-priority logic, and series cross-mapping tailored to multi-show performing arts seasons.

## Key Functions

- Auto-renewal window management with early-bird pricing incentives
- Seat-priority logic preserving subscriber seating preferences across seasons
- Series cross-mapping allowing subscribers to swap between performance dates
- Targeted renewal campaign management integrated with subscriber CRM
- Subscriber benefits administration (pre-sale access, parking, lobby events)

## Technology Platforms

Performing arts ticketing systems such as Theatre Manager and TicketPeak implement subscription-specific logic not found in standard sports or general-admission ticketing platforms. Features include complex renewal campaigns, subscription series packaging, seat priority algorithms, and subscriber communication workflows.

## Venue Type Variations

PACs rely most heavily on subscription revenue, with some organizations deriving 40-60% of house capacity from subscriber commitments. Arenas and stadiums offer season tickets but through different models focused on seat licensing, premium packages, and dynamic pricing rather than arts-series subscriptions. Convention centres operate event registration rather than subscription ticketing.
