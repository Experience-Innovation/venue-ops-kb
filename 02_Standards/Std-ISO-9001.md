# ISO 9001:2015 — Quality Management Systems

ISO 9001 provides the foundational quality management framework for venue service delivery organizations. It structures the QMS around seven principles and uses the ISO High Level Structure (HLS) 10-clause architecture shared with ISO 14001, 45001, and other management system standards.

## Venue Applicability

Every core venue function — booking, operations, event execution, client services, food and beverage, ticketing — constitutes a "process" that ISO 9001 Clause 8 (Operation) requires to be planned, controlled, and evaluated. The standard's risk-based thinking requirements align with the operational risk management inherent in public assembly venue management.

## Relationship to Baldrige and EFQM

ISO 9001 and Baldrige are complementary, not competing. NIST explicitly positions them as such: ISO 9001 is process-focused and compliance-based with auditable "shall" statements; Baldrige is strategy-focused and results-driven, assessing performance across leadership, strategy, customers, workforce, operations, and results ([[Source-0448-NIST-Baldrige-ISO-Complementary]]). ISO 9001 creates the operational foundation; Baldrige drives the strategic and cultural layer above it.

## Certification and Cost

Stage 1 (documentation readiness) followed by Stage 2 (implementation audit). System must be operational with records for minimum 3 months before Stage 2. Annual surveillance audits precede full recertification at end of 3-year cycle. Mid-size venue costs: CAD $7,000-$13,000 initial certification; CAD $15,000-$40,000 over the 3-year cycle. Implementation timeline: 9-12 months for mid-size organizations.
