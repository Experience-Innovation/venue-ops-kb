# Sanders et al. 1986 — Mass Gathering Medical Care Analysis

This 1986 paper by Sanders AB et al., published in the Annals of Emergency Medicine (15(5):515-519, PMID 3963529), is the foundational reference for mass gathering medical staffing standards in the United States. The study surveyed 15 Arizona facilities providing public events and established the response-time benchmarks that remain widely cited: Basic Life Support (BLS) first aid within 4 minutes, Advanced Life Support (ALS) within 8 minutes, and transport to a medical facility within 30 minutes.

The paper also contributed to the staffing ratio framework frequently attributed to "Sanders et al." in subsequent literature, including the recommendation of approximately 12 physicians per 50,000 attendees. This study is cited by the NIH StatPearls chapter on EMS Mass Gatherings and the NAEMSP Position Statement as the foundational US survey establishing baseline patient presentation rate ranges. Note: some subsequent publications misspell the lead author's name as "Saunders" rather than the correct "Sanders."
