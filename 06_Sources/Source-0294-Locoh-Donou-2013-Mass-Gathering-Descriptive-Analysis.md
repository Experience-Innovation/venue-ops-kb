# Locoh-Donou et al. 2013 — Mass Gathering Descriptive Analysis

This 2013 study by Locoh-Donou S et al., published in the American Journal of Emergency Medicine (31(5):843-6, DOI: 10.1016/j.ajem.2013.01.016, PMID 23453125), is the origin of the widely cited 95.97% mild presentations statistic in mass gathering medicine. The study analyzed EMS records from October 24, 2009 to August 27, 2011 at a southeastern US university, covering 79 mass gathering events with 670 patient presentations and cumulative attendance of 917,307 persons.

Severity categories were classified as mild (non-life-threatening, no transport required), moderate (transport required), and severe (life-threatening with transport). The key finding was that 95.97% of cases were mild, 27 cases required hospital transport, and 3 were life-threatening. This statistic is cited by the NIH StatPearls chapter on EMS Mass Gatherings as the benchmark for severity distribution in mass gathering medical planning.
