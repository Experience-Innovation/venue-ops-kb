# SALT Mass Casualty Triage Endorsement Paper (2008)

This 2008 publication in Disaster Medicine and Public Health Preparedness (2(4):245-6, DOI: 10.1097/DMP.0b013e31818d191e, PMID 19050431) documents the SALT (Sort, Assess, Lifesaving Interventions, Treatment/Transport) triage system developed by a CDC-sponsored expert working group. SALT was endorsed by the American College of Emergency Physicians (ACEP), ACS Committee on Trauma, American Trauma Society, National Association of EMS Physicians (NAEMSP), National Disaster Life Support Education Consortium (NDLSEC), and State and Territorial Injury Prevention Directors Association (STIPDA).

SALT uses five triage categories (Dead, Immediate, Delayed, Minimal, Expectant), differentiating it from START's four categories by separating Expectant from Dead and Minimal from Delayed. A key innovation is allowing lifesaving interventions (hemorrhage control, airway management, chest decompression) before category assignment. SALT is compliant with the Model Uniform Core Criteria (MUCC), the proposed US national standard for mass casualty triage, and covers all ages and all hazards.
