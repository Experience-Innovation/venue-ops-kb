# Alberta OHS Code Part 7 — Emergency Preparedness and Response

This is the primary legislative source for Alberta's emergency preparedness requirements for workplaces, including assembly venues. Part 7 of the Occupational Health and Safety Code contains Sections 115-118, which together form the most prescriptive provincial emergency response planning framework in Canada.

Section 115 requires employers to establish an ERP for any emergency requiring rescue or evacuation, with mandatory worker involvement in the planning process. Section 116 specifies a 10-element mandatory content list: identification of potential emergencies, procedures for identified emergencies, location and operation of emergency equipment and PPE, training requirements, location and use of emergency facilities, fire protection requirements, alarm and emergency communication requirements, first aid services, procedures for rescue and evacuation, and designated rescue and evacuation workers. Section 117 requires training including exercises simulating potential emergencies. Critically, Part 7 does not specify a minimum occupant threshold — the trigger is any emergency "requiring rescue or evacuation," applying to all public assembly occupancies.
