# NFPA 101 Fire Watch Trigger Thresholds — Sections 9.6.1.6 and 9.7.6.1

This document from Hopewell Township's Fire Prevention Bureau cites the specific NFPA 101 Life Safety Code sections governing fire watch requirements. Section 9.6.1.6 requires fire watch when a fire alarm system is out of service for more than 4 cumulative hours in a 24-hour period, with notification to the AHJ required. Section 9.7.6.1 applies the same 4-hour cumulative threshold to automatic sprinkler system impairments.

The document also provides the NFPA 101 fire watch definition from Section 3.3.91: "The assignment of a person or persons to an area for the express purpose of notifying the fire department, the building occupants, or both of an emergency; preventing a fire from occurring; extinguishing small fires; or protecting the public from fire or life safety dangers." The cumulative nature of the 4-hour threshold is a critical operational detail — the obligation triggers when the sum of all outages within a 24-hour period exceeds 4 hours, not when a single continuous outage reaches that duration.
