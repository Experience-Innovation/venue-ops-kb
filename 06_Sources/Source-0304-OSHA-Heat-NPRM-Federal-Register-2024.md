# OSHA Heat Injury and Illness Prevention NPRM (29 CFR 1910.148)

This Federal Register entry documents OSHA's Notice of Proposed Rulemaking for heat injury and illness prevention in outdoor and indoor work settings, published August 30, 2024 (89 FR [2024-14824]). The proposed rule would establish two trigger thresholds: an initial heat trigger at a heat index of 80F or higher (aligned with the NIOSH Recommended Alert Limit), and a high heat trigger at 90F or higher (aligned with the NIOSH Recommended Exposure Limit).

As of April 2026, the rule is currently suspended under a regulatory freeze imposed by Presidential Memorandum on January 20, 2025 ("Regulatory Freeze Pending Review"). The rule has not been formally withdrawn but has not been finalized. OSHA currently enforces heat safety through the General Duty Clause (Section 5(a)(1) of the OSH Act), which requires employers to provide a workplace free from recognized hazards. Venue operators should monitor the regulatory status of this proposed rule while maintaining General Duty Clause compliance.
