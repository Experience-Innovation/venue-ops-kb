# NWS Lightning Safety Toolkit for Outdoor Venues

This official National Weather Service PDF is the primary government-issued guidance document for lightning safety at outdoor venues. The toolkit establishes two operationally distinct resumption criteria rather than a three-radius system. With a professional meteorologist and real-time lightning data, activities may resume when lightning has moved beyond 8 miles from the venue and the threat of new lightning within 8 miles has ended. Without a professional meteorologist or real-time data, venues must wait 30 minutes after the last observed thunder or lightning before resuming activities.

The toolkit also documents the NWS StormReady Recognition Program for outdoor venues, a voluntary program requiring information reception systems, decision support protocols, public notification methods, and patron protection programs. The toolkit notes that "a significant lightning threat extends outward from the base of a thunderstorm cloud about 6 to 10 miles" and that organized events should account for the time required to evacuate all patrons to safe shelter.
