# FEMA P-361 — Safe Rooms for Tornadoes and Hurricanes (3rd Edition)

FEMA P-361 (3rd Edition, 2015), titled "Safe Rooms for Tornadoes and Hurricanes: Guidance for Community and Residential Safe Rooms," provides guidelines for designing, constructing, and operating safe rooms. As a guideline document, it uses "should/recommended" language and is not an enforceable standard — it becomes mandatory only when FEMA grant funds are used to construct the safe room.

Design criteria include a 250 mph design wind speed (3-second gust at 33 feet above ground in open terrain) and missile impact resistance of a 15-pound 2x4 wood stud at 100 mph horizontal for vertical surfaces and 67 mph for horizontal surfaces. FEMA P-361 requires full compliance with ICC 500 plus additional siting and fire-resistance requirements, and provides "near-absolute" life-safety protection. This distinguishes it from ICC 500, which provides "life safety" protection with wind speed requirements ranging from 130 to 250 mph depending on location within the tornado wind design zone.
