# ICC 500-2020 — Standard for Storm Shelter Design and Construction

This Structure Magazine article analyzes the structural changes in the 2020 edition of ICC 500 (ICC/NSSA Standard for the Design and Construction of Storm Shelters), the ANSI-approved enforceable consensus standard referenced in the 2021 International Building Code (IBC) and 2021 International Existing Building Code (IEBC). Unlike FEMA P-361's guideline approach, ICC 500 uses "shall/required" language.

Critically for venue operators, IBC Section 423 mandates ICC 500-compliant storm shelters for Group E occupancies (K-12 schools with occupant load of 50 or more), 911 call stations, emergency operations centers, and fire/rescue/police stations within the 250-mph tornado design wind speed zone. However, Group A assembly occupancies (arenas, stadiums, concert venues) are NOT currently required to construct storm shelters under IBC Section 423. ICC 500-2020 Section 108 requires the owner to submit a written statement of responsibility and emergency preparedness plan to the AHJ during the permit process, and Section 113 requires annual evaluations of the storm shelter envelope.
