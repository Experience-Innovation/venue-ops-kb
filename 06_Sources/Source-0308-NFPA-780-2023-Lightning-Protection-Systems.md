# NFPA 780 (2023) — Lightning Protection Systems Installation

NFPA 780 (2023 Edition), Standard for the Installation of Lightning Protection Systems, provides requirements for air terminals (lightning rods), down conductors, grounding electrodes, bonding, and surge protection devices for structures including assembly venues. The standard's scope (Section 1.1.1) covers "ordinary structures" and "miscellaneous structures and special occupancies," categories that encompass arenas, amphitheatres, and grandstands.

The 2023 edition added requirements for lightning protection for additions to protected structures and revised definitions for surge protective devices. NFPA 780 uses the Rolling Sphere Method and Protection Angle Method to determine coverage zones and integrates with NFPA 70 (National Electrical Code) grounding requirements. NFPA 780 is not mandatory unless adopted by the local Authority Having Jurisdiction; it does not require lightning protection systems to be installed (that decision is risk-based), but where a system is installed, NFPA 780 governs design and installation.
