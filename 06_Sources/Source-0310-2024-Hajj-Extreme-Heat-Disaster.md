# 2024 Hajj Extreme Heat Disaster — Wikipedia / Human Rights Watch

This Human Rights Watch report documents the 2024 Hajj pilgrimage heat disaster (June 14-19, 2024) in Mecca, Saudi Arabia, where at least 1,301 people died due to extreme heat. Temperatures exceeded 50C (122F), with the hottest recorded temperature at the Grand Mosque reaching 51.8C. Sources for the death toll include the Saudi Ministry of Health, WHO situation reports, and Human Rights Watch field research.

This incident represents the largest documented mass gathering heat-related fatality event in recent history and serves as a critical case study for venue operators worldwide. While the Hajj context differs significantly from Western venue operations in scale, infrastructure, and regulatory environment, the incident underscores the lethal potential of extreme heat at mass gatherings and the critical importance of heat illness prevention protocols, cooling infrastructure, and medical resource planning.
