DR PROMPT R-08 — Tenant and Partner Relations in Multi-Use Venues

CONTEXT: The KB has zero concept notes in tenant-and-partner-relations — one of three empty domains blocking v1.0 release. This domain was added during the 26-domain taxonomy expansion (Session 9, Decision #30) because multi-tenant venue operations — managing NHL/NBA/concert/event tenant relationships, partner service providers, and shared infrastructure — represent distinct operational complexity not covered by other domains. The existing source corpus (290 notes) contains references to anchor tenants and promoters in other domain contexts (event-operations, premium-VIP) but no dedicated coverage of tenant relationship management.

RESEARCH OBJECTIVE: Produce comprehensive operational reference on tenant management, partner relations, license agreements, shared infrastructure governance, and multi-use scheduling in public assembly venues (arenas, stadiums, convention centres, performing arts centres, fairgrounds) as of April 2026.

PRIMARY RESEARCH QUESTIONS:

1. ANCHOR TENANT RELATIONSHIPS IN ARENAS AND STADIUMS: How are anchor tenant agreements structured for professional sports venues? License agreement vs. lease agreement — key distinctions and trend toward license models. Term lengths, renewal options, relocation clauses. Revenue sharing — which revenue streams does the tenant control vs. the venue operator (ticket surcharges, premium seating, F&B, parking, sponsorship, naming rights)? Operational responsibility splits — who manages what on game day vs. non-game day? Tenant improvement allowances and capital expenditure responsibilities. How do multi-tenant venues (NHL + NBA sharing an arena, NFL + MLS sharing a stadium) manage competing tenant priorities?

2. PROMOTER AND TOURING PRODUCTION RELATIONSHIPS: How do venue operators work with concert promoters and touring production companies? Flat rental vs. co-promotion vs. four-wall deals — terms, risk allocation, and revenue splits. Exclusive promotion agreements (Live Nation/AEG venue deals) vs. open booking policies. Advance and settlement processes. Production requirements and technical riders — who bears the cost? Relationship management when the promoter is also the venue operator (AEG Presents at AEG venues, Live Nation at Live Nation venues — vertical integration dynamics).

3. CONVENTION AND TRADE SHOW PARTNER ECOSYSTEM: How do convention centres manage relationships with show organizers, general service contractors (GSCs), and destination marketing organizations? Show organizer as client vs. partner — contractual models. GSC/official services contractor relationships (Freeman, GES, Shepard) — exclusive vs. preferred contractor models. Exhibitor services coordination — who the exhibitor interacts with and who's responsible. CVB/destination marketing organization partnership — lead generation, bid support, citywide coordination. Multi-year booking commitments and their operational implications.

4. THIRD-PARTY SERVICE PROVIDER MANAGEMENT: How do venues manage outsourced service providers as operational partners? F&B concessionaire relationships (Levy, Aramark, Delaware North, Sodexo Live, OVG Hospitality) — contract structures, performance guarantees, revenue splits. Security contractor management — integration with venue security leadership. Parking operator partnerships. Cleaning and facility maintenance contractors. Technology and connectivity providers (in-venue Wi-Fi, DAS operators). Performance metrics and contract compliance monitoring. Transition management when changing service providers.

5. SHARED INFRASTRUCTURE AND COST ALLOCATION: How do multi-tenant venues allocate shared costs and manage common infrastructure? Common area maintenance (CAM) charges — calculation methods and dispute resolution. Capital improvement decision-making when multiple tenants benefit. Technology infrastructure sharing (scoreboard, ribbon boards, Wi-Fi, POS systems) — who owns, who maintains, who upgrades? Locker room and back-of-house space allocation in multi-sport venues. Loading dock and staging area scheduling across tenants.

6. PARTNERSHIP GOVERNANCE AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION: What governance structures manage multi-stakeholder venue operations? Joint operating committees and their authority. Regular business reviews — frequency, metrics, escalation paths. Dispute resolution mechanisms (mediation, arbitration, operational escalation). Performance benchmarking against contract SLAs. Brand standards enforcement when multiple parties operate under one venue brand. How do public-sector venue owners (cities, counties, authorities) manage private operator and tenant relationships?

7. EMERGING PARTNERSHIP MODELS: How is the tenant/partner landscape evolving? Vertical integration trend (Live Nation, AEG, Oak View Group owning venues + promoting + ticketing). Public-private partnerships (P3) for new venue development and operations. Community benefit agreement integration into tenant relationships. ESG/sustainability commitments shared across tenants and operators. Technology partnerships replacing traditional vendor relationships (data-sharing agreements, platform partnerships).

SOURCE QUALITY:
- PRIORITY 1: IAVM publications — venue management textbook chapters on tenant relations, VenueConnect conference proceedings, Facility Manager magazine
- PRIORITY 2: Industry operator documentation — ASM Global, Oak View Group, AEG Facilities, Live Nation/House of Blues operational frameworks (where publicly available)
- PRIORITY 3: Convention industry — PCMA, IAEE (International Association of Exhibitions and Events), UFI (Union des Foires Internationales), CEIR
- PRIORITY 4: Legal and business frameworks — venue lease/license agreement analyses (law firm publications, real estate journals), municipal auditor reports on public venue operations
- AVOID: Real estate investment analysis focused on venue valuation rather than operations. Team relocation speculation. Single-venue marketing materials without operational content.

OUTPUT STRUCTURE:
- Anchor tenant agreement framework (structure, revenue splits, operational responsibilities by venue type)
- Promoter relationship models (deal types, risk allocation, vertical integration dynamics)
- Convention centre partner ecosystem map (show organizer, GSC, CVB, exhibitor service relationships)
- Third-party service provider management guide (contract structures, performance metrics, transition protocols)
- Shared infrastructure cost allocation model (CAM, capital improvements, technology)
- Partnership governance framework (committees, reviews, dispute resolution, SLAs)
- Emerging partnership model radar (vertical integration, P3, ESG integration)

KNOWN CONTEXT: The KB already covers: event-operations (12 concepts — changeovers, show management, production coordination), commercial-and-revenue (2 concepts — community funding, sponsorship), premium-and-vip (6 concepts — hospitality delivery, suite operations), F&B operations (13 concepts — kitchen, concessions, compliance), strategy-and-governance (8 concepts — organizational models, ESG, AI adoption). This prompt targets ONLY the relationship/contract/governance layer between the venue operator and its tenants, promoters, and service partners — not event execution itself, not F&B service delivery, not premium hospitality operations.

CONSTRAINTS: North American focus (US + Canada) with international examples for global operators (ASM Global, AEG). Include all major venue types — the tenant/partner dynamics differ significantly between arenas (sports anchor tenants), convention centres (show organizer clients), performing arts centres (resident companies), and fairgrounds (seasonal exhibition operators). Any Calgary Stampede content tagged as exemplar only per KB policy. Distinguish between publicly-owned venues with private operators (most convention centres, many arenas) and privately-owned venues (AEG, Oak View Group properties) — the governance dynamics are fundamentally different.
