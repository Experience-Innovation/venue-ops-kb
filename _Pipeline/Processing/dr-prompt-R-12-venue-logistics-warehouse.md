DR PROMPT R-12 — Venue Logistics and Warehouse Operations

CONTEXT: The logistics-and-warehouse domain is the smallest in the KB — 3 concept notes (Changeover-Logistics, Dock-Scheduling-Freight-Management, Equipment-Inventory-Tracking), 2 at medium confidence. All three concepts originate from a single research batch (v2-prompt-08-fb-supply), which was primarily focused on food and beverage operations; logistics coverage was incidental, not targeted. No cross-batch corroboration exists. This domain is operationally critical for every venue type — arenas flip between hockey and concerts in hours, convention centres turn exhibit halls between shows, stadiums manage freight for touring productions. The gap between the domain's operational weight and its KB representation is the largest in the vault.

RESEARCH OBJECTIVE: Produce comprehensive operational reference on venue logistics and warehouse operations — loading dock management, equipment lifecycle tracking, changeover planning and execution, freight coordination, warehouse safety and compliance, inventory management systems, and supply chain logistics specific to public assembly venues (convention centres, arenas, stadiums, performing arts centres, fairgrounds, amphitheatres) as of April 2026.

PRIMARY RESEARCH QUESTIONS:

1. LOADING DOCK OPERATIONS AND MANAGEMENT: How do public assembly venues manage loading dock operations? Dock scheduling systems — how venues prioritize competing demands (exhibitor freight, F&B deliveries, production equipment, waste removal). Security protocols at loading docks — credential verification, vehicle screening, TWIC or equivalent requirements for venues near restricted zones. Multi-tenant dock coordination in convention centres with concurrent events. Dock design standards — turning radii, dock leveler specifications, covered vs. uncovered loading areas. Time-slot management during high-volume periods (move-in/move-out days at convention centres, concert load-in at arenas). Integration of dock scheduling with event management and facility booking systems. How do mega-venues (500K+ sq ft) handle 50+ dock appointments per day?

2. EVENT CHANGEOVER LOGISTICS: What are the operational frameworks for venue changeovers (flips/turns)? Arena changeover scenarios — ice-to-concert, concert-to-basketball, basketball-to-concert same-day turns. Convention centre room turns — breakout reconfiguration, exhibit hall transformations, ballroom resets. Stadium changeover for multi-use facilities (football-to-concert, concert-to-soccer). Changeover staffing models — in-house conversion crews vs. contracted labor, IATSE jurisdiction on production changeovers. Changeover time standards by venue type and complexity. Technology supporting changeover planning — CAD floor plans, changeover management software, digital checklists. Critical path analysis for complex multi-stage changeovers. Equipment staging and pre-positioning strategies to minimize changeover time.

3. EQUIPMENT INVENTORY AND LIFECYCLE MANAGEMENT: How do venues track and manage physical equipment assets? RFID and barcode tracking systems for venue equipment (tables, chairs, staging, pipe-and-drape, AV gear). Preventive maintenance scheduling for high-value assets (ice resurfacers, forklifts, scissor lifts, floor scrubbers). Depreciation tracking and capital replacement planning for venue equipment fleets. Equipment checkout/return workflows for internal departments and external rentals. Asset condition monitoring and inspection protocols. Integration with financial systems for asset valuation and insurance purposes. Par stock management — how venues determine minimum inventory levels for tables, chairs, linens, and other event supplies.

4. WAREHOUSE OPERATIONS IN VENUES: How do venues manage on-site and off-site warehouse and storage facilities? Storage allocation strategies — dedicated vs. shared storage by department, tenant storage areas, long-term vs. event-specific storage. Climate-controlled storage requirements (F&B dry goods, chemical storage for cleaning supplies, wine and spirits). Hazardous materials handling and storage in venue warehouses — OSHA Hazard Communication Standard, SDS requirements, secondary containment. Inventory management systems (WMS) adapted for venue operations vs. traditional warehouse WMS. Off-site warehouse coordination — shuttle logistics, advance shipment staging. Space optimization in constrained venue environments — vertical storage, mobile shelving, seasonal storage rotation. Security and access control for warehouse areas.

5. FREIGHT AND SHIPPING COORDINATION FOR EVENTS: How do venues coordinate freight logistics for different event types? Exhibitor freight management at convention centres — advance warehouse, show-site receiving, exhibitor services, empty container handling. Touring production equipment logistics — truck counts, load-in sequences, rigging advance, backline and band gear. Drayage operations — freeman, GES, and other general service contractors. Customs and cross-border freight for international events and touring shows. Freight insurance and liability — who bears risk at each handoff point (carrier, venue, exhibitor, decorator). Small package and FedEx/UPS receiving for events — chain of custody, delivery notification. How do venues handle freight for events with 500+ exhibitors and 1,000+ crates?

6. VENUE-SPECIFIC SAFETY AND COMPLIANCE FOR LOGISTICS OPERATIONS: What OSHA and equivalent safety requirements apply to venue logistics operations? Forklift and powered industrial truck operation — OSHA 29 CFR 1910.178 compliance, operator certification, pedestrian safety in mixed-use areas where public may be present. Fall protection for warehouse and dock operations. Ergonomic standards for repetitive lifting (event setup, changeover crews). Lock-out/tag-out procedures for venue equipment maintenance. Fire safety in venue storage areas — NFPA 1 and local fire code requirements for storage occupancies. Canadian equivalents — provincial OHSA requirements for warehouse operations in venues. Personal protective equipment requirements by task category. Incident reporting and investigation for logistics operations.

7. LOGISTICS TECHNOLOGY PLATFORMS: What technology platforms support venue logistics operations? Warehouse Management Systems (WMS) — features needed for venue operations vs. traditional distribution. Dock scheduling software — platforms used in the venue industry (Dock Scheduler, C3 Solutions, iDock). Fleet management for venue vehicle fleets (golf carts, forklifts, utility vehicles). Changeover management tools — digital floor plans, crew dispatch, progress tracking. RFID and IoT applications in venue asset tracking. Integration requirements — how logistics technology connects to event management (Momentus/Ungerboeck), financial systems, and building automation. Mobile applications for logistics crew coordination during events.
