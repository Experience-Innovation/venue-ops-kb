DR PROMPT R-13 — Venue Financial Management and Fiscal Operations

CONTEXT: The KB has 4 financial-operations concepts (Budgeting-Forecasting, Public-Venue-Financial-Governance, Revenue-Management-Optimization, Internal-Audit-Compliance), all originating from a single research batch (v2-prompt-09-financial). Senior venue operators — VPs of Operations, General Managers, CFOs — the target audience for this knowledge base — live in financial data daily. Event settlements, capital budgets, insurance renewals, and financial KPIs are their operational language. At 4 concepts, this domain's depth does not match its operational weight. The commercial-and-revenue domain (6 concepts) covers the revenue generation side; this prompt targets the financial management execution side — how venues plan, track, report, insure, and account for money.

RESEARCH OBJECTIVE: Produce comprehensive operational reference on venue financial management — budgeting cycles, capital planning, financial reporting for public and private venues, event settlement processes, insurance and risk financing, financial KPIs, and audit compliance specific to public assembly venue operations (convention centres, arenas, stadiums, performing arts centres, fairgrounds, amphitheatres) as of April 2026.

PRIMARY RESEARCH QUESTIONS:

1. VENUE FINANCIAL PLANNING CYCLES: What are the standard financial planning and budgeting cycles for public assembly venues? Annual operating budget development — timeline, departmental input process, revenue forecasting methodologies, expense categories specific to venues. Quarterly forecast updates and variance analysis practices. Capital budget planning — how venues separate operating expenditures from capital investments, capitalization thresholds, multi-year capital plans. Reserve fund management — facility renewal reserves, equipment replacement reserves, operating contingency reserves. How do financial planning cycles differ between publicly owned venues (authority/municipal), privately operated venues, and public-private partnership structures? What financial planning software and tools are venues using (Sage Intacct, Oracle NetSuite, Momentus financial modules, Excel-based models)?

2. EVENT SETTLEMENT AND RECONCILIATION: How do venues execute financial settlement with event promoters, exhibitors, and tenants? Promoter settlement structures — flat rental, percentage of gross, co-promotion splits, hybrid models. Settlement timelines — when does money change hands, what triggers final settlement, what are industry-standard payment terms. F&B settlement — how ancillary revenue from food, beverage, and catering is reconciled between venue, concessionaire, and event organizer. Ancillary revenue allocation — parking, merchandise, ATM fees, Wi-Fi charges, sponsorship activation fees. Box office settlement and ticket revenue reconciliation. Exhibitor services settlement at convention centres — how general service contractor charges, utilities, and exhibitor services fees are collected and distributed. Dispute resolution for settlement disagreements. What systems support settlement automation (ticketing system integration, POS reconciliation, ERP event modules)?

3. PUBLIC VENUE FINANCIAL GOVERNANCE: How do publicly owned venues manage financial governance and accountability? Municipal authority oversight structures — board reporting, public disclosure requirements, freedom of information compliance. Government Accounting Standards Board (GASB) requirements for venue authorities — GASB 34 (financial reporting), GASB 87 (leases), GASB 96 (IT subscriptions). Bond financing — revenue bonds, general obligation bonds, tax increment financing, debt service requirements. Taxpayer accountability and transparency — how public venues report financial performance to their communities. Annual audit requirements — independent auditor selection, audit committee structures, management letter responses. How do public venue financial officers balance commercial competitiveness with public accountability? Comparison of financial governance between convention centre authorities, arena authorities, and municipal recreation departments.

4. INSURANCE AND RISK FINANCING: What insurance and risk financing structures do public assembly venues require? Commercial general liability (CGL) coverage — limits, deductibles, additional insured requirements for events. Property insurance for large venues — replacement cost vs. actual cash value, business interruption coverage, flood and earthquake sublimits. Event cancellation insurance — who purchases (venue vs. promoter), coverage triggers, communicable disease exclusions post-COVID. Workers compensation in high-risk venue environments — experience modification rates, return-to-work programs. Directors and officers (D&O) liability for venue authority boards. Cyber liability insurance for venues handling payment card data and personal information. Umbrella and excess liability structures. Self-insurance and captive insurance programs for large venue portfolios. Risk transfer through contracts — hold harmless clauses, indemnification requirements, minimum insurance requirements for vendors and contractors. How has the venue insurance market changed post-pandemic (premium increases, exclusion modifications, capacity constraints)?

5. FINANCIAL KPIs BY VENUE TYPE: What financial key performance indicators do venue operators use to measure and benchmark performance? Revenue metrics — revenue per seat, revenue per square foot, revenue per available event day, total revenue per attendee, ancillary revenue per cap. Cost metrics — cost per event day, cost per attendee, overhead cost ratio, F&B cost of goods sold percentage, energy cost per square foot, labor cost as percentage of revenue. Profitability metrics — operating margin by event type, contribution margin by department, EBITDA for private venues, net position change for public authorities. Utilization metrics — event days per year, occupancy rate, average attendance as percentage of capacity, dark day ratio. Benchmarking sources — IAVM Venue Management School financial curriculum, convention centre benchmarking studies (CEIR, Destination International), arena financial benchmarks. How do KPIs differ between convention centres (space utilization, delegate spend), arenas (per-cap revenue, event count), and performing arts centres (subscription revenue, contributed revenue ratio)?

6. CAPITAL PROJECT PLANNING AND FINANCING: How do venues plan and finance major capital projects? Capital needs assessment methodologies — facility condition assessments, remaining useful life analysis, deferred maintenance quantification. Financing structures — revenue bonds, general obligation bonds, certificates of participation, public-private partnerships, naming rights revenue allocation to debt service. Renovation vs. replacement decision frameworks. Project delivery methods from a financial perspective — design-bid-build, design-build, CM-at-risk, and their financial risk profiles. Construction contingency and change order management. Federal and state/provincial funding sources — economic development grants, tourism development taxes, hotel/motel taxes dedicated to venue facilities. Privately financed venue developments — ownership structures, return expectations, lease-back arrangements. How do venues manage financial risk on $100M+ capital projects?

7. TAX CONSIDERATIONS FOR VENUE OPERATIONS: What tax considerations are specific to public assembly venue operations? Sales tax on admissions and ticket sales — state-by-state variation, exemptions for non-profit presenters. Entertainment and amusement taxes — municipal surcharges on event tickets. Property tax status — exemptions for publicly owned venues, PILOT (payment in lieu of taxes) agreements for private venues on public land. Tax-exempt bond financing — IRS private activity bond limitations, rebate requirements, post-issuance compliance. Canadian GST/HST and PST application to venue services and admissions. F&B tax treatment — prepared food taxation, liquor tax, catering exemptions. Venue operator tax reporting requirements — 1099 reporting for contractors and performers, withholding requirements for non-resident entertainers (state-level and IRS). How do tax considerations influence venue ownership structure decisions (public authority vs. private operator vs. non-profit)?

SOURCE QUALITY:
- PRIORITY 1: Government Accounting Standards Board (GASB) — pronouncements applicable to public venue authorities; Government Finance Officers Association (GFOA) — best practices, distinguished budget awards, financial reporting standards; IRS guidance on tax-exempt bonds and venue-related tax treatment
- PRIORITY 2: IAVM financial management publications — Venue Management School curriculum, professional development materials, industry benchmark reports; Destination International (formerly DMAI) — convention centre performance benchmarks, economic impact methodology
- PRIORITY 3: Professional services firm industry reports — PwC, Deloitte, EY, KPMG venue and entertainment industry publications with verifiable data; municipal bond rating agency reports (Moody's, S&P, Fitch) on venue authority creditworthiness; Risk Management Society (RIMS) and insurance industry publications on venue coverage
- PRIORITY 4: Trade press — VenuesNow, Pollstar (financial coverage), Sports Business Journal (venue finance), Convention Center Business (facility finance), Facilities & Event Management; venue authority annual reports and comprehensive annual financial reports (CAFRs) available as public records
- AVOID: Financial advisory firm pitch materials without substantive content. Generic municipal finance content not specific to venue operations. Insurance broker marketing materials. Investment banking tombstone announcements without deal structure details.

OUTPUT STRUCTURE:
- Venue financial planning cycle framework (annual budgets, capital plans, reserves, by ownership structure)
- Event settlement and reconciliation guide (promoter, F&B, exhibitor services, ancillary revenue, dispute resolution)
- Public venue financial governance reference (GASB requirements, board oversight, bond compliance, public disclosure)
- Insurance and risk financing matrix (coverage types, limits guidance, risk transfer mechanisms, post-pandemic market changes)
- Financial KPI dashboard framework (revenue, cost, profitability, utilization metrics by venue type with benchmarking sources)
- Capital project financing guide (funding sources, delivery methods, risk management, federal/state/provincial programs)
- Venue tax compliance reference (admissions tax, property tax, tax-exempt bonds, cross-border considerations, reporting requirements)
