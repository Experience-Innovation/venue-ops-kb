DR PROMPT R-15 — Venue Supply Chain and Procurement Operations

CONTEXT: The supply-chain-and-procurement domain has 4 concept notes (GPO-Procurement-Models, Sustainable-Procurement, Vendor-Management-Performance, Pouring-Rights-Beverage-Contracts). All originate from v2-prompt-08-fb-supply. The F&B supply chain is particularly thin given that F&B represents 30-40% of non-ticket venue revenue — the KB's supply chain coverage must match F&B's operational weight. GPO model coverage is introductory, lacking the structural detail venue procurement professionals need to evaluate and implement GPO strategies. Vendor management coverage describes performance monitoring but lacks the RFP process structure and SLA framework content that drives day-to-day procurement operations.

RESEARCH OBJECTIVE: Comprehensive research on venue supply chain and procurement operations — GPO models and contract structures, vendor management platforms, F&B supply chain resilience, pouring rights negotiation, sustainable procurement mandates, and procurement technology as of April 2026.

PRIMARY RESEARCH QUESTIONS:

1. GPO (GROUP PURCHASING ORGANIZATION) MODELS FOR VENUES: How do Group Purchasing Organizations operate in the venue and hospitality sector?
   - Aramark, Levy Restaurants, and Centerplate/Sodexo Live! GPO structures — how do managed F&B operators leverage purchasing scale for their venue clients?
   - Independent venue GPO options — Foodbuy (Compass Group), Avendra (Aramark), Entegra (Sodexo) availability for non-managed venues
   - GPO contract structures — committed volume requirements, rebate mechanisms, pricing transparency, category coverage
   - When does GPO membership make financial sense vs. direct procurement?
   - Comparison of GPO savings by category (proteins, produce, dry goods, chemicals, paper goods, smallwares)
   - GPO limitations — product specification flexibility, local sourcing conflicts, menu creativity constraints

2. VENDOR MANAGEMENT AND PERFORMANCE MONITORING: What are the standard procurement processes for venue operations?
   - RFP (Request for Proposal) development and evaluation — standard RFP components for venue services (F&B supplies, janitorial, equipment, AV)
   - SLA (Service Level Agreement) structures — delivery windows, fill rates, product quality standards, substitution policies
   - Vendor scorecards — KPIs for vendor performance (on-time delivery rate, fill rate, quality rejection rate, price compliance, responsiveness)
   - Vendor diversification strategies — single-source risk mitigation, approved vendor lists, minority/women-owned business enterprise (MWBE) procurement targets
   - Contract lifecycle management — term lengths, renewal options, escalation clauses, termination provisions

3. F&B SUPPLY CHAIN RESILIENCE: How do venues manage F&B supply chain disruption and ensure continuity?
   - Cold chain management — temperature monitoring, HACCP integration in receiving, cold storage capacity planning for event peaks
   - Local sourcing mandates — farm-to-table venue programs, "buy local" percentage targets, seasonal menu adaptation to local supply
   - Supply disruption planning — substitute product protocols, dual-sourcing for critical items, strategic inventory buffers
   - Event-driven demand spikes — pre-event ordering cycles, par level adjustment for different event types, just-in-time delivery coordination
   - Product recall management — traceability requirements, lot tracking, recall notification procedures

4. POURING RIGHTS AND BEVERAGE CONTRACTS: How are exclusive beverage agreements structured in venue operations?
   - Coca-Cola vs. PepsiCo venue deals — typical term lengths (5-15 years), annual guarantee structures, capital investment (fountain equipment, signage, coolers)
   - Beer pouring rights — exclusive vs. semi-exclusive agreements, craft beer integration within exclusive contracts, local brewery carve-outs
   - Spirit pouring rights — well brand contracts, premium brand placement fees, brand activation opportunities
   - Financial structures — upfront rights fees, annual guarantees, volume-based incentives, marketing fund contributions
   - How do pouring rights intersect with F&B operator contracts when the venue uses a third-party concessionaire?
   - Renegotiation triggers and market-rate benchmarking

5. PROCUREMENT TECHNOLOGY PLATFORMS: What technology platforms support venue procurement operations?
   - Coupa — procurement-to-pay automation, contract management, spend analytics capabilities
   - SAP Ariba — supplier management, sourcing, procurement integration with ERP
   - Venue-specific procurement tools — how do venue management platforms (Momentus, VenueOps) handle procurement workflows?
   - POS-to-procurement integration — automated reordering based on sales data, inventory management system connections
   - E-procurement and marketplace platforms for venue supplies — catalog management, punch-out procurement, approval workflows
   - Spend analytics and category management tools — spend visibility, savings identification, contract compliance monitoring

6. SUSTAINABLE PROCUREMENT MANDATES: What sustainability requirements affect venue procurement decisions?
   - Plastic reduction mandates — single-use plastic bans by jurisdiction, compostable packaging requirements, reusable cup programs
   - Compostable and recyclable packaging requirements — BPI certification, ASTM D6400 standards, compostable vs. "biodegradable" distinction
   - Supply chain sustainability reporting — Scope 3 emissions from procurement, sustainability scorecards for vendors, ESG reporting
   - Sustainable seafood and protein sourcing — MSC certification, cage-free/free-range mandates, plant-based menu integration
   - Green cleaning and chemical procurement — Green Seal certification, EPA Safer Choice, LEED-aligned purchasing requirements
   - Venue-specific sustainability certifications that mandate procurement standards (LEED Operations, Green Venue Program, ISO 20121)

7. COST CONTROL AND WASTE REDUCTION: How do venues optimize procurement costs and minimize waste?
   - Par level management — establishing and maintaining par levels by event type, seasonal adjustment, data-driven par optimization
   - Just-in-time delivery for events — coordinating supplier deliveries with event schedules, dock scheduling for multi-event days
   - Waste tracking systems — food waste measurement technology (Leanpath, Winnow), waste-to-cost analysis, waste reduction targets
   - Inventory management — perpetual vs. periodic inventory, variance analysis, shrinkage control
   - Cost-per-serve analysis — benchmarking food and beverage cost-per-serve against industry standards, cost allocation by event type
   - Rebate and incentive optimization — tracking and capturing earned rebates, volume incentive management, co-marketing fund utilization
