DR PROMPT R-16 — Guest Experience Design for Public Assembly Venues

CONTEXT: The guest-experience domain has 5 concept notes (Guest-Journey-Mapping, Service-Recovery-Frameworks, Digital-Wayfinding-Systems, Guest-Feedback-Analytics, Concierge-Guest-Services). Guest experience is a core outcome domain in the VEF — every venue professional in the KB's target audience of 35,000-50,000 mid-to-senior practitioners cares about guest experience. The current 5-concept depth does not match the domain's strategic weight as a primary outcome measure. Service design methodology coverage is introductory, wayfinding lacks accessibility integration depth, and guest satisfaction benchmarking by venue type is absent. The domain needs both deeper treatment of existing concepts and expansion into experience personalization, queue psychology, and venue-type-specific experience design.

RESEARCH OBJECTIVE: Comprehensive research on venue guest experience design — journey mapping methodologies, service design frameworks, wayfinding systems, accessibility integration, guest feedback technology, satisfaction benchmarking, and experience personalization in public assembly venues as of April 2026.

PRIMARY RESEARCH QUESTIONS:

1. GUEST JOURNEY MAPPING FOR VENUE OPERATIONS: How do venue operators map and optimize the full guest journey?
   - Pre-event touchpoints — ticket purchase experience, pre-event communications, parking/transit planning, mobile app onboarding
   - Arrival touchpoints — parking/drop-off experience, exterior wayfinding, entry and security screening, credential scanning
   - In-venue touchpoints — seat finding, concessions, restrooms, merchandise, premium experiences, in-seat service
   - Departure touchpoints — egress management, transportation coordination, post-event wayfinding
   - Post-event touchpoints — feedback solicitation, loyalty program engagement, re-marketing
   - Journey mapping methodologies — service blueprinting, customer effort scoring, emotion mapping
   - How do journey maps differ by venue type (convention centre multi-day vs. arena single-event vs. performing arts season)?

2. SERVICE DESIGN FRAMEWORKS APPLIED TO VENUES: What service design models have been adopted or adapted by venue operators?
   - Disney's service model — the "Guest Experience" philosophy, "on stage/off stage" concept, SCSE priority framework (Safety, Courtesy, Show, Efficiency)
   - Ritz-Carlton Gold Standards — the Credo, Three Steps of Service, "Ladies and Gentlemen" service culture, applicability to premium venue operations
   - IAVM service excellence programs — VenueConnect programming, Certified Venue Professional curriculum's guest experience components
   - Service profit chain model (Heskett, Sasser, Schlesinger) — employee satisfaction driving guest satisfaction in venue operations
   - SERVQUAL framework — applying the five dimensions (tangibles, reliability, responsiveness, assurance, empathy) to venue service measurement

3. WAYFINDING AND SIGNAGE SYSTEMS: What are current best practices for venue wayfinding?
   - ADA compliance requirements — tactile signage, Braille, mounting height, font size, contrast ratios
   - AODA (Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act) signage requirements — how they differ from ADA
   - Digital wayfinding systems — interactive kiosks, mobile app wayfinding, augmented reality navigation, turn-by-turn indoor navigation
   - Multilingual signage — language selection methodology, pictographic universal design, cultural considerations
   - Sensory wayfinding — auditory cues, tactile ground surface indicators, scent-based wayfinding for visually impaired guests
   - Wayfinding design principles for large-scale venues — landmark-based navigation, progressive disclosure, decision point hierarchy
   - Emergency wayfinding integration — how routine wayfinding connects with emergency egress signage and systems

4. GUEST FEEDBACK AND SATISFACTION MEASUREMENT: What measurement frameworks and technologies do venues use?
   - Net Promoter Score (NPS) — application methodology in venue context, benchmarks by venue type
   - Customer Satisfaction Score (CSAT) — survey design for post-event measurement, in-venue intercept methodology
   - Real-time feedback technology — in-venue kiosks (HappyOrNot, Smiley Digital), SMS-based feedback, QR code triggers
   - Post-event survey design — timing, length, response rate optimization, question frameworks for actionable data
   - Social listening and sentiment analysis — monitoring social media, review platform management (Google, Yelp, TripAdvisor)
   - Mystery shopper programs — design for venue operations, scoring frameworks, frequency and coverage
   - Voice of the Customer (VoC) programs — integrating multiple feedback channels into unified guest experience intelligence

5. EXPERIENCE PERSONALIZATION TECHNOLOGY: How are venues personalizing the guest experience through technology?
   - Venue mobile apps — feature sets (mobile ordering, seat upgrades, wayfinding, loyalty), adoption rates, platform providers
   - Beacon and proximity technology — Bluetooth Low Energy beacons for location-aware messaging, push notification strategies, privacy
   - CRM-driven personalization — using purchase history, attendance patterns, and preferences to customize communications and offers
   - Facial recognition and biometric entry — operational implementations, privacy considerations, guest consent frameworks
   - Digital twin and IoT integration — using venue sensor data to optimize guest flow, climate, and service deployment in real time

6. QUEUE MANAGEMENT AND WAIT TIME PERCEPTION: How do venues manage queues and perceived wait times?
   - Virtual queuing systems — mobile-based virtual queues for concessions, merchandise, entry (Accesso, Qless, Waitwhile)
   - Wait time perception psychology — the Maister principles (unoccupied time feels longer, uncertain waits feel longer, unexplained waits feel longer)
   - Entertainment-in-line strategies — pre-show content, interactive displays, performance in queue areas
   - Queue design — single-line vs. multi-line, serpentine routing, express lanes, ADA-accessible queue lanes
   - Real-time wait time display — digital signage showing current wait times, impact on guest distribution across concession points
   - Mobile ordering as queue elimination — adoption rates, operational integration, impact on revenue per cap

7. FIRST-TIME VS. REPEAT VISITOR EXPERIENCE DESIGN: How should venues differentiate the experience?
   - First-visit orientation — digital and physical orientation tools, welcome programs, ambassador/greeter stations
   - Loyalty and membership programs — tiered loyalty structures, experiential rewards, subscription models
   - Season ticket holder experience management — dedicated entry, reserved parking, member lounges, exclusive events
   - Habit formation design — behavioral design principles for converting first-time attendees to repeat visitors
   - Churn prediction and intervention — using attendance data to identify at-risk patrons and trigger retention outreach

8. GUEST EXPERIENCE KPIs AND BENCHMARKING BY VENUE TYPE: What metrics do venues use to measure and benchmark?
   - Convention centre metrics — attendee satisfaction, exhibitor satisfaction, net promoter, rebooking intent
   - Arena/stadium metrics — fan experience score, return intent, spend per cap, dwell time
   - Performing arts metrics — subscriber retention, single-ticket conversion, interval experience satisfaction
   - Industry benchmarks — J.D. Power venue/event studies, IAVM member benchmarking data, Turnkey Intelligence fan experience surveys
   - Experience ROI measurement — correlating guest experience investment with revenue outcomes
   - Competitive benchmarking frameworks — how venues benchmark against peers in same market and venue type

SOURCE QUALITY:
- PRIORITY 1: IAVM venue management publications and VenueConnect conference proceedings, Cornell Hospitality Research (Center for Hospitality Research) — peer-reviewed hospitality experience research
- PRIORITY 2: Disney Institute publications — "Be Our Guest" and service model documentation, PCMA attendee experience research, J.D. Power venue and event satisfaction studies
- PRIORITY 3: Service design literature — Heskett/Sasser/Schlesinger service profit chain, Parasuraman/Zeithaml/Berry SERVQUAL, Don Norman design principles applied to venue environments
- PRIORITY 4: Technology vendor operational case studies with measurable outcomes, trade publications (VenuesNow, Sports Business Journal, Facilities & Event Management)
- AVOID: Venue marketing materials presenting aspirational guest experience without operational specifics. Technology vendor demos or feature lists without implementation data. Guest review compilations without analytical framework.

OUTPUT STRUCTURE:
- Guest journey mapping framework (touchpoints by phase, mapping methodologies, venue-type variations)
- Service design model comparison (Disney, Ritz-Carlton, SERVQUAL — principles, venue application, measurement)
- Wayfinding best practices guide (ADA/AODA compliance, digital systems, multilingual, sensory, emergency integration)
- Guest feedback technology landscape (NPS/CSAT frameworks, real-time kiosks, post-event surveys, social listening)
- Experience personalization technology catalog (mobile apps, beacons, CRM, biometrics — capabilities and privacy)
- Queue management and wait time psychology reference (virtual queuing, Maister principles, mobile ordering impact)
- Guest experience KPI framework by venue type (metrics, benchmarks, ROI measurement)
