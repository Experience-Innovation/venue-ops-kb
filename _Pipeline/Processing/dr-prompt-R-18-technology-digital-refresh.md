DR PROMPT R-18 — Venue Technology and Digital Infrastructure Refresh

CONTEXT: The technology-and-digital domain has 5 concept notes (Venue-Network-Infrastructure, IoT-Sensor-Networks, Digital-Twin-Technology, Cybersecurity-Physical-Security-Convergence, EV-Charging-Infrastructure). All were extracted from v2-prompt-05-tech-digital research conducted in early 2026. Technology moves fast — AI/ML applications in venue operations, WiFi 6E/7 venue deployments, 5G private networks, and autonomous systems have evolved materially since the original research. DELTA-019 flagged EV Charging Infrastructure specifically: the NEVI program details are unsourced. At 5 concepts this domain is at minimum viable coverage but well below working depth (8+), and the existing content is the most likely to age poorly of any domain in the KB.

RESEARCH OBJECTIVE: Updated research on venue technology and digital transformation — AI/ML applications in venue operations, network infrastructure evolution, IoT implementations, cybersecurity convergence, digital twin maturity, EV charging infrastructure (NEVI program), and emerging technologies specifically deployed in public assembly venues as of mid-2026.

PRIMARY RESEARCH QUESTIONS:

1. AI/ML APPLICATIONS IN VENUE OPERATIONS: What AI and machine learning applications are currently deployed in public assembly venues?
   - Predictive crowd analytics (computer vision-based occupancy and flow modeling)
   - Dynamic pricing engines for tickets and concessions
   - Chatbot and AI concierge systems for guest services
   - Computer vision for security screening and safety monitoring (weapon detection, distress detection)
   - AI-powered predictive maintenance scheduling for building systems
   - Named deployments at specific venue types where available; ROI evidence for venue AI implementations

2. NETWORK INFRASTRUCTURE EVOLUTION FOR VENUES: What is the current state of high-capacity wireless deployment in large public assembly venues?
   - WiFi 6E and WiFi 7 deployment status — Wi-Fi Alliance venue deployment guidance and case studies
   - 5G private network implementations — carrier partnerships (Verizon, T-Mobile, Rogers), CBRS spectrum, network slicing
   - DAS architecture for high-density venue environments
   - Bandwidth-per-attendee benchmarks for 2026-2027; convention centre vs. stadium vs. arena differences

3. IOT SENSOR DEPLOYMENTS IN VENUES: What IoT sensor technologies are venues deploying for operational intelligence?
   - Occupancy counting systems (LiDAR, thermal, vision-based) and accuracy benchmarks
   - Environmental monitoring (air quality, temperature, humidity, CO2) for HVAC optimization
   - Asset tracking (RTLS/UWB) for equipment, vehicles, and high-value inventory
   - Smart building integration platforms; standards governing IoT security (NIST IoT frameworks, UL)

4. CYBERSECURITY FOR VENUE OPERATIONS: How are venues addressing IT/OT convergence security?
   - Ransomware threats targeting venue operations — documented incidents, impact on event continuity
   - PCI DSS 4.0 compliance for ticketing and concession POS systems
   - SOC models for large venues — in-house vs. managed
   - CISA guidance for public assembly cybersecurity; frameworks adopted (NIST CSF 2.0, CIS Controls)

5. DIGITAL TWIN TECHNOLOGY MATURITY: What is the current maturity of digital twins for venue operations?
   - 3D venue modeling for event setup visualization and space planning
   - Simulation for crowd flow, emergency evacuation, and HVAC load prediction
   - BIM integration with CMMS for maintenance visualization
   - ROI evidence; which venue types are furthest along in adoption

6. EV CHARGING INFRASTRUCTURE FOR VENUES: Specifics of the NEVI Formula Program for venue parking.
   - FHWA NEVI documentation — funding mechanisms, eligible locations, technical standards (150kW DCFC minimum)
   - DOE AFDC station locator data for venue-adjacent charging installations
   - Venue-specific EV charging case studies (convention centres, stadiums, arenas)
   - Revenue models — free amenity vs. fee-based vs. third-party operator; Canadian ZEV Infrastructure Program

7. AUTONOMOUS AND ROBOTICS APPLICATIONS: What autonomous/robotic technologies are deployed in venue environments?
   - Cleaning robots (autonomous floor scrubbers, UV disinfection) — vendors, deployment scale
   - Delivery and service robots for concession or in-seat delivery
   - Autonomous shuttles for remote parking lot-to-venue transportation; drone surveillance/inspection
   - Liability and insurance considerations for autonomous systems in public assembly venues

8. DATA PRIVACY REGULATIONS AFFECTING VENUE TECHNOLOGY: What regulations impact venue tech deployments?
   - CCPA/CPRA requirements for guest data collected through WiFi, apps, loyalty programs
   - PIPEDA requirements for Canadian venues
   - Biometric consent laws (Illinois BIPA, Texas CUBI, Washington) affecting facial recognition
   - GDPR implications for international events; privacy-by-design frameworks venues are implementing

SOURCE QUALITY:
- PRIORITY 1: Government and standards bodies — FHWA NEVI program documentation, DOE Alternative Fuels Data Center, CISA cybersecurity guidance, NIST frameworks (CSF 2.0, IoT), FCC spectrum allocation
- PRIORITY 2: Industry standards and alliances — Wi-Fi Alliance venue deployment studies, PCI Security Standards Council (PCI DSS 4.0), UL standards for IoT, IEEE venue networking
- PRIORITY 3: Analyst and industry research — Gartner venue technology hype cycles, Forrester venue digital transformation reports, IAVM technology surveys, Venues Now, Sports Business Journal
- PRIORITY 4: Vendor-neutral case studies — university research (MIT Media Lab, Carnegie Mellon), conference proceedings (IEEE, ACM) on venue-scale IoT and networking
- AVOID: Vendor marketing without independent verification. Press releases without deployment evidence. "Future of venues" speculation without current data. Social media commentary.
