DR PROMPT R-19 — Parking and Transportation Source Enrichment

CONTEXT: The parking-and-transportation domain has 10 concept notes at working depth, but 4 carry medium confidence (Technology-Systems, Pre-Paid-Reserved, Self-Operated-Vs-Contracted, Transit-Multimodal) — the highest medium-confidence concentration of any domain at 40%. The A-06 confidence tier audit identified vendor-sourced concentration as the root cause: ParkMobile case study metrics serve as the sole evidence for parking technology claims, and the NPA (National Parking Association) rate study is the only source for operational model cost comparisons. DELTA-019 independently flagged two specific gaps: TNC/ride-share zone management references are unsourced, and MUTCD traffic management for special events lacks specific section citations. This domain does not need more concepts — it needs independent, non-vendor sources to corroborate existing claims and move medium-confidence concepts to high confidence.

RESEARCH OBJECTIVE: Source enrichment research on venue parking and transportation — independent (non-vendor) sources for parking technology, operational models, transit integration, traffic management, and ride-share coordination specific to public assembly venue operations. Priority on regulatory, academic, and association sources over vendor documentation.

PRIMARY RESEARCH QUESTIONS:

1. TNC/RIDE-SHARE ZONE MANAGEMENT AT VENUES: What standards and guidelines exist for TNC pickup/dropoff zone design at public assembly venues?
   - NACTO curb management guidelines — specific sections addressing event venue curbside operations
   - Municipal TNC regulations in cities with major venues (Nashville, Las Vegas, Toronto, Denver)
   - Venue-specific pickup/dropoff design standards — lane geometry, queue management, signage, lighting
   - Data on TNC trip volumes at major venue events (MLB, NFL, NHL, MLS game-day usage)

2. MUTCD TRAFFIC MANAGEMENT FOR SPECIAL EVENTS: What does the MUTCD specify for special event traffic management?
   - Chapter 6I (Traffic Incident Management Areas) application to planned special events
   - Part 6 Temporary Traffic Control provisions applicable to venue event days
   - State/provincial supplements with venue-specific requirements (California MUTCD, Ontario Traffic Manual Book 7)
   - TMP requirements — FHWA Planned Special Events guidance, permit requirements by jurisdiction
   - Training or certification required for traffic control personnel at venue events

3. PARKING OPERATIONAL MODELS — INDEPENDENT SOURCES: What non-NPA sources document cost comparison data for self-operated vs. contracted parking?
   - Municipal parking authority published rate studies (City of Chicago, City of Toronto, Metro Nashville)
   - University venue parking operations research — academic studies on management models
   - IPMI operational benchmarks — revenue per space, operating cost ratios, staffing models
   - GAO or state auditor reports on publicly-owned venue parking operations
   - Self-operated vs. contracted vs. P3 model comparison on net revenue, satisfaction, flexibility

4. PRE-PAID AND RESERVED PARKING PROGRAMS: What independent research exists on pre-paid/reserved parking for event venues?
   - Consumer behavior research — price sensitivity, booking lead times, no-show rates
   - Pricing elasticity studies (academic or government-sourced, not vendor white papers)
   - Mobile payment adoption rates from independent researchers (Pew Research, university transportation labs)
   - Pre-paid vs. drive-up percentages across venue types; operational impacts on ingress traffic flow

5. TRANSIT AND MULTIMODAL INTEGRATION FOR VENUES: What FTA guidance exists for transit to major event venues?
   - Transit-oriented development and transit access standards for public assembly facilities
   - League venue transit partnerships — published ridership data (Wrigley Field CTA, MetLife Stadium NJ Transit, Rogers Centre GO Transit)
   - Bicycle infrastructure — NACTO bikeway design standards, bike parking in venue building codes
   - Pedestrian access — ADA pathway requirements, wayfinding standards
   - Percentage of attendees arriving by transit at venues with dedicated transit service

6. AUTONOMOUS VEHICLE STAGING AND FUTURE-PROOFING: What DOT guidance exists for AV operations at venues?
   - USDOT AV guidance (AV 4.0+) — implications for venue curbside design
   - ITE curbside management frameworks addressing AV pickup/dropoff
   - AV readiness assessments for venue parking infrastructure — modifications needed
   - SAE J3016 automation levels and implications for venue valet, shuttle, and parking
   - Pilot programs for autonomous shuttles serving venue parking areas

7. PARKING SUSTAINABILITY: What sustainability practices are being implemented in venue parking?
   - EV charging in parking structures — cross-reference R-18; focus on parking-specific installation (electrical capacity, space allocation, accessibility)
   - Solar canopy installations — case studies, energy generation data, cost-benefit analyses
   - Stormwater management in surface lots — permeable pavement, bioswales, retention requirements
   - LEED and Parksmart certification for venue parking structures; IPMI and USGBC green design standards

SOURCE QUALITY:
- PRIORITY 1: Government regulatory sources — MUTCD official documentation (FHWA), FTA transit publications, USDOT AV guidance, state/provincial traffic manuals, municipal parking authority reports
- PRIORITY 2: Professional association standards — NACTO curb management and bikeway design guides, ITE event traffic studies and parking generation rates, IPMI operational benchmarks and certification
- PRIORITY 3: Academic transportation research — MIT Transit Lab, UC Davis Institute of Transportation Studies, Georgia Tech, university parking studies with peer-reviewed methodology
- PRIORITY 4: Industry data with methodology disclosure — sports league transportation reports with cited ridership data, municipal planning documents for venue districts, government audit reports
- AVOID: Parking technology vendor marketing (ParkMobile, SpotHero, ParkWhiz white papers). NPA-only-sourced claims where independent corroboration is the explicit goal. Vendor case studies presented as independent research. Press releases without verifiable data.

OUTPUT STRUCTURE:
- TNC/ride-share zone design standards reference (NACTO sections, municipal regulations, design parameters)
- MUTCD special event traffic control reference (specific sections, state supplements, TMP requirements)
- Parking operational model comparison (independent sources only — municipal data, IPMI, academic research)
- Pre-paid parking program evidence base (consumer research, pricing studies, adoption data — non-vendor)
- Transit integration catalog (FTA guidance, league-specific ridership data, modal split evidence)
- AV readiness framework reference (DOT guidance, ITE standards, pilot program inventory)
- Parking sustainability practices catalog (EV charging integration, solar canopy, stormwater, certifications)

KNOWN CONTEXT: The KB has 10 existing concept notes covering parking-and-transportation: Parking-Technology-Systems, Pre-Paid-Reserved-Parking, Self-Operated-Vs-Contracted, Transit-Multimodal-Integration, Traffic-Management-Event-Day, ADA-Parking-Compliance, Revenue-Control-Systems, Wayfinding-Signage-Parking, TNC-Ride-Share-Coordination, and Sustainable-Parking-Practices. This prompt does NOT seek to replicate existing concept coverage — it seeks independent, non-vendor sources to strengthen confidence ratings on medium-rated concepts. Any new concepts that emerge are welcome but are not the primary objective. The technology-and-digital domain (R-18) covers EV charging infrastructure broadly; this prompt addresses EV charging only as it relates to parking structure integration.

CONSTRAINTS: Explicitly prefer government, academic, and professional association sources over vendor documentation. The existing vendor-sourced claims (ParkMobile, NPA) are not presumed wrong — they need corroboration from independent sources to move from medium to high confidence. Every source found must be classifiable as non-vendor or, if vendor-adjacent (e.g., IPMI with vendor members), must have transparent methodology. North American focus (US and Canada) with attention to jurisdictional differences. MUTCD citations must include specific Part/Chapter/Section references. NACTO citations must identify the specific guide and edition. Expected yield: 2-5 new concepts (source-heavy by design), 20-30 source notes.
