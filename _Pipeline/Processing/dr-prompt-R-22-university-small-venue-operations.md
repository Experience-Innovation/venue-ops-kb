DR PROMPT R-22 — University Venue Operations and Small/Mid-Scale Venue Management

CONTEXT: The venue-ops-kb contains 207 concept notes across 26 domains, but the corpus is implicitly written from the perspective of large and mega venues — convention centres with 250K+ sq ft, arenas with 15K+ seats, stadiums with 30K+ capacity. The venue_scale cross-cutting dimension exists in the schema but is populated on only 19% of concept notes, meaning scale-dependent operational differences are rarely surfaced. The university-venue value exists in the venue_type vocabulary but appears in only 1 concept's venue_types list across the entire corpus. The people-and-culture domain (7 concepts) covers workforce management from a departmentalized organization perspective — dedicated HR departments, formal training programs, specialized recruitment. The facilities-and-building-systems domain (15 concepts) covers building operations from a large-facility perspective — central plant operations, BMS integration, dedicated maintenance teams. The event-operations domain (12 concepts) covers event execution with assumed dedicated event management staff. None of these domains address how venue operations work when the annual budget is $2M instead of $50M, when one person covers four functional areas, when the workforce is primarily students, or when the venue reports to both an athletic director and a VP of administration.

RESEARCH OBJECTIVE: Comprehensive research on university venue operations and small/mid-scale venue management — dual governance models (athletics + administration), NCAA compliance, student workforce dynamics, small venue staffing and budget models, scale-dependent operational differences, multi-venue campus management, community performing arts centre operations, and how venue management principles apply differently at small and mid-scale operations as of April 2026.

PRIMARY RESEARCH QUESTIONS:

1. UNIVERSITY VENUE GOVERNANCE: How are university-owned venues governed, and what makes their governance distinct?
   - Dual reporting structures — athletic director vs. VP administration/student affairs, which venue types report where, conflict resolution when priorities differ (athletic scheduling vs. academic events vs. community rental)
   - NCAA and conference facility requirements — Division I/II/III facility standards, conference-specific mandates (SEC, Big Ten, ACC facility investment requirements), Title IX facility equity obligations (equal-quality facilities for men's and women's programs), how these requirements drive capital investment
   - Tax-exempt operations — UBIT (Unrelated Business Income Tax) concerns for commercial events in tax-exempt university venues, IRS guidance on "substantially related" activities, revenue thresholds, how universities structure event rental programs to maintain tax-exempt status
   - Academic calendar constraints on scheduling — final exam blackout periods, graduation ceremony priority, summer conference season operations, residence hall move-in/move-out impact on venue parking/access
   - Board of regents/trustees oversight — how university governing boards interact with venue operations, approval thresholds for contracts, procurement rules specific to public universities (state procurement codes)

2. UNIVERSITY VENUE WORKFORCE: How does the student-worker-dependent staffing model affect venue operations?
   - Student workers — federal work-study program integration with venue operations, hourly limits, academic priority constraints, training challenges with high turnover (50-100% annual), scheduling around class schedules
   - Internship and practicum programs — academic credit for venue operations work (sport management, hospitality, event management programs), liability considerations, supervision ratios, learning outcome requirements vs. operational needs
   - Graduate assistant roles — GA positions in university venue management, stipend models, supervisory authority, how GAs bridge student and professional staff
   - Volunteer management for collegiate athletics — volunteer usher programs, booster club integration, NCAA rules on volunteer involvement in athletic events, liability and insurance for volunteers
   - Part-time and seasonal student staffing models — game-day surge staffing, how universities recruit and retain event staff when competing with off-campus employers, campus minimum wage vs. market rate, union considerations at public universities

3. SMALL VENUE OPERATIONS (< 5K CAPACITY): How do venue management principles apply when scale is constrained?
   - Multi-role staffing — how one person covers event management, facility maintenance, marketing, and bookkeeping, role design for 1-3 person venue teams, which functions get covered first and which suffer
   - Outsource vs. in-house decision framework at different budget levels — when does it make financial sense to outsource security, cleaning, F&B, AV, ticketing? Budget thresholds for each function, total cost of outsourcing vs. in-house at small scale
   - Technology decisions at constrained budgets — what a $5K/year vs. $25K/year vs. $100K/year technology budget buys in venue management capability, cloud-based tools for small venues (ticketing, CRM, building management), when spreadsheets are actually appropriate
   - Simplified versions of enterprise venue management systems — how small venues adapt EBMS, Momentus, VenueOps concepts to simpler tools, when a full VMS is justified vs. when calendar + spreadsheet works
   - Small venue financial sustainability — revenue diversification strategies for sub-5K venues, contributed revenue models (donations, grants, sponsorship at local scale), earned revenue optimization, break-even analysis frameworks

4. MID-SIZE VENUE CHALLENGES (5K-20K CAPACITY): What operational transitions occur as venues grow from small to departmentalized?
   - The departmentalization transition — when to add dedicated roles (security director, F&B manager, events coordinator, marketing manager), how to sequence role creation for maximum impact, organizational design at 10, 25, and 50 FTE levels
   - Budget thresholds for technology investment — when does a BMS pay for itself? When is CMMS justified? When does a dedicated POS system replace manual F&B tracking? Capital vs. operating expense trade-offs at mid-scale
   - Mid-size venue operational standards — IAVM benchmarks for mid-size venues, INTIX small/mid-size venue programming, how industry certifications (CVE, CFE) apply at mid-scale
   - Insurance and risk management at mid-scale — how insurance costs scale with capacity, when a venue needs a dedicated risk manager vs. outsourced risk consulting, coverage adequacy benchmarks
   - Mid-size venue technology stack — what a mid-size arena or performing arts centre actually uses vs. what enterprise venues use, integration challenges with fewer IT staff

5. MULTI-VENUE CAMPUS OPERATIONS: How are multiple venues managed on a single campus or in a compact district?
   - Centralized vs. decentralized management — single director for 5+ campus venues vs. venue-specific managers reporting to different departments, shared services models (maintenance, custodial, security, IT), coordination mechanisms
   - Unified scheduling systems — campus-wide event scheduling across academic spaces, athletic venues, performing arts facilities, and outdoor event spaces, conflict resolution, calendar integration
   - Shared services economics — cost allocation models for shared maintenance crews, shared equipment pools, shared security teams, centralized purchasing, when sharing saves money and when it creates bottlenecks
   - Convention district multi-venue coordination — downtown convention districts (Nashville, San Diego, Austin) managing multiple publicly-owned venues, coordinated event bidding, shared infrastructure

6. COMMUNITY PERFORMING ARTS CENTRES: What operational models govern community-scale performing arts venues?
   - Municipal governance structures — city-owned performing arts centres, management agreements (direct municipal operation vs. nonprofit management agreement vs. management company), board composition, public accountability requirements
   - Mixed programming models — presented (venue-produced) vs. rental (third-party) programming, revenue models for each, how the balance affects financial sustainability, typical presented/rental ratios by venue size
   - Contributed revenue models — individual donations, corporate sponsorship at community scale, foundation grants, government arts funding, annual fund campaigns, gala/benefit event revenue, how contributed revenue typically represents 30-50% of community PAC budgets
   - Subscriber relationship management — subscription series design, retention strategies, dynamic pricing for subscriptions, subscriber demographics and aging concerns, conversion from single-ticket to subscriber
   - Volunteer usher and docent programs — recruitment, training, scheduling, recognition, liability considerations, how volunteer programs reduce operating costs, program management overhead

7. SCALE-DEPENDENT REGULATORY DIFFERENCES: How do regulatory requirements change based on venue capacity?
   - NFPA occupancy thresholds — when do NFPA 101 assembly occupancy provisions change (300, 1000 occupant thresholds), what additional requirements trigger at each level, sprinkler requirements by occupancy size, emergency communication system requirements by capacity
   - ADA compliance scaling — when are specific ADA provisions triggered (accessible seating ratios, assistive listening device requirements, accessible route requirements), how compliance costs scale with venue size
   - Safety staffing ratios — published guidelines for crowd management staff-to-attendee ratios by event type and venue capacity, when a venue needs a dedicated safety officer vs. shared responsibility
   - Fire marshal inspection frequency — how inspection schedules vary by occupancy classification and capacity, annual vs. event-specific inspections, variation by jurisdiction
   - Alcohol service scaling — when do different liquor licensing tiers apply, server-to-patron ratio requirements by jurisdiction, when TIPS/Smart Serve certification is legally required vs. best practice
